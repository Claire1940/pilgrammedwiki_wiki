Fishing in the open-world RPG environment of Pilgrammed has evolved from a simple side-activity into a legitimate progression path for dedicated players. Whether you are looking to complete a "fishing only" challenge or simply want to unlock some of the most unique weapons in the game, understanding the mechanics of **pilgrammed fishing** is essential. In 2026, the meta has shifted to favor players who diversify their resource gathering, and the rewards offered by the sea are often more lucrative than standard mob grinding.

To excel at **pilgrammed fishing**, you must move beyond the basics of standing at a dock. You will need to navigate dangerous swamps, brave the frozen north, and complete a series of randomized quests for the legendary NPC Jared. This guide covers everything from your first basic rod to the high-tier Rod of Kings, ensuring you know exactly where to cast your line for the rarest catches and gear.

## Getting Started: Your First Cast

Before you can begin your journey into **pilgrammed fishing**, you need to gather a few essential resources to purchase your first rod. Unlike combat-centric builds that focus on swords or spells, a fisherman’s early game is all about exploration and gathering.

To unlock the basic fishing rod from Jared, you will need:
1.  **200 Gold:** Easily acquired by farming chests in the King's Cabin or completing the Blacksmith’s initial quest.
2.  **Red and Blue Berries:** These spawn in bushes throughout the Forest area. Alternatively, you can buy them from the Red Apple NPC on the mountain, though it is significantly more expensive.
3.  **Iron Ore:** You will need a bronze pickaxe to mine iron in the Desert region.

Once you have these items, head to the **Elite Knights Barracks** located past the mountain in the Forest. Jared sits at the dock, ready to sell you your starting equipment.

| Item | Cost/Requirement | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Basic Fishing Rod** | 200 Gold + Berries + Iron | Your entry-level tool for all water bodies. |
| **Bait** | Varies (Gold) | Essential for attracting fish; buy in bulk from Jared. |
| **Lantern** | Hardy Hardware Quest | Provides visibility for night fishing and dark zones. |
| **Angler Hat** | Sea Bass, Salmon, Tin | Increases fishing efficiency and completes the aesthetic. |

> **💡 Tip:** Chest farming by rejoining servers is a fast way to accumulate the gold needed for large quantities of bait early on.

## Advanced Rod Progression and Crafting

As you progress, the basic rod will no longer suffice for the high-level zones. To catch exotic species like Salt Eels or the elusive Angler Fish, you must upgrade your gear. The jump from the basic rod to the Advanced Rod requires specific materials that can only be found by traveling across the map.

### The Advanced Rod
To craft the Advanced Rod, you must venture to Bear Island and the Northern Sea. This rod significantly increases your reel speed and allows you to pull up heavier treasures.

*   **Salt Eels:** Found specifically at Bear Island.
*   **Jellyfish:** Located in the Northern Sea (prepare for a wait, as these are rarer).
*   **Squid:** Common in Big Bay.
*   **12 Zinc:** Mined from higher-level ore veins.

### The Rod of Kings
The pinnacle of **pilgrammed fishing** is the Rod of Kings. This is not a crafted item in the traditional sense; it is a reward for dedication. You must complete **20 fishing quests** for Jared to unlock this powerhouse. The Rod of Kings has the highest luck stat in the game, making it much easier to pull up rare weapons and chests rather than common junk.

## Global Fishing Locations & Loot Table

Different regions in Pilgrammed host unique species. Knowing where to go for specific quest items will save you hours of aimless casting. Be wary, however, as many of these locations are infested with hostile mobs that will attack you while you fish.

| Region | Primary Fish | Rare Drops / Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Big Bay** | Sea Bass, Salmon, Squid | Great for early gold and quest completion. |
| **Bear Island** | Salt Eels | Essential for the Advanced Rod upgrade. |
| **Northern Sea** | Jellyfish, Frozen Fish | Watch out for cold debuffs in this region. |
| **Deadly Swamp** | Mudfish | Beware of frogs; they deal high contact damage. |
| **Cobalt Caverns** | Angler Fish | Requires a lantern; high chance for rare ores. |
| **Arctic Tundra** | Arctic Tuna | Late-game quest fish with high sell value. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Pulling up a chest in Big Bay or the Swamp can occasionally spawn enemies. Always fish from a raised rock or a boat to avoid being ambushed.

## Fishing Quests and Unique Rewards

Jared’s quests are randomized, meaning he might ask for 10 Salmon one minute and 5 Mudfish the next. It is highly recommended to keep a stockpile of every fish you catch in your inventory. Completing these quests provides more than just gold; it unlocks exclusive gear that cannot be obtained through standard combat.

### Weapon Rewards
Every five quests, you are typically presented with a choice of rewards. Some of the most notable weapons obtained through **pilgrammed fishing** include:

*   **The Cutlass:** A reliable melee weapon for mid-game players.
*   **The Musket:** A powerful ranged option that allows fishermen to defend themselves from a distance.
*   **The Swordfish:** A high-speed piercing weapon that excels in rapid-fire combat.
*   **Swordfish Trident:** One of the rarest pulls, offering incredible reach and damage scaling.

### Naval Upgrades
A true fisherman needs a vessel. Once you complete your first few quests, you can purchase a basic boat. In 2026, the ship progression is a significant gold sink but offers great utility for reaching offshore islands.

1.  **Basic Boat:** Cheap, slow, but gets the job done.
2.  **Sailboat (5,000 Gold):** Significantly faster and more durable.
3.  **Luxury Ship (50,000 Gold):** The ultimate status symbol with massive health and speed.

## Enhancing Your Fisherman Build

To maximize your efficiency, you should look for gems and accessories that complement the fishing lifestyle. While fishing itself doesn't require combat stats, the enemies you encounter while traveling do.

The **Lightning Gem** is a popular choice for fishermen. When applied to weapons like the Cutlass or Trident, it allows you to apply shock damage. Pair this with the **Spark Scale** accessory to increase your lightning damage output, making quick work of the annoying frogs in the Deadly Swamp or the urchins in the Cobalt Caverns.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find Jared to start fishing?**

A: Jared is located at the docks of the Elite Knights Barracks. From the starting forest, head past the mountain on the top right to find the barracks area.

**Q: How do I get the Rod of Kings in Pilgrammed?**

A: You must complete 20 randomized fishing quests for Jared. Once you hit this milestone, he will offer you the Rod of Kings as a reward, which is currently the best rod for **pilgrammed fishing** in 2026.

**Q: Can you actually beat the game using only items from fishing?**

A: It is possible but extremely difficult. You can obtain weapons like the Musket and Swordfish Trident through fishing, but you will still need to upgrade them and potentially use armor from other quests to survive late-game bosses like Oldfar.

**Q: What is the best way to get bait?**

A: The most efficient way is to farm gold via the King's Cabin chests or the Blacksmith's quest and then buy bait in bulk from Jared. Some fish can also be traded back for bait supplies.

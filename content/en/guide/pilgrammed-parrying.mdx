## The Fundamentals of Parrying Mechanics

At its core, parrying is performed by pressing the **F** key. While many combat games encourage players to tap the block button at the exact moment of impact, this game functions slightly differently. Veteran players suggest that holding the key is often more reliable than a quick tap, especially when dealing with server latency or multi-hit attacks.

| Method | Execution | Best Used For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Reaction Parrying** | Pressing F when you see a visual swing animation. | Slow enemies, Ogres, and heavy melee units. |
| **Prediction Parrying** | Pressing F slightly before an action occurs. | Ranged projectiles, fast thieves, and high-ping environments. |
| **Hold-Block** | Maintaining the F key through a sequence. | Multi-hit enemies like Slimes or rapid-fire projectiles. |

> **Warning:** Some enemies possess "Hyper Armor," meaning they will not be stunned even if you successfully parry their attack. Always be ready to dodge-roll immediately after a parry if the enemy continues their combo.

## Reaction vs. Prediction Parrying

Understanding when to react and when to predict is the hallmark of an elite player. Reaction parrying is the most straightforward method. When an enemy like an Ogre winds up for a heavy swing, you wait for the downward motion and hold F. This is generally effective for most melee-centric mobs found in the early to mid-game.

However, as you move into the Desert and Savannah regions, prediction becomes vital. Projectiles are notoriously difficult to parry based on sight alone because the travel time is often faster than the average human reaction speed combined with server ping. To parry a projectile, you should aim to press the parry button right as the enemy fires, rather than waiting for the bullet or arrow to reach your character's hitbox.

## Essential Items to Buff Your Parry Window

If you find the default timing too tight, several items can significantly increase your success rate. These items are essential for players who struggle with high ping (80ms to 120ms+) or those tackling endgame bosses.

### The Hoplite Brace
The Hoplite Brace is arguably the most balanced defensive accessory in the game. It provides a noticeable boost to your defensive capabilities without the severe drawbacks of other items. You can purchase this from the Blacksmith NPC located in the Savannah.

**Crafting/Purchase Requirements:**
*   **800 Gold**
*   **8 Brass** (Crafted using 1 Copper and 1 Zinc)
*   **3 Emeralds** (Mined from ore veins in the Desert)

### The Delicate Egg
For those who want the largest possible window, the Delicate Egg is the go-to choice. It doubles your parry window, making it almost impossible to miss a timed block. However, it comes with a massive "glass cannon" drawback: you take 25% more damage from all sources.

| Item | Benefit | Drawback |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Spears (Weapon Class)** | +50% Parry Window | Limited to specific weapon movesets. |
| **Delicate Egg** | **+100% Parry Window** | +25% Damage Taken. |
| **Hoplite Brace** | Increased block stability | Requires significant crafting materials. |
| **Foe Ring** | Improved parry rewards | Dropped by Green Men (Rare). |

> **Tip:** The Delicate Egg is sold by the same NPC that offers the Challenger Emblem in the Desert. To find him, head into the Desert and keep to the left side of the map.

## Advanced Strategies for Difficult Enemies

Not all enemies in Pilgrammed are created equal. Some require specific parry rhythms that can be counter-intuitive.

### Handling Slimes
Slimes are often cited as one of the most frustrating enemies due to their "multi-jump" attack. If you parry the first hit but let go of the F key too early, the second or third jump will stun-lock you. When engaging slimes, it is often better to parry once and then immediately back away rather than trying to parry the entire sequence.

### Dealing with Thieves and Ogres
Thieves are fast but lack Hyper Armor. This means a successful parry will almost always interrupt their flow, allowing you to counter-attack safely. Ogres, on the other hand, will swing through your parry. You must successfully time the block to negate the damage, but do not expect the Ogre to stop moving. You should treat **pilgrammed parrying** against Ogres as a damage mitigation tool rather than a way to create an opening.

## Improving Your Setup for Better Timing

Your hardware and game settings can have a significant impact on your ability to perform a **pilgrammed parry**. Since the game relies on server-side registration for blocks, even a small amount of lag can result in a "failed" parry where the animation plays but you still take damage.

*   **Graphics Settings**: Lower your graphics to ensure a stable 60 FPS. Frame drops during an enemy's wind-up animation are the leading cause of missed parries.
*   **Ping Monitoring**: If your ping is consistently above 150ms, you must rely almost entirely on prediction parrying. You will need to press F significantly earlier than players with 20ms ping.
*   **Audio Cues**: Many enemies have a distinct "shing" or grunt sound right before they strike. Learning these audio cues is often more effective than watching the visual animations.

## FAQ

**Q: Why does my parry fail even when I press F on time?**

A: This is usually due to one of two things: server latency (ping) or the enemy having a multi-hit attack. If your ping is high, you must parry earlier. If the enemy hits multiple times, you must hold the F key rather than tapping it.

**Q: Is the Delicate Egg worth the extra damage taken?**

A: For beginners learning **pilgrammed parrying** timings, the Delicate Egg is an excellent training tool. However, for endgame bosses that can one-shot you, the 25% damage penalty is often too risky. Most pro players switch to the Hoplite Brace once they have the timing down.

**Q: Do all weapons have the same parry window?**

A: No. Spears generally have a 50% larger parry window compared to daggers or heavy hammers. If you are struggling with defensive timing, switching to a spear-based build can make the game significantly more forgiving.

**Q: Where can I find the Green Men to farm the Foe Ring?**

A: Green Men are found in the Desert biome. They are aggressive mobs that spawn in groups. While the Foe Ring is a decent drop, it is not considered "meta" compared to the Hoplite Brace in the current 2026 build of the game.

In the vast and often treacherous world of Pilgrammed, mobility is just as important as your raw damage output. If you want to traverse the landscape with ease and conquer the game's most difficult platforming challenges, obtaining the **pilgrammed scurry boots** is a top priority for any serious adventurer. These boots, which grant the coveted Jump Master movement upgrade, provide a significant boost to your verticality and overall speed. By mastering the **pilgrammed scurry boots**, you can access hidden areas, escape dangerous mobs like Tendrilings, and dodge boss attacks that would otherwise be fatal.

The journey to unlock these boots takes you through the heights of Cloud City and requires a combination of rare materials and precise movement. In this guide, we will break down the exact steps needed to find Adagio, collect the necessary items, and secure your movement upgrades for the 2026 meta.

## Obtaining the Pilgrammed Scurry Boots

The **pilgrammed scurry boots** aren't just a simple drop from a boss; they are a specialized upgrade that requires a bit of legwork. To begin your quest, you must make your way to **Cloud City**. This zone is known for its verticality and reliance on slingshots for navigation. Once you arrive, you need to locate an NPC named **Adagio** (sometimes referred to as Adat or Adagio by the community).

Adagio is typically found near the central slingshot hub in Cloud City. Before you speak to him, you should ensure you have the prerequisite materials in your inventory. Adagio won't just hand over the Jump Master tech for free; he expects a trade of rare resources that prove your worth as an explorer.

### Required Materials for Scurry Boots

| Material | Quantity | Location/Source |
|---|---|---|
| **World Rubber** | 20 | Common drop/find in various world zones |
| **Cloud Boots** | 1 | Standard movement item found in Cloud City |
| **Great Red Bird Feather** | 1 | Plucked from the Great Red Bird on the High Island |

> **💡 Tip:** Most players will already have the World Rubber and Cloud Boots by the time they reach Cloud City, but the Great Red Bird Feather is a unique quest item that requires a specific trip.

## How to Get the Great Red Bird Feather

The most challenging part of unlocking the **pilgrammed scurry boots** is acquiring the Great Red Bird Feather. This item is located on a remote, high-altitude island far above the standard paths of Cloud City. To reach it, you will need specific gear to assist with your ascent.

### Recommended Loadout for the Ascent
To make the climb easier and safer, consider equipping the following items:
*   **Noxious Motor:** Essential for propelling yourself upward during the flight segments.
*   **Glider:** Necessary to interact with the white orbs that boost your height.
*   **Sport Shawl:** Highly recommended to reduce the annoyance of aggressive mobs like Tendrilings.
*   **Sandy Boots:** Useful for general movement speed before you hit the vertical sections.

### Step-by-Step Feather Guide

1.  **Locate the High Island:** From the main Cloud City area, look for a distant island populated by Tendrilings and white floating orbs.
2.  **Use the White Orbs:** These orbs act as jump pads. However, they only work if you have your **Glider** held out. Fly into them to be propelled higher into the sky.
3.  **Navigate with the Noxious Motor:** Between orbs, use your Noxious Motor to maintain height and reach the next platform.
4.  **Avoid the Tendrilings:** These enemies are incredibly annoying and can knock you off your path. If you have a Sport Shawl, use it to bypass them.
5.  **Reach the Summit:** At the very top of the island chain, you will find a large, stationary Red Bird.
6.  **Pluck the Feather:** Approach the bird and interact with it to "Pluck" the Great Red Bird Feather. You do not need to engage it in combat.

## Movement Upgrades: The "Prejunks" Trio

The **pilgrammed scurry boots** (Jump Master) are part of a trio of movement upgrades introduced in the Prejunks update. To have the best possible mobility in 2026, you should aim to collect all three. Each one provides a different "Master" skill that can be checked in your character's tech menu.

### Movement Tech Comparison

| Skill Name | Master Title | Source NPC | Location |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Scurry Boots** | **Jump Master** | Adagio | Cloud City |
| **Climbing Claws**| **Climb Master**| Duke of Rocks | Mountain Cabin |
| **Dash Upgrade** | **Dash Master** | Garth | Elite Brax |

### Climbing Claws (Climb Master)
To get the Climbing Claws, head to the Mountain region. Near the cabin where the Ice King resides, look for the **Duke of Rocks**. Talking to him will grant you the Climbing Claws movement upgrade. This allows you to perform up to three consecutive climbs or wall jumps, which synergizes perfectly with the extra height provided by the scurry boots.

### Dash Upgrade (Dash Master)
The Dash Master upgrade is obtained from **Garth** at the Elite Brax location. While the requirements can vary based on your progression, simply speaking to Garth after achieving certain milestones usually unlocks the ability. This dash provides essential horizontal movement and I-frames for dodging.

> **Warning:** Some of these NPCs are located near world bosses like the Ice King. Be prepared for a fight or be ready to run if you are under-leveled!

## Advanced Movement Tips with Scurry Boots

Once you have successfully equipped the **pilgrammed scurry boots**, your gameplay style will likely shift. The Jump Master status changes how you approach both exploration and combat. 

1.  **Combining Techs:** Use your Dash Master to gain horizontal momentum, then trigger your Jump Master (Scurry Boots) for a massive leap. This "super jump" can clear large gaps in Cloud City that previously required slingshots.
2.  **Combat Evasion:** In boss fights, the extra jump height allows you to stay above ground-based AOE attacks longer. This is particularly useful against bosses in the Mountain and Desert regions.
3.  **The "Cheesy" Bow Build:** As seen in many high-level runs, using a Heavy Rusty Bow along with the mobility of the **pilgrammed scurry boots** allows you to kite enemies indefinitely. By jumping and dashing away while firing, you can take down high-health targets without taking a single hit.

## FAQ

**Q: Where exactly is Adagio located in Cloud City?**

A: Adagio is located near the main slingshot platforms. If you are having trouble finding him, look for the NPC standing near the edge of the clouds overlooking the lower islands.

**Q: Do I need to kill the Great Red Bird to get the feather for the pilgrammed scurry boots?**

A: No, you do not need to kill the bird. You simply need to reach its platform on the high island and interact with it to "pluck" the feather. Avoiding combat is often safer as the bird can be quite powerful.

**Q: Can I use the Scurry Boots and Climbing Claws at the same time?**

A: Yes! These are considered movement upgrades or "techs." Once unlocked, they function as permanent improvements to your movement suite, allowing you to jump higher (Jump Master) and climb walls more effectively (Climb Master) simultaneously.

**Q: What is the best way to farm the 20 World Rubber needed for the pilgrammed scurry boots?**

A: World Rubber is found in various crates and as drops from mechanical enemies throughout the world. The easiest way to farm it is to do a sweep of the industrial or junk-themed areas of the map where scrap materials are common.

## Getting Started: The First Steps

When you first spawn into the world, you are equipped with an Old Sword and an Old Pickaxe. Your immediate priority is to gather resources. Around the starting area, you will notice small rock formations containing ores. Use your pickaxe to mine these and head to the blacksmith.

To progress beyond the basic starter gear, you need to craft Bronze. This is done by trading in Copper and Tin at the blacksmith. Bronze weapons are significantly more effective than your starting equipment and are necessary for taking on the first major challenges.

### Essential Early Game Materials

| Material | Components Required | Primary Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bronze** | 3 Copper + 3 Tin | Early-game swords and tools |
| **Iron** | Mined in Desert | Mid-tier weapons and armor |
| **De-metal** | Mined in Volcano | Fire-infused weaponry |
| **Mithril** | Mined in Mountains | Frost-infused weaponry |

> **💡 Tip:** Always use your Encyclopedia. When you kill a new enemy, it records their health, damage, drop chances, and locations. This is the most reliable way to track down specific items for your build.

## Combat Mechanics: Parrying and Rolling

Combat in this game is heavily inspired by soulslike mechanics. You cannot simply "spam" your attacks and expect to win. You must learn the rhythm of your enemies.

1.  **Parrying:** Most melee attacks can be parried. Timing a parry perfectly negates damage and often leaves the enemy vulnerable to a counter-attack.
2.  **Rolling:** Some attacks, particularly from bosses or large enemies like Ogres, are unparriable. You must use your roll to gain i-frames (invincibility frames) and dodge out of the way.
3.  **Sneak Attacks:** If you approach an enemy from behind before they notice you, your first hit will be a "Sneak Attack," dealing massive bonus damage.

## The First Boss: The Thief King

Following the initial questline from the NPC Nebraska will lead you to investigate the thieves. This culminates in a battle with the Thief King. He typically swings his weapon twice, but can occasionally chain a third hit. 

**Thief King Attack Patterns:**
*   **Minion Summon:** He blows a trumpet to call smaller thieves. Focus them down quickly so they don't overwhelm you.
*   **Jump Attack:** When he leaps into the air, prepare to roll. This move deals high area damage upon landing.
*   **Charge:** He will run toward you at high speed. This can be parried if timed correctly, providing a great window for extra damage.

### Notable Thief King Drops

| Item | Type | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **View Zella** | Accessory | A trumpet that summons a thief to fight for you |
| **Thief Pendant** | Accessory | Chance to spawn a thief minion when you are hit |
| **Thief Cloak** | Armor | Grants a specialized dash ability |

## Character Progression and Stats

Every time you level up, you gain Skill Points. These points are spent in the Skill Tree to define your "class" or playstyle. In 2026, the meta for beginners focuses on survivability.

*   **Strength:** Increases raw damage for melee weapons.
*   **Dexterity:** Increases your swing speed, allowing for faster combos.
*   **Defense:** Reduces the amount of damage you take from all sources.
*   **Intellect:** Increases your Mana pool for magic-based builds.
*   **Agility:** Increases movement speed and roll distance.

### Recommended Early Skills

1.  **Rally:** This is highly recommended for players who are good at parrying. Every successful parry restores 5% of your maximum HP.
2.  **Tank:** If you find yourself struggling with dodge timings, the Tank skill provides a flat increase to your total Health Points, allowing for more mistakes.

> **⚠️ Warning:** When you die, you lose 50% of the gold currently in your inventory. Always visit a Banker in major villages (like the Desert or Volcano villages) to deposit your gold safely.

## Advanced Exploration: The Desert and Beyond

Once the Thief King is defeated, your journey takes you to the Desert. This area is a significant step up in difficulty. Avoid the Cacti enemies early on, as they deal disproportionate damage to low-level players.

### Reaching the Volcano and Tundra
After completing the questline in the Desert Tower, you should head toward the Volcano. Here, you can obtain **De-metal**, which is used to craft weapons that set enemies on fire. Fire is particularly effective against enemies in the Tundra (Mountain) region.

Conversely, the Mountain region offers **Mithril**, used for Frost-based weapons. Frostburn slows enemies and is highly effective against the fire-based creatures of the Volcano. This elemental rock-paper-scissors mechanic is a core part of this **pilgrammed guide** strategy.

## Weapon Archetypes and Recommendations

Choosing a weapon depends on your preferred range and speed. The game categorizes weapons into Melee, Ranged, and Magic.

### Top Early-Game Melee Options
| Weapon | Location | Special Property |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Crude Club** | Forest (Ogre drop) | Deals Blunt damage; great against armored bosses |
| **Cutlass** | Beach | Immediate upgrade over basic Bronze |
| **De-metal Sword** | Volcano | Inflicts Fire status on every hit |
| **Mithril Sword** | Mountain | Inflicts Frostburn status on every hit |

### Ranged and Magic Options
For those who prefer distance, the **Triplex Bow** from the Desert or the **Flintlock** from the Pirate Captain at the beach are excellent choices. Magic users should seek out the **Zap Antenna** in the "Sus Lands" for its ability to stun enemies with shock damage, or the **Icicle Rod** for consistent frost application.

## Reforging and Gems

To truly optimize your build, you must utilize the Reforging system and Gems. Reforging allows you to add prefixes to your gear that grant stat bonuses.

*   **Weapon Reforges:** Aim for **Legendary**, which provides a 10% boost to both damage and swing speed.
*   **Armor Reforges:** Aim for **Wonderful**, which provides a balanced +2 bonus to all major stats.

Gems are rare drops from "Enhanced Enemies" (enemies with glowing colors and higher HP). You can socket these gems into your equipment at the Observatory to add elemental damage or utility effects like lifesteal.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I get the climbing gear?**

A: The climbing gear is sold by an NPC named Rag, who can randomly spawn in the Mountain area or certain villages. It requires Iron and Zinc to purchase and is essential for scaling steep cliffs.

**Q: How do I unlock the Vampire race?**

A: You must find the Vampire NPC in the Forest. Becoming a Vampire makes you take 25% less damage during the day and allows you to heal HP whenever you kill an enemy, though it may have other trade-offs depending on your current build.

**Q: What is the best way to farm gold in this pilgrammed guide?**

A: Fishing at Big Bay is one of the most consistent ways to earn gold early. By using red berries and blueberries as bait, you can catch fish or rare chests that contain up to 200 gold and occasional gems.

**Q: Can I reset my skill points?**

A: Yes, but it usually requires a specific item or a fee in gold at certain NPCs. It is better to plan your build early—decide if you want to be a heavy hitter (Strength/Defense) or a fast attacker (Dexterity).

## How to Unlock the Climbmaster Skill

The journey to becoming a **climbmaster pilgrammed** expert begins at the base of the Mountain. This skill is widely considered the most important early-game acquisition because it functions like a permanent set of Climbing Claws, freeing up your accessory slots for more combat-oriented gear.

To begin, you must locate the **Duke of Rocks**. He is an NPC found near the mountain entrance who tasks players with proving their worth through a series of combat and traversal challenges.

### The Duke of Rocks Questline

1.  **Locate the Duke:** Find him at the start of the mountain path.
2.  **Defeat Mountain Enemies:** You will need to clear out a specific number of local hostile mobs. These typically include rock-based entities and aggressive mountain fauna.
3.  **Summit the Mountain:** After the combat phase, you must reach the very peak of the mountain. This serves as a test of your natural platforming ability before the game grants you the enhanced skill.
4.  **Claim Your Reward:** Return to the Duke of Rocks to finalize the quest and receive the skill.

| Feature | Climbmaster Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Effect** | Allows **3 consecutive wall climbs** |
| **Reset Trigger** | Touching the ground once |
| **Requirement** | Duke of Rocks Mountain Quest |
| **Utility Rank** | S-Tier (Essential for Exploration) |

> **💡 Tip:** Once you have the skill, you no longer need to equip Climbing Claws. This allows you to use that accessory slot for items that boost your damage or defense.

## Mastering the Dash Technique

While the **climbmaster pilgrammed** skill handles verticality, the Dash technique is your bread and butter for horizontal movement and combat evasion. This skill is located at the **Elite Barracks**, a hub for high-level players and those looking to join the knightly ranks.

To obtain this, you must speak with **Gareth** (sometimes referred to as Garth). Unlike the Duke of Rocks, Gareth’s requirements are tied more closely to your overall progression and reputation within the game's factions.

### Requirements for the Dash Skill

While the exact triggers can vary based on your character's current state, most players find success by meeting the following criteria:
*   Achieving a high level within the **Elite Knight Guild**.
*   Defeating the **Skeleton Boss** to prove combat proficiency.
*   Having a "Full Account" status (progressed through the main story beats).

The Dash technique replaces your standard roll with an **Empowered Dash**. These are significantly quicker than the default roll and cover more distance, making them perfect for "speed-clearing" dungeons or escaping area-of-effect (AoE) attacks from bosses.

## The Advanced Double Jump: Redbird Quest

The final piece of the movement puzzle is the mid-air jump, often referred to as the Double Jump or the "Scurry" ability. This is the most complex skill to obtain and requires a variety of rare materials and a specific traversal sequence.

### Required Materials Checklist

Before heading out, ensure you have the following items in your inventory. Without these, the NPC will not grant you the skill even if you complete the physical challenge.

| Material | Quantity | Acquisition Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cloud Boots** | 1 | Purchased from the Shop Vendor |
| **Royal Rubber** | 20 | Dropped by Royal-tier enemies or found in chests |
| **Great Redbird Feather** | 1 | Stolen from the Sleeping Redbird on the far island |

### Reaching the Far Island

To get the Great Redbird Feather, you must travel to a remote island. There are two primary ways to reach this location:
1.  **Noxious Mortar:** Use the mortar's launch capability to propel yourself across the ocean.
2.  **Imperial Knight Teleporter:** Travel to the island where the Imperial Knights reside, glide to the smaller adjacent island, and use the hidden teleporter located behind the knight camp.

Once on the small island, you must use **White Orbs** to maintain flight. Equip your glider and pass through these orbs to refresh your flight duration. You will need to use your **climbmaster pilgrammed** wall-climb ability to scale the cliffs between orb segments.

> **⚠️ Warning:** During the flight sequence, "Tangerine" enemies will spawn and attempt to knock you out of the sky. You cannot parry while gliding, so you must maneuver manually to dodge their projectiles.

After reaching the summit, look for the sleeping Great Redbird. Interact with it to "steal" the feather, then return to the NPC with the slingshot to craft your new skill.

Possessing the **climbmaster pilgrammed** skill alongside the Double Jump and Dash creates a movement suite that makes you nearly untouchable. In the 2026 meta, players often combine these with specific weapons to stay airborne indefinitely.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the climbmaster pilgrammed skill stack with Climbing Claws?**

A: No, the skill essentially replaces the need for the accessory. While you can wear both, you will not receive additional climb charges beyond the three granted by the skill itself. It is better to use that accessory slot for something else.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get Royal Rubber for the Double Jump?**

A: Farming the Imperial Knights or checking high-tier chests in the Royal District is the most consistent method. Some players also trade for it in community hubs if they have excess gold.

**Q: Can I get the Dash technique if I am not in the Elite Knight Guild?**

A: While being in the guild makes it easier to access Gareth, you can typically unlock the skill by proving your strength through boss kills, specifically the Skeleton boss found in the lower catacombs.

**Q: Is the Great Redbird Feather a guaranteed drop?**

A: It is not a "drop" in the traditional sense. You must interact with the sleeping bird on the far island to steal it. As long as you reach the bird and have the quest active, you are guaranteed to get the feather.

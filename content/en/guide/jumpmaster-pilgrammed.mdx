## Understanding the Jumpmaster Skill

The Jumpmaster skill is often considered the pinnacle of movement progression in Pilgrammed. Unlike the basic dash or the wall-climbing technique provided by the Duke of Rocks, Jumpmaster focuses on aerial superiority. When equipped, players can perform a second jump while in mid-air. Additionally, it allows you to extend your dive distance by pressing the jump button again during a fall, effectively acting as a permanent version of the Scurry Boots buff without occupying an accessory slot.

| Feature | Description | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Double Jump** | Perform a second leap in mid-air. | Reach higher ledges and dodge ground-based AoE attacks. |
| **Dive Extension** | Press jump during a dive to gain horizontal momentum. | Cross large gaps between islands or escape combat quickly. |
| **Slot Efficiency** | Functions as a permanent skill rather than an item. | Frees up accessory slots for damage or defense-oriented gear. |

> **💡 Tip:** Combining Jumpmaster with the Climbing Claws technique allows for unparalleled vertical mobility, making it much easier to reach hidden chests and secret NPCs located on high mountain peaks.

## Prerequisites: Gathering the Materials

Before you can seek out the specific NPC to teach you the skill, you must gather a list of rare components. The **jumpmaster pilgrammed** quest is not a simple conversation; it is a crafting challenge that tests your ability to farm and navigate.

### 1. Cloud Boots
The first item you need is the Cloud Boots. These can be purchased directly from the standard Shop Vendor. While they provide a decent jump boost on their own, they serve as the "base" for the Jumpmaster upgrade. Ensure you have enough gold saved up before heading to the vendor.

### 2. Royal Rubber
You will need **20 Royal Rubber**. This material is typically dropped by rubber-themed enemies or found in specific high-tier zones. Farming these can take some time, so it is recommended to use equipment that increases drop rates if available in your current build.

### 3. The Great Redbird Feather
This is the most difficult component to acquire. It is a unique item found in a specific location that requires a "parkour" sequence involving gliders and environmental orbs.

| Material | Quantity | Primary Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cloud Boots** | 1 | Shop Vendor (Gold Purchase) |
| **Royal Rubber** | 20 | Royal/Rubber Enemy Drops |

## Step-by-Step: Obtaining the Great Redbird Feather

To secure the feather for your **jumpmaster pilgrammed** unlock, you must travel to a remote island. Follow these steps carefully to navigate the flight sequence.

### Reaching the Island
If you do not have the Noxious Mortar for easy travel, start at the island where the Imperial Knights are stationed. From there, glide to the smaller adjacent island. Look for a teleporter located on the lower levels of this small island. Stepping through this teleporter will take you to the base of the Great Redbird's mountain.

### The Aerial Challenge
Once you arrive, you will see glowing white orbs. These are essential for your ascent.
1. **Equip your Glider:** You cannot complete this without a glider equipped.
2. **Hit the Orbs:** Passing through a white orb refreshes your flight and gives you a vertical boost.
3. **Climbing Intermission:** At certain points, the orbs will lead to a sheer cliff face. Use your climbing techniques (like the ones learned from the Duke of Rocks) to scale the wall and reach the next set of orbs.
4. **Dodge the Tangerines:** As you get higher, Tangerine enemies will begin to spawn and attack you mid-air.
   
> **⚠️ Warning:** You cannot parry while gliding. You must manually move and dodge the Tangerine attacks. If you get hit, you may lose your momentum and fall, forcing you to restart the climb from the bottom.

### Stealing the Feather
Once you reach the very top of the flight path, you will find the Great Redbird sleeping. Do not engage it in combat. Instead, interact with the bird to "steal" a feather. Once the feather is in your inventory, you are ready to complete the skill training.

## Finalizing the Jumpmaster Skill

With the Cloud Boots, 20 Royal Rubber, and the Great Redbird Feather in your possession, you must find the specific NPC to finalize the process.

The NPC you are looking for is the **Slingshot Vendor**. He is the master of projectile and movement physics in Pilgrammed. Talk to him and select the option indicating you have the materials he requested. He will take the items and grant you the permanent Jumpmaster skill.

## Advanced Tips for Using Jumpmaster

Once you have unlocked the skill, you should practice the "Jump-Dive-Jump" combo. This sequence allows you to cover massive horizontal distances that were previously impossible. In the 2026 update, many hidden areas in the sky islands are specifically designed around the assumption that the player has **jumpmaster pilgrammed** active.

Another advanced tactic involves using the double jump to reset your falling velocity right before hitting the ground. This can prevent fall damage if timed correctly, though it requires precision. In boss fights like the Skeleton or the Imperial Knight commanders, the double jump allows you to stay above ground-based shockwaves, providing a much safer window for ranged attacks or healing.

For players who enjoy the "Pre-Junks" style of play, this skill is often the first major goal after hitting mid-game. The sheer utility of being able to double jump changes the way you approach every encounter in the game world.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use Jumpmaster and Dash at the same time?**

A: Yes! Skills in Pilgrammed are generally permanent upgrades that can be used in tandem. You can dash on the ground and then immediately transition into a double jump for maximum mobility.

**Q: What happens if I die while trying to get the Great Redbird Feather?**

A: If you die during the ascent, you will respawn at your last set spawn point. You will not lose the materials you have already gathered (like the Royal Rubber), but you will have to restart the climbing and gliding sequence from the start of the island.

**Q: Is the Jumpmaster skill better than the Scurry Boots?**

A: The **jumpmaster pilgrammed** skill provides the same movement benefits as the Scurry Boots but as a permanent skill. This means you can get those benefits while wearing other, more powerful boots for your build, making the skill objectively superior to relying on the item alone.

**Q: Do I need to be a certain level to talk to the Slingshot Vendor?**

A: While there isn't a strict level requirement, the areas you must visit to gather the 20 Royal Rubber and the Redbird Feather are dangerous. It is recommended to be at least mid-level with decent gear before attempting the quest.

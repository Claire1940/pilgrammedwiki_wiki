## Key Features in the Latest Pilgrammed Updates

The core focus of the recent **pilgrammed updates** has been the transition from item-based movement to skill-based movement upgrades. Previously, players had to occupy valuable accessory slots with items like Climbing Claws or Scurry Boots. Now, these abilities can be permanently unlocked as character skills, freeing up your build for more offensive or defensive utility.

| Upgrade Name | Type | Primary Benefit | Key NPC |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Climbing Claws** | Passive Skill | Allows vertical scaling of walls | Duke of Rocks |
| **Dash Master** | Active Skill | Triple dash capability | Garth |
| **Jump Master** | Passive Skill | Enhanced jump height and "Scurry" tech | Adagio |

These upgrades are not merely handed to you; they require specific materials, boss encounters, and precise navigation through some of the game's most dangerous zones.

## Unlocking the Climbing Claws: The Duke of Rocks

The first stop on your journey through the **pilgrammed updates** content is the Mountain biome. This area has always been a test of verticality, but the new Climbing Claws skill makes it much more manageable. To begin, you will need to locate a specific cabin tucked away in the mountain range.

### Locating the Duke
From the main Mountain entrance, head toward the right side of the cabin. You may encounter the Ice King in this vicinity. It is highly recommended to engage him, as his 2026 rework has updated his loot table to include essential Soul Fragments. Once past the Ice King's arena, continue upward until you find the NPC known as the Duke of Rocks.

### Skill Acquisition
The Duke of Rocks will grant you the Climbing Claws skill upon interaction. This skill functions identically to the old Climbing Claws item but exists as a permanent movement upgrade. 

> **Warning:** While the Climbing Claws allow for impressive verticality, they do not grant infinite stamina. Monitor your blue bar closely to avoid a lethal fall.

## Mastering the Dash: Garth and Elite Brax

The next phase of the **pilgrammed updates** progression involves the Dash Master skill. This is perhaps the most impactful upgrade for combat, as it allows for a triple-dash maneuver that can be used to dodge boss telegraphs or cross large gaps.

To find this upgrade, you must travel to the Elite Brax area and locate Garth. While the specific level requirements for Garth's dialogue can vary based on your current progression, most players find that having a basic mastery of the Mountain biome is sufficient.

| Dash Level | Charges | Recharge Rate | Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard** | 1 | Slow | Basic dodging |
| **Dash Master** | 3 | Fast | High-mobility combat & traversal |

Once you speak to Garth, the Dash Master skill will be added to your character sheet. You can verify this by checking your "Movements" tab in the menu, where it should be listed alongside your other Master-tier skills.

## Cloud City and the Jump Master Upgrade

The final and most difficult movement upgrade introduced in the latest **pilgrammed updates** is the Jump Master skill, often referred to by the community as the "Scurry Tech." This requires a trip to Cloud City and a dangerous hunt for rare materials.

### The Shopping List for Adagio
Locate Adagio (or Adagio) near the slingshot platforms in Cloud City. He acts as the vendor for the Jump Master upgrade, but he requires a steep price for his knowledge:

*   **20 World Rubber:** Generally found through exploration and standard drops in the mid-game.
*   **Cloud Boots:** A prerequisite item that many veteran players may already have in their inventory.
*   **Great Red Bird Feather:** The rarest component, obtained from a specific mini-boss.

### Hunting the Great Red Bird
To get the feather, you must navigate to a remote island floating far above the main Cloud City hub. This is where the **Noxious Motor** becomes mandatory. Use the Noxious Motor to propel yourself toward the white orbs floating in the sky.

> **💡 Tip:** To interact with the white orbs, you must have your glider equipped and active. The orbs act as refueling stations and propulsion boosts to keep you airborne.

Avoid the Tendrilings during your flight; they are notoriously aggressive in the 2026 version of the game and can easily knock you out of the sky. Once you reach the highest island, you will find the Great Red Bird. You do not necessarily need to engage in a full boss fight; you can simply "pluck" the feather from the bird and retreat. However, be warned that the bird can one-shot lower-level players.

## Recommended Gear for 2026 Traversal

With the complexity of the new **pilgrammed updates**, your loadout is more important than ever. While movement skills are now passives, certain items can augment your ability to reach these NPCs safely.

1.  **Noxious Motor:** The gold standard for vertical mobility. It is almost impossible to reach the Great Red Bird without it.
2.  **Sport Shawl:** An excellent utility item that reduces the "annoyance factor" of smaller mobs like Tendrilings.
3.  **Sandy Boots:** Useful for maintaining speed on difficult terrain before you have fully unlocked the Jump Master skill.
4.  **Heavy Rusty Bow:** A "cheesy" but effective weapon choice for clearing out enemies from a distance while you focus on platforming.

| Item | Best Used For | Obtainability |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Noxious Motor** | Infinite-ish flight | Mid-game Crafting |
| **Sport Shawl** | Resistance to knockback | Shop Purchase |
| **Great Red Bird Feather** | Jump Master Crafting | Rare Drop/Interact |

The synergy between the **Heavy Rusty Bow** and the new movement skills allows players to kite enemies indefinitely. By using the triple dash from Dash Master to create distance and the Climbing Claws to reach high ground, you can safely pick off targets that would otherwise be overwhelming.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to keep the Climbing Claws item in my inventory after talking to the Duke of Rocks?**

A: No. Once you have talked to the Duke of Rocks in the latest **pilgrammed updates**, the ability becomes a permanent skill. You can safely sell or bank the physical Climbing Claws item to save inventory space.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get World Rubber for the Jump Master upgrade?**

A: World Rubber is most commonly found in the industrial and cloud-themed zones. Farming the smaller constructs in Cloud City is the most reliable method to reach the 20 rubber requirement quickly.

**Q: Can I get the Great Red Bird Feather without the Noxious Motor?**

A: While technically possible with extremely precise glider management and the use of every white orb, it is highly discouraged. The Noxious Motor provides the necessary height buffer to survive the Tendriling attacks and the bird's initial aggro.

**Q: Does the Ice King rework affect the movement upgrades?**

A: Not directly, but the Ice King is located right next to the path to the Duke of Rocks. Since he now drops updated Soul Fragments required for high-tier 2026 builds, it is worth defeating him every time you pass through the area to unlock your movement skills.

## The Pilgrammed Dashmaster: Mobility and Mechanics

The core of the **pilgrammed dashmaster** build is the "Dash Technique," a skill that significantly enhances your standard roll. While the base roll provides a modest burst of speed and limited invulnerability, the empowered dash is designed for high-velocity repositioning.

Unlike standard movement options, the dash technique provides what many players describe as "empowered dashes." These are essentially quicker, more responsive rolls that allow for rapid chaining. In the current 2026 meta, the speed increase is noticeable enough to bypass certain area-of-effect (AoE) attacks that would typically catch a player mid-roll.

| Feature | Standard Roll | Dashmaster Technique |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Speed** | Moderate | **High/Empowered** |
| **Recovery Time** | Standard | **Reduced** |
| **Distance** | Short | **Extended** |
| **Visual Cue** | Basic Animation | **Wind/Speed Trails** |

> **Warning:** While the dash is faster, it does not make you invincible for the entire duration. Timing is still critical when facing high-level enemies in the mountain regions.

## How to Unlock the Dash Technique

To start your journey toward becoming a **pilgrammed dashmaster**, you must locate the Elite Barracks. This area is home to the most skilled warriors in the realm and serves as the primary hub for advanced combat techniques.

### Locating Gareth (Garth)
Once you arrive at the Elite Barracks, you need to find an NPC named Gareth (sometimes referred to by the community as Garth). He is the gatekeeper of the dash skill. Unlike some early-game NPCs who give away skills for free, Gareth requires you to prove your worth.

While the exact requirements can vary based on your current standing with the factions, the general consensus for unlocking the skill involves a combination of the following:

1.  **Elite Knight Guild Progress:** You should ideally have a high level or positive reputation with the Elite Knights.
2.  **Boss Completion:** Many players report that the skill only becomes available after successfully defeating the Skeleton boss.
3.  **Account Age/Level:** If you are playing on a "full" or veteran account, the dialogue options may trigger more easily.

## Advanced Movement: Synergy with Other Skills

A true **pilgrammed dashmaster** doesn't rely on dashing alone. To maximize your efficiency, you must combine the dash technique with other movement skills introduced or refined in the Prejunks update.

### The Climbing Technique
Before you can master the horizontal plane, you should master the vertical. By visiting the Duke of Rocks in the mountain region, you can obtain a permanent climbing skill. This skill allows you to climb up walls three times consecutively after touching the ground. It functions similarly to climbing claws but remains a permanent part of your character's kit, freeing up an accessory slot.

### The Double Jump (Cloud Jump)
The final piece of the mobility puzzle is the double jump. This is a more complex acquisition that requires specific crafting materials:
*   **Cloud Boots:** Purchased from the shop vendor.
*   **Royal Rubber (x20):** Farmed from royal-tier enemies.
*   **Great Redbird Feather:** Obtained by sneaking up on the sleeping Redbird on the far islands.

## Mastering Dashmaster Combat Strategies

Utilizing the **pilgrammed dashmaster** technique in combat requires a shift in mindset. You are no longer just reacting to attacks; you are using your superior speed to dictate the pace of the fight.

### The "Dash-Dive" Combo
By combining the dash with the double jump (obtained via the Redbird quest), you can perform an extended dive. Pressing the jump key mid-air after a dash allows you to maintain forward momentum for a significantly longer distance. This is exceptionally useful for crossing gaps in the Imperial Knight territory or escaping a "jump" by a group of Tangerines.

### Dodging Tangerines and Imperial Knights
In the far islands, Tangerines can swarm players quickly. The dash technique allows you to exit their attack range before their hitboxes activate. 

> **💡 Tip:** When navigating the white orbs for the Redbird quest, use your dash to reach the next orb faster. This minimizes the time you spend vulnerable to Tangerine projectiles.

## Crafting and Material Farming

To reach the peak of the **pilgrammed dashmaster** build, you will need to farm specific materials for your supporting gear. The Cloud Boots are the foundation of your aerial mobility, but the materials required for the upgrade are scattered across dangerous zones.

| Material | Primary Source | Best Farming Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Royal Rubber** | Royal Slimes/Enemies | Farm near the **Imperial Barracks** |
| **Redbird Feather** | Sleeping Redbird | Use the **Glider + Orbs** sequence |
| **Cloud Boots** | Shop Vendor | Purchase with gold in the main hub |

If you find it difficult to reach the far islands for the Redbird feather, consider using the **Noxious Mortar** for a vertical boost or the teleporter located near the Imperial Knights. Once you reach the small island, use the white orbs to maintain flight with your glider. Remember to dodge Tangerine attacks, as getting hit will stun you and likely cause you to fall into the void.

## FAQ

**Q: Where exactly is Gareth located?**

A: Gareth is located within the Elite Barracks. You can find this area by following the main path toward the knight-occupied territories. He is usually standing near the training grounds.

**Q: Does the pilgrammed dashmaster technique cost gold?**

A: The skill itself is usually granted through a quest or by meeting specific reputation requirements with Gareth, rather than a direct gold purchase. However, the supporting items like Cloud Boots will require gold.

**Q: Can I use the dash technique while mid-air?**

A: The dash technique primarily enhances your ground-based rolls. However, if you have the double jump skill unlocked via the Redbird Feather quest, you can extend your aerial momentum by pressing jump after a ground dash.

**Q: What is the best way to farm Royal Rubber for movement upgrades?**

A: The most efficient way is to patrol the outskirts of the Imperial Knight territory. Look for high-tier rubber-based enemies that spawn in groups. Using a weapon with high AoE (Area of Effect) damage can speed up the process significantly.

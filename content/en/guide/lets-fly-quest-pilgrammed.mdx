## The "Let's Fly" Skill: Double Jump and Gliding

The "Let's Fly" skill is widely considered the best mobility upgrade in the game. Unlike basic equipment that might offer a small jump boost, this skill is a permanent addition to your character's repertoire. It grants a mid-air jump and the ability to extend your dive, effectively mimicking the buffs of the Scurry Boots without requiring the item slot.

To begin the journey toward unlocking this power, you must locate the NPC with the slingshot. However, simply talking to him isn't enough; you must present a specific set of rare materials to prove your worthiness.

### Required Materials for Let's Fly

Before you set out on the platforming challenge, you must ensure your inventory is stocked with the necessary components. These items are scattered across different zones and require a mix of currency and farming.

| Material | Quantity | Acquisition Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **Great Redbird Feather** | 1 | Obtained from the Sleeping Redbird on the High Island |

> **Warning:** Do not attempt the Great Redbird Feather sequence without a Glider. The white orbs require you to be in a gliding state to activate their propulsion effect.

## Step-by-Step: Obtaining the Great Redbird Feather

The most difficult part of the **lets fly quest pilgrammed** is obtaining the feather. This requires navigating a series of floating islands and surviving an onslaught of aggressive Tangerines.

### 1. Reaching the Imperial Knight Island
To start, head toward the island occupied by the Imperial Knights. This is your primary launching point. If you have the Noxious Mortar, you can use its recoil to launch yourself across large gaps. If not, you must take the teleporter located beneath the Imperial Knight outpost. Follow the path past the knights—be careful of their high damage—and drop down to find the teleporter that leads to the smaller, high-altitude islands.

### 2. The White Orb Sequence
Once you arrive at the small island, look for floating white orbs. These are the "boosters" for your glider.
*   **Activation:** Equip your glider and jump into the first orb. It will launch you upward and forward.
*   **The Chain:** You must move quickly from one orb to the next. The boost duration is short, and falling usually results in a reset or death.
*   **Climbing Claws Synergy:** If you have already unlocked the Wall Climb skill (from the Duke of Rocks), use it to scale the vertical walls between orb sections to maintain your height.

### 3. Evading the Tangerines
As you reach the final stretch of orbs, Tangerines will begin to spawn and "jump" you in mid-air. Since you cannot parry while gliding, you must manually steer your glider to dodge their projectiles and lunges. Getting hit will cause "hit stun," which often leads to a fatal fall.

### 4. Stealing the Feather
At the end of the orb path, you will find a massive, sleeping Redbird. Approach it quietly and interact with it to "steal" the Great Redbird Feather. Once the feather is in your possession, you can teleport back to safety or use your glider to return to the mainland.

## Additional Essential Skills in Pilgrammed

While the **lets fly quest pilgrammed** is the highlight of the Pre-Junks update, there are two other skills you should acquire to become a true master of movement.

### The Wall Climb (Duke of Rocks)
This skill allows you to climb up walls three times consecutively before needing to touch the ground. It functions like a permanent version of the Climbing Claws accessory.
*   **Location:** The Duke of Rocks (Mountain Region).
*   **Quest:** Complete the "Mountain Quest" by defeating a specific number of mountain-dwelling enemies and reaching the summit.
*   **Utility:** Excellent for bypassing difficult platforming sections and finding shortcuts in the overworld.

### The Dash Technique (Elite Barracks)
The Dash skill provides "Empowered Dashes," which are significantly faster and cover more distance than the standard roll.
*   **Location:** Elite Barracks.
*   **NPC:** Gareth (or Garth).
*   **Requirement:** While the exact unlock trigger is debated, it generally requires a high level within the Elite Knight Guild or having defeated the Skeleton boss.
*   **Utility:** Essential for combat. The empowered dash allows you to reposition instantly during boss fights, making it much easier to avoid AOE (Area of Effect) attacks.

## Advanced Movement Strategies for 2026

Once you have completed the **lets fly quest pilgrammed**, your movement options expand exponentially. Expert players use a "momentum-chaining" technique to travel across the map at high speeds.

1.  **Mortar Launching:** Use the Noxious Mortar pointed at the ground to blast yourself into the air.
2.  **Mid-Air Jump:** At the peak of your mortar jump, use the Let's Fly double jump to maintain momentum.
3.  **Glide and Dive:** Open your glider, then use the dive extension to swoop down toward your destination.
4.  **Dash Recovery:** Upon landing, use the Empowered Dash to cancel any landing lag and keep moving forward.

This sequence allows you to cross the entire map in a fraction of the time it would take on foot. Furthermore, these skills do not consume stamina in the same way as standard sprinting, allowing for better resource management during exploration.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I complete the lets fly quest pilgrammed without the Cloud Boots?**

A: No, the Cloud Boots are a mandatory requirement for the NPC to accept your materials. You must have them in your inventory (or equipped) when talking to the slingshot NPC to finish the quest.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get 20 Royal Rubber?**

A: The fastest way is to farm the Royal Guards or Imperial Knights found near the castle regions. They have a high drop rate for Royal Rubber. Alternatively, high-tier chests in the same area often contain 2-3 pieces of rubber each.

**Q: Is the double jump permanent?**

A: Yes, once you complete the **lets fly quest pilgrammed** and receive the skill from the NPC, it is permanently bound to your character. You do not need to keep the materials or the feather in your inventory afterward.

**Q: How do I dodge the Tangerines while gliding?**

A: The best strategy is to "wiggle" your glider. By rapidly turning left and right, you change your hitbox's trajectory, making it harder for the Tangerines to land a hit. If you see one lunging, use your dive extension (bound to the skill key) to quickly drop below their flight path.

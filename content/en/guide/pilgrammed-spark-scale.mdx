## How to Obtain the Spark Scale: Defeating the Big Fish

To get your hands on the **pilgrammed spark scale**, you must venture into the more aquatic regions of the map to face **The Big Fish**. This boss is notorious for being a "pain in the neck" due to the environmental hazards involved in the fight—specifically the risk of drowning while trying to land hits in open water.

### Locating the Big Fish
The Big Fish typically spawns in the **Sunken City** area. To reach this location effectively:
1. Travel to the coastal regions and deploy your boat.
2. Look for large whirlpools in the water. 
3. Entering a whirlpool will often teleport you to random locations within the Sunken City, which is the prime hunting ground for this aquatic beast.

### The "Top G" Boat Cheese Strategy
Fighting the Big Fish fairly is difficult because of the water mechanics. Many top-tier players in 2026 suggest "cheesing" the boss using the following method:
*   **Lure to Land:** Try to lead the Big Fish toward a small island or a piece of protruding land.
*   **Ranged Superiority:** Use magic weapons or ranged tools like the Mana Sword's projectiles while staying safely on your boat or on a ledge.
*   **Circling:** If you must fight in the water, keep your boat moving in a wide circle (the "Top G" maneuver) to avoid its lunge attacks while your ranged abilities chip away at its health.

## The Zenitsu Build: Maximum Lightning DPS

The most popular use for the **pilgrammed spark scale** is the "Zenitsu Build," named after the iconic lightning-fast character. This build focuses on high mobility, rapid-fire lightning strikes, and extreme "glass cannon" stats—meaning you deal massive damage but have very low health.

### Core Equipment for the Build
To recreate this build, you will need more than just the spark scale. You need a full kit that synergizes with speed and magic.

1.  **Mana Sword:** This is your primary weapon. It scales with Intellect and allows for fast, magic-infused strikes.
2.  **Shock Runner Boots:** Obtained from the Shock Runner boss in the Savannah. These allow you to dash around the battlefield like a bolt of lightning.
3.  **Cursed Null:** This item is high-risk, high-reward. It significantly lowers your health but grants a massive 25% boost to magic damage.
4.  **Diver Armor:** This armor provides a unique buff: if you spend 70 or more mana within 3 seconds, you receive a 50% mana cost reduction for the next 5 seconds.

### Obtaining the Mana Sword
The Mana Sword requires a multi-step crafting process. First, visit the **Friendly Witch** in the Swamp. You will need:
*   **600 Gold**
*   **1 Mithril Sword** (Crafted at the Mountain blacksmith using Mithril ore found near the Snail area).
*   **10 Arch Energy** (Obtained by defeating the Arch Mage in the Swamp).

> **Warning:** The Arch Mage is an incredibly difficult boss. You may need to defeat him multiple times to gather enough Arch Energy. Most players recommend a high-defense build just for this fight before switching back to the Zenitsu speed build.

## Recommended Skill Tree Allocation

To make the **pilgrammed spark scale** truly shine, your skill tree must support high mana regeneration and mobility. In 2026, the following distribution is considered optimal for lightning mages:

*   **Wizardry:** Essential for maximizing the magic damage output of the Mana Sword and spark scale.
*   **Mana Regen:** You will be burning through mana quickly with the Shock Runner boots; this skill is non-negotiable.
*   **Rally Ace:** Provides much-needed healing and sustain, which is vital since the Cursed Null leaves you with very low HP.
*   **Athlete:** Increases your base movement speed, allowing you to catch up to teleporting bosses like the Shock Runner.

## Farming the Shock Runner for Boots
While the **pilgrammed spark scale** provides the damage, the **Shock Runner Boots** provide the utility. To find the Shock Runner boss:
1. Head to the **Savannah** biome.
2. If the boss isn't spawned, purchase a **Copper Crystal** from the local Shop NPC to summon him.
3. Be prepared for a frantic fight. The Shock Runner teleports constantly and uses a triple-shot lightning attack.
4. **Pro Tip:** Use the "Rally Ace" skill here to parry his red-dot projectiles for health regeneration.

## Advanced Combat Tactics
When using a build centered around the **pilgrammed spark scale**, your combat flow should look like this:
1.  **Engage:** Use the Shock Runner dash to close the distance instantly.
2.  **Burst:** Spam your Mana Sword abilities to trigger the Diver Armor's mana reduction buff.
3.  **Reposition:** Once your mana is low or the boss prepares a big attack, use the speed from your boots to retreat and regenerate.
4.  **Parry:** Because your health is likely low due to the Cursed Null, mastering the parry timing is essential for survival.

## FAQ

**Q: Where exactly does the Big Fish spawn for the pilgrammed spark scale?**

A: The Big Fish spawns in the Sunken City. You can find this area by taking a boat into the ocean and looking for large whirlpools that teleport you into the city ruins.

**Q: Can I use the spark scale with a physical strength build?**

A: While you *can* equip it, it is not recommended. The spark scale specifically boosts lightning and magic-based damage. If you are running a heavy strength build, you are better off using accessories like the Warrior's Emblem or physical damage charms.

**Q: Is the Cursed Null necessary for the lightning build?**

A: It is not strictly necessary, but it is the best way to maximize damage. If you find yourself dying too often, you can swap the Cursed Null for "Unharmed Energy" to gain more survivability at the cost of some DPS.

**Q: How do I get more Arch Energy for the Mana Sword?**

A: Arch Energy is a guaranteed drop from the Arch Mage boss located in the Swamp. You will typically need to kill him twice to get the 10 Arch Energy required for the Mana Sword recipe.

## How to Obtain the Ammo Box in Pilgrammed

The journey to becoming a master gunslinger begins with gathering the right resources and defeating specific early-game hurdles. The Ammo Box is not a simple drop from a common mob; it requires a bit of progression and a healthy amount of Gold.

To unlock the ability to purchase the Ammo Box, players generally need to progress past the Thief King. This boss acts as a gatekeeper for many of the more advanced "civilized" items in the game. Once you have proven your worth, you can find specialized vendors who cater to the ranged playstyle.

| Requirement | Details |

## Essential Gun Progression Path

Using an **ammo box pilgrammed** build requires more than just the box itself; you need a reliable firearm. The progression of guns in Pilgrammed follows a specific curve of difficulty and resource gathering.

1.  **The Blunderbuss:** This is often the first "real" gun players craft. It requires Iron, Brass, and Sulfur. Sulfur is found in the Volcano region, which can be dangerous for low-level players.
2.  **The Uzi:** A fast-firing alternative that excels at close-to-medium range. It allows for a "kick" mechanic which can be useful for creating space between you and aggressive mobs.
3.  **The Revolver:** Often considered a direct upgrade in terms of handling. It features a fast fire rate and high accuracy, making it a favorite for those who have mastered parrying while shooting.
4.  **Hand Cannon / Pop-up Pellets:** These heavier options deal massive burst damage but often require Scrap Metal and more advanced materials to maintain.

## Optimizing the Gunslinger Build

To make the most of your **ammo box pilgrammed** setup, you must equip gear that synergizes with ranged damage and critical hit chance. A "glass cannon" approach is common, but 2026 balance changes have made some defensive utility necessary.

### Recommended Armor and Accessories

| Item | Slot | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Gunslinger Uniform** | Armor | Increases ranged damage and reload speed |
| **Cowboy Hat** | Head | Boosts accuracy and provides a slight crit buff |
| **Acute Lens** | Accessory | Significantly increases critical hit chance |
| **Outlaw Glasses** | Accessory | Improves vision and adds flat ranged damage |
| **Ranger Emblem** | Accessory | Provides a percentage boost to all ranged attacks |

The Outlaw Glasses are particularly sought after, dropped by specific NPCs in the Savanna. Farming these can be tedious due to the high density of aggressive mobs like Morus, so it is best to lure the target away from the pack before engaging.

## Advanced Crafting and Materials

As you move into the mid-to-late game, your **ammo box pilgrammed** will be supporting much hungrier weapons. You will need to venture into Egg Roost Island and Cloud City to find the rarest materials.

*   **Diamond Pebbles:** Found on Egg Roost Island. These are essential for high-tier accessory upgrades like the Diamond Bracelet.
*   **Scrap Metal:** Dropped by mechanical enemies or found in shipwrecks. Necessary for crafting advanced ammunition types like Piercing Bullets or Pop-up Pellets.
*   **Sulfur:** Found exclusively in the Volcano. You will need a significant amount of this for any gunpowder-based weaponry.

> **Warning:** Entering the Volcano without heat-resistant gear or high health regen is a recipe for disaster. Speed is your friend when mining Sulfur.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the ammo box pilgrammed actually give infinite ammo?**

A: No, the Ammo Box generally functions as an accessory that increases your ammo capacity or provides buffs to ranged damage/reload speed depending on the current game version. You still need to manage your resources, but it makes the "Gun Only" playstyle much more viable.

**Q: Where is the best place to farm Gold for the Gunslinger set?**

A: The Savanna and the Desert are the most efficient areas for early-to-mid game gold farming. Killing Hunters for the Ranger Emblem also provides a decent amount of Gold as a byproduct.

**Q: Can I use the Ammo Box with magic staves?**

A: No, the Ammo Box specifically targets "Physical" ranged weapons like guns and bows. Magic builds require different accessories, such as Mana Crystals or Mage Robes, to increase their effectiveness.

**Q: Is the Blunderbuss better than the Revolver?**

A: The Blunderbuss acts more like a shotgun, dealing high damage in a wide spread at close range. The Revolver is better for consistent DPS and mid-range encounters. Most players prefer the Revolver for boss fights because it allows for safer positioning while maintaining high damage output.

## Unlocking the Blackbird Pilgrammed Skill

The "Blackbird" skill, often associated with the Great Redbird Feather, is the pinnacle of movement in the game. It allows players to perform a mid-air jump and extend their dives, effectively mimicking the buffs of Scurry Boots without occupying a valuable equipment slot. To obtain this skill, you must interact with the Slingshot NPC after gathering several rare materials.

### Required Materials for Blackbird

Before embarking on the journey to the far islands, you must ensure your inventory is stocked with the necessary components. The Slingshot NPC will not grant the skill until every item is present.

| Item | Quantity | Source/Location |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cloud Boots** | 1 | Purchased from the Shop Vendor |
| **Royal Rubber** | 20 | Dropped by Royal/Imperial enemies |
| **Great Redbird Feather** | 1 | Stolen from the Sleeping Redbird |
| **Gold/Currency** | Varies | General gameplay and questing |

> **💡 Tip:** Always buy the Cloud Boots first. They are a prerequisite for the quest and provide a baseline jump boost that makes the platforming sections significantly easier.

## The Journey for the Great Redbird Feather

The most difficult part of the **blackbird pilgrammed** quest is obtaining the Great Redbird Feather. This item is located on a remote island that requires precise navigation and the use of environmental boosters.

1.  **Reaching the Imperial Island:** Start by traveling to the island inhabited by Imperial Knights. If you have the Noxious Mortar, you can use it to launch yourself toward the smaller islands to save time.
2.  **The Teleporter Shortcut:** If you lack high-tier launch items, look for the teleporter located behind the Imperial Knight camp. This will transport you to a smaller sub-island.
3.  **The White Orb Sequence:** Once on the small island, look for floating white orbs. Equipping your glider and flying over these orbs will provide a massive lift. You must chain these orbs together, as their boost is temporary.
4.  **Avoiding the Tangerines:** As you ascend toward the final orb, aggressive "Tangerine" enemies will begin to spawn. These enemies can hit-stun you out of your glide, leading to a fatal fall. Do not attempt to parry while gliding; instead, focus on erratic movement to dodge their projectiles.
5.  **The Sleeping Redbird:** At the very top of the sequence, you will find a large, sleeping bird. Approach it carefully and interact with it to "steal" the feather.

## Essential Movement Techniques

While the **blackbird pilgrammed** feather is the star of the show, there are other skills you should acquire to round out your character's mobility. These skills are often found through specific NPC quests scattered throughout the world.

### The Duke of Rocks: Wall Climbing

The first skill most players should aim for is the Wall Climb. This is obtained by talking to the Duke of Rocks and completing his mountain-themed quest.

*   **Quest Objective:** Kill a specific number of mountain enemies and reach the summit of the Duke's mountain.
*   **Reward:** The ability to climb walls three times consecutively after touching the ground.
*   **Utility:** This functions as a permanent version of the Climbing Claws, freeing up an accessory slot for more combat-oriented items.

### The Elite Barracks: Dash Technique

For those focused on combat, the Dash Technique is a must-have. Located at the Elite Barracks, this skill is provided by an NPC named Gareth (or Garth).

| Skill Name | NPC | Location | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Empowered Dash** | Gareth | Elite Barracks | Quicker rolls with increased distance |
| **Wall Climb** | Duke of Rocks | The Mountain | 3x consecutive vertical climbs |
| **Double Jump** | Slingshot NPC | Starter Area/Shop | Mid-air jump and dive extension |

> **Warning:** To unlock the Dash Technique, you may need a high reputation level with the Elite Knight Guild or have defeated the Skeleton boss. Check your guild progress before heading to the barracks.

## Advanced Navigation and Combat Tips

Integrating the **blackbird pilgrammed** skill into your regular gameplay requires a bit of practice. The mid-air jump isn't just for reaching high places; it is a vital tool for resetting your momentum during boss fights.

### Combining Skills for Maximum Airtime
By combining the Wall Climb from the Duke of Rocks with the Double Jump from the Redbird feather, you can scale almost any vertical surface in the game. Start with your three climbs, jump away from the wall, use your mid-air jump to return to the wall, and your climb charges will often partially refresh depending on your current buffs.

### The "Dive Extension" Mechanic
When using the Blackbird skill, pressing the jump button again while falling initiates a dive extension. This allows you to clear massive horizontal gaps that a standard glide cannot reach. This is particularly useful in the 2026 map expansions where islands are spaced further apart.

## Farming Royal Rubber
Since 20 Royal Rubbers are required for the **blackbird pilgrammed** unlock, you should focus on farming Imperial Knights or high-level mechanical enemies. These enemies are found most commonly in the late-game desert or tundra biomes. 

| Enemy Type | Drop Rate | Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Imperial Knight** | High | Medium |
| **Mechanical Scout** | Medium | Low |
| **Elite Guard** | Guaranteed | High |

For the most efficient farming, use a weapon with high "Impact" stats to break the guards of the Imperial Knights quickly.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I get the blackbird pilgrammed skill without Cloud Boots?**

A: No, the Slingshot NPC specifically requires Cloud Boots to be in your possession (or equipped) to trigger the dialogue for the skill. They are a fundamental part of the upgrade path.

**Q: What happens if I fall during the white orb sequence?**

A: If you fall, you will likely take significant fall damage or respawn at your last checkpoint. It is highly recommended to set your spawn point at the Imperial Island before attempting the climb to the Redbird.

**Q: Is the blackbird pilgrammed double jump better than Scurry Boots?**

A: It provides the same primary movement benefits as Scurry Boots but as a permanent skill. This is superior because it allows you to wear other boots, such as those that provide defense or speed buffs, while still retaining the double jump ability.

**Q: How do I stop the Tangerines from knocking me down?**

A: The best strategy is to keep moving. The Tangerines have a predictable firing pattern. If you move in a slight zig-zag motion while gliding between the white orbs, most of their attacks will miss. You can also use items that provide temporary invincibility or high poise if you find yourself getting hit-stunned frequently.

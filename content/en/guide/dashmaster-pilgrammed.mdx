Mobility is the cornerstone of survival in the treacherous world of Pilgrammed. Whether you are dodging the lethal strikes of the Ice King or navigating the floating islands of Cloud City, having the right movement skills is often more important than your raw damage output. The **dashmaster pilgrammed** upgrade, in particular, has become a "must-have" for players looking to optimize their builds for the 2026 meta. This skill allows for rapid repositioning, making it significantly easier to avoid area-of-effect attacks and close the gap on elusive enemies.

In this guide, we will break down exactly how to secure the **dashmaster pilgrammed** skill, along with other essential movement masteries like the Climbing Claws and Jump Master. These upgrades are not just luxury items; they are fundamental mechanics that change how you interact with the game's vertical environments and high-speed combat encounters. Follow these steps to ensure your character is as agile as possible.

## How to Unlock Dashmaster in Pilgrammed

The Dashmaster skill is one of the most sought-after movement upgrades because it provides a reliable burst of speed on a short cooldown. To obtain this, you need to seek out a specific NPC located in a dangerous territory.

### Locating Garth at Elite Brax
To begin your journey toward the **dashmaster pilgrammed** upgrade, you must head toward the Elite Brax area. This region is known for its tough enemies, so ensure you are adequately geared before making the trek. You are looking for an NPC named Garth. 

While the specific level requirements can vary based on world state, most players find that simply interacting with Garth after reaching the mid-game progression is enough to trigger the dialogue for the upgrade. Once you speak with him, he will grant you the Dashmaster skill, which can be tracked in your movement menu.

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Location** | Elite Brax |
| **NPC** | Garth |
| **Primary Benefit** | Rapid horizontal dash for dodging and traversal |
| **Skill Category** | Dash Master |

> **Warning:** The path to Garth is often patrolled by high-level mobs. It is recommended to use stealth or high-speed gear like Sandy Boots to bypass unnecessary fights if you are under-leveled.

## Climbing Claws: Mastering Verticality

Before or after obtaining your **dashmaster pilgrammed** skill, you should prioritize the Climbing Claws (often referred to as Climb Master). This upgrade functions similarly to the Climbing Claws item but exists as a permanent movement upgrade, freeing up your accessory slots for more combat-oriented gear.

### Finding the Duke of Rocks
The Duke of Rocks is the gatekeeper for this skill. You can find him in the Mountain region. Start at the main cabin in the Mountain area and head to the right. 

1.  **Navigate the Mountain:** Follow the path past the Ice King's arena. (Note: The Ice King has been reworked in 2026, so be prepared for a fight if you draw his aggro).
2.  **Locate the Duke:** He is typically found perched on a rocky outcrop.
3.  **Dialogue:** Speak with him to receive the "Climb Master" upgrade.

| Feature | Climbing Claws (Climb Master) |
|---|---|
| **Usage** | Allows sticking to and scaling vertical walls |
| **Limit** | Can be used up to three times before needing a reset |
| **Location** | Mountain Region (Duke of Rocks) |

## Jump Master: The Cloud City Tech

The final piece of the movement trinity is the Jump Master upgrade (associated with Scurry Boots tech). This is arguably the most difficult to obtain due to the material requirements and the platforming challenge involved.

### Adagio's Request
In Cloud City, near the central slingshot hub, you will find an NPC named Adagio. He requires a set of rare materials before he will teach you the Jump Master techniques.

**Required Materials for Jump Master:**
*   **20 World Rubber:** Generally found through exploration or specific mob drops.
*   **Cloud Boots:** A standard movement item found in Cloud City chests or shops.
*   **Great Red Bird Feather:** The rarest component, requiring a specific trek to a remote island.

> **Tip:** Equip a Sport Shawl while navigating Cloud City. The local enemies (Tendrilings) are incredibly aggressive and can knock you off platforms easily. The Sport Shawl helps mitigate some of the frustration during the climb.

## Obtaining the Great Red Bird Feather

Securing the feather is a test of your existing movement skills, including how well you can utilize the **dashmaster pilgrammed** ability in mid-air.

### The Noxious Motor Strategy
To reach the Great Red Bird, you will need a **Noxious Motor**. This item provides the necessary lift to reach the higher elevations of the remote islands.

1.  **Locate the White Orbs:** These orbs are scattered across the floating islands. To use them, you must have your glider held out.
2.  **Orb Chain:** Hit the first white orb to be propelled upward. Use your glider to steer toward the next orb.
3.  **Avoid Tendrilings:** These enemies will attempt to intercept your flight. Use your dash to adjust your trajectory if they get too close.
4.  **The Red Bird:** Once you reach the highest peak, you will see a large red bird. You do not need to kill it; simply interact with it to "pluck" the feather.

| Step | Action | Item Needed |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Travel to the remote Cloud City island | Noxious Motor |
| **2** | Use white orbs for elevation | Glider |
| **3** | Reach the summit | **Dashmaster Pilgrammed** (for air control) |
| **4** | Collect Feather | Interaction |

## Movement Upgrade Synergy

Once you have unlocked Dash Master, Climb Master, and Jump Master, your traversal capabilities in Pilgrammed increase exponentially. You can chain these abilities together to reach areas that were previously inaccessible.

### The "Infinite" Climb Combo
By combining the Climbing Claws with the **dashmaster pilgrammed** skill, you can scale almost any surface.
*   **Climb:** Use your first two climb charges to gain height.
*   **Dash:** Dash away from the wall and then immediately back toward it.
*   **Reset:** This often allows for a slight reset in momentum, letting you maximize the third climb charge.

### Combat Applications
In boss fights, movement is your best defense. The Dashmaster skill provides I-frames (invincibility frames) in some versions of the game or at least moves your hitbox fast enough to clear projectile paths. Pairing this with the Jump Master's enhanced verticality allows you to stay above ground-based shockwaves, a common mechanic in 2026 boss encounters.

| Build Type | Recommended Movement Skill | Why? |
|---|---|---|
| **Melee/Tank** | **Dashmaster Pilgrammed** | Essential for closing distance and sticking to bosses. |
| **Ranged/Archer** | Jump Master | Allows for maintaining high-ground advantage. |
| **Explorer** | Climbing Claws | Necessary for finding hidden chests in the Mountains. |

## Essential Gear for Movement Builds

While the skills are permanent, certain items can enhance your **dashmaster pilgrammed** experience. 

1.  **Noxious Motor:** As mentioned, this is vital for vertical launches.
2.  **Sandy Boots:** Increases base run speed, which scales the distance of your dash.
3.  **Sport Shawl:** Reduces knockback and annoyance from minor mobs.
4.  **Heavy Rusty Bow:** Interestingly, some players use the recoil of heavy weapons to add a "fourth" movement option, pushing themselves backward in mid-air.

> **Expert Advice:** Always check your "Movement Upgrades" tab in the menu to confirm which skills are active. It is easy to forget to talk to an NPC and miss out on the permanent version of a skill you thought you already had via an item.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the dashmaster pilgrammed skill better than just using Scurry Boots?**

A: Yes, because the Dashmaster skill is a permanent movement upgrade that does not take up an accessory slot. This allows you to stack the benefits of the dash with other powerful combat accessories.

**Q: Where can I find World Rubber for Adagio's quest?**

A: World Rubber is most commonly found in the industrial or scrap-heavy areas of the map. In 2026, many players farm it in the outskirts of Cloud City where mechanical enemies frequent.

**Q: Does the Great Red Bird fight back when you pluck the feather?**

A: The bird itself is relatively passive during the interaction, but the challenge lies in the environment. The high altitude and surrounding Tendrilings are the real threats. If you fall, you will likely need to restart the entire climb from the first white orb.

**Q: Can I use the dashmaster pilgrammed ability while in the air?**

A: Yes, the dash can be used mid-air to adjust your landing or to reach a platform that is just out of jumping range. This is a vital technique for the more advanced platforming sections in the late-game regions.

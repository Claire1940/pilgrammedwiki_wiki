## What is the Adagio Skill?

Adagio is a permanent movement skill that enhances your character's natural agility. Unlike temporary consumables or specific armor buffs, once you unlock Adagio, it becomes a core part of your kit. 

The primary benefit of Adagio is the **Double Jump**. This allows you to reach ledges that were previously inaccessible and provides a secondary window to dodge ground-based shockwaves from bosses. Additionally, the skill allows you to extend your "dive" mechanic. By pressing the jump button mid-air during a dive, you gain a slight horizontal boost and height, effectively mimicking the effects of the Scurry Boots without taking up an equipment slot.

## Required Materials for Adagio

Unlocking the **adagio pilgrammed** skill isn't as simple as talking to an NPC. You must prove your worth by gathering rare items and completing a dangerous aerial challenge. The quest is handed out by the NPC known as **Prejunks**, who is located near the slingshot in the main hub area.

To complete the quest, you will need the following items:

1.  **Cloud Boots:** These must be purchased from the shop vendor.
2.  **20 Royal Rubber:** Collected from various mechanical or royal enemies throughout the world.
3.  **1 Great Redbird Feather:** This is the rarest component, found on a remote island.

## Step-by-Step Guide to the Great Redbird Feather

The most difficult part of obtaining the **adagio pilgrammed** skill is retrieving the feather. This requires you to travel to a far-off island and navigate a series of "white orbs" that grant temporary flight.

### Reaching the Island
To get to the island where the Great Redbird resides, head toward the Imperial Knight territory. There is a small island nearby with a teleporter. If you have the **Noxious Mortar**, you can use it to launch yourself toward the higher platforms. If not, follow the standard path past the Imperial Knights and take the teleporter located beneath the main bridge.

### The Aerial Challenge
Once you reach the island, you will see glowing white orbs. Equipping your glider is mandatory here. 
1.  **Jump into the first orb:** This will launch you upward. 
2.  **Chain the orbs:** You must move quickly from one orb to the next. The flight buff lasts only a few seconds.
3.  **Avoid the Tangerines:** As you ascend, hostile "Tangerine" enemies will spawn. They will try to knock you out of the air. Since you cannot parry while gliding, you must manually dodge their projectiles.
4.  **Climbing Claws/Clamber:** Use your wall-climbing skills if you fall short of a platform to regain your footing and reach the next orb.

### Stealing the Feather
At the very top of the island, you will find a large, sleeping Redbird. Do not attempt to fight it unless you are extremely over-leveled. Simply walk up to the bird and interact with it to "steal" the feather. Once you have the Great Redbird Feather in your inventory, you can safely teleport back to the town.

## Finalizing the Adagio Skill

With the Cloud Boots, 20 Royal Rubber, and the Great Redbird Feather in hand, return to **Prejunks** at the slingshot. Talk to him and select the option to turn in the materials. He will then teach you the Adagio technique.

> **💡 Tip:** If Prejunks doesn't give you the quest immediately, ensure you have completed the basic tutorial quests and have at least one other movement skill unlocked, as some players report a hidden progression requirement.

## Advanced Movement with Adagio

Once you have unlocked **adagio pilgrammed**, your movement options expand significantly. You can now perform "super-jumps" by combining the double jump with specific weapon arts.

### Combat Applications
In boss fights like the Old King or the Mountain Golem, Adagio allows you to stay airborne longer than usual. This is particularly useful for avoiding ground-slams. 
*   **The Dive Extension:** While in the air, press the dive key, then immediately press jump. This gives you a burst of speed that can get you out of the range of a boss's "aoe" (area of effect) attack.
*   **Aerial Recovery:** If you are knocked off a platform, the double jump can save you from a lethal fall into the void.

### Exploration Synergy
Adagio works best when paired with the **Clamber** skill (obtained from the Duke of Rocks). While Clamber lets you scale vertical walls, Adagio allows you to jump *away* from a wall and then jump back toward it, effectively resetting your climb count or allowing you to navigate around overhangs that would normally block your path.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I get Adagio without buying Cloud Boots?**

A: No, the Cloud Boots are a required quest item. Even if you have other boots equipped, you must have the Cloud Boots in your inventory or have purchased them for Prejunks to recognize the quest completion.

**Q: Does the Adagio double jump use stamina?**

A: Yes, the double jump and the extended dive both consume a small portion of your stamina bar. It is recommended to invest points into your Stamina stat if you plan on using **adagio pilgrammed** frequently in combat.

**Q: Is Adagio better than the Scurry Boots?**

A: Adagio provides the same benefits as the Scurry Boots' ability but as a permanent skill. This means you can wear higher-defense boots (like the Magma Boots or Knight Sabatons) while still enjoying the mobility of the Scurry Boots.

**Q: What happens if I die while trying to get the Great Redbird Feather?**

A: If you die, you will lose any progress on that specific island run and will need to start the orb-chaining sequence from the beginning. However, you do not lose the items you have already collected (like the Royal Rubber).

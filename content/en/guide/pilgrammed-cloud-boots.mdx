Navigating the vertical landscapes of Cloud City and the treacherous peaks of the Mountains requires more than just basic platforming skills. In the current 2026 meta, obtaining the **pilgrammed cloud boots** has become a priority for players looking to master the "Jump Master" movement tech. These boots aren't just a simple stat buff; they represent a fundamental shift in how you traverse the world, allowing for a "Scurry Boots" style of movement that is essential for end-game boss fights and exploration.

Mastering the **pilgrammed cloud boots** involves a multi-step questline that takes you from the heights of the Cloud City slingshots to a remote island guarded by lethal avian creatures. Whether you are a veteran player or a newcomer trying to optimize your mobility, understanding the specific requirements—including the elusive Great Red Bird Feather—is key to unlocking your character's full potential. ## How to Unlock the Pilgrammed Cloud Boots

The journey to acquiring the **pilgrammed cloud boots** begins in Cloud City, a region known for its high-altitude platforms and reliance on slingshot travel. To start the process, you must locate an NPC named Adagio. Adagio is typically found situated near the primary slingshot hubs. Speaking with him reveals the blueprint for the movement upgrade, but he won't hand it over for free.

To craft this upgrade, you will need a specific set of rare materials. Unlike basic armor sets, these components require you to have explored significant portions of the map and defeated specific environmental hazards.

### Required Materials for Cloud Boots

| Material | Quantity | Primary Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **World Rubber** | 20 | Foraged in various world biomes |
| **Cloud Boots (Item)** | 1 | Purchased or found in Cloud City chests |
| **Great Red Bird Feather** | 1 | Plucked from the Great Red Bird on the remote island |

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Great Red Bird island is extremely dangerous. Many enemies in this area can one-shot players with low defense or poor positioning.

## Locating the Great Red Bird Feather

The most difficult component of the **pilgrammed cloud boots** is undoubtedly the Great Red Bird Feather. To find it, you must travel to a specific isolated island reachable from the outskirts of Cloud City. This island is infested with Tendrilings—agile, aggressive enemies that can easily knock you off platforms.

To reach the summit where the Great Red Bird resides, you should equip a **Noxious Motor** and a **Glider**. The path involves a series of "white orbs" suspended in the air. When you hold out your glider and make contact with these orbs, they propel you upward, allowing you to reach higher elevations that are otherwise inaccessible.

### Step-by-Step Feather Retrieval

1.  **Prepare your Build:** It is highly recommended to wear a **Sport Shawl** to mitigate environmental annoyance and use a ranged weapon like the **Heavy Rusty Bow** to clear Tendrilings from a distance.
2.  **Navigate to the Island:** Head past the main Cloud City structures toward the isolated northern islands.
3.  **Use the White Orbs:** Deploy your glider and aim for the glowing white orbs. Each orb provides a significant vertical boost.
4.  **Avoid the Guardian:** A powerful, high-damage enemy patrols the lower levels of the bird's peak. It is often safer to bypass this enemy using the Noxious Motor's verticality rather than engaging in a direct fight.
5.  **Pluck the Feather:** Once you reach the very top, you will find a large, stationary Red Bird. Interact with it to "pluck" the feather. You do not need to kill the bird to receive the item.

## Other Essential Movement Upgrades

While the **pilgrammed cloud boots** (Jump Master) are vital, they are part of a trio of "Prejunks" movement upgrades introduced to improve player agency. To become a true master of mobility, you should also seek out the Climbing Claws and the Dash Master skill.

### Climbing Claws (Climb Master)
Located in the Mountain biome, you must find the **Duke of Rocks**. He resides in a cabin to the right of the Ice King's arena. Speaking with him grants you the Climbing Claws movement upgrade, which functions similarly to the old item but as a permanent skill. This allows you to scale vertical surfaces up to three times before needing to refresh your stamina.

### Dash Master
To unlock the Dash Master upgrade, you must visit **Garth** at the Elite Brax location. While the specific level requirements can vary, most players who have reached Cloud City will meet the criteria. This upgrade significantly improves your horizontal mobility and provides i-frames during the animation, which is crucial for dodging boss mechanics.

| Upgrade Name | Trainer NPC | Location | Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Climb Master** | Duke of Rocks | Mountain Cabin | Enhanced wall climbing |
| **Dash Master** | Garth | Elite Brax | Triple dash capability |
| **Jump Master** | Adagio | Cloud City | Scurry movement (Cloud Boots) |

## Combat Synergy and Build Recommendations

The **pilgrammed cloud boots** shine brightest when paired with a build that rewards high mobility. Because the "Scurry" tech allows for rapid repositioning, glass cannon builds (high damage, low health) become much more viable.

If you are struggling with the platforming required for these upgrades, consider using **Sandy Boots** temporarily. They provide a more stable footing in slippery environments, though they lack the explosive verticality of the completed Cloud Boots upgrade. 

> **💡 Tip:** Check your progress in the "Tech" or "Skills" menu. You should see entries for Climb Master, Dash Master, and Jump Master once these are successfully unlocked.

For those using a bow build, the ability to "scurry" away while drawing a heavy string (like the Heavy Rusty Bow) allows you to maintain distance from aggressive melee mobs. This is particularly effective against the reworked Ice King, who now requires more precise movement to avoid his AOE shards.

## Exploring the Cloud City Biome

Cloud City is more than just a place to pick up your **pilgrammed cloud boots**. It is a hub for high-tier materials and unique NPC interactions. When exploring, always keep an eye on your stamina bar. The "slingshot" mechanics of the city can often launch you into areas where a fall would mean instant death if your glider isn't ready.

## FAQ

**Q: Where exactly is Adagio located for the pilgrammed cloud boots?**

A: Adagio is found in Cloud City, usually standing near the central slingshot platforms. He is the NPC responsible for the "Jump Master" upgrade which utilizes the Cloud Boots.

**Q: Do I need to kill the Great Red Bird to get the feather?**

A: No, you do not need to kill the bird. You simply need to navigate to its nest at the highest point of the island and interact with it to "pluck" the feather. This is much safer than attempting to fight the bird or its guardians.

**Q: What is the "Scurry" tech mentioned in the guide?**

A: Scurry tech refers to a specific movement pattern enabled by the **pilgrammed cloud boots** that allows for faster-than-normal ground movement and improved air control, similar to the mechanics of the Scurry Boots item but integrated as a permanent movement upgrade.

**Q: Can I get the Cloud Boots upgrade without the Noxious Motor?**

A: While technically possible with very precise platforming and glider usage, it is extremely difficult. The Noxious Motor provides the necessary vertical lift to reach the white orbs that lead to the Great Red Bird, making the process significantly more consistent.

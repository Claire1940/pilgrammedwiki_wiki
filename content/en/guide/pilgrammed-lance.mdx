## Skill Tree Allocation

To make the most of your lance, your skill tree must balance offensive pressure with reliable healing. Since this build does not utilize the standard "Bloodthirsty" talent, we rely on alternative healing methods to stay in the fight.

| Skill Talent | Purpose | Key Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Thirsty Ace** | Core Damage | Synergizes with fast-hitting weapons for consistent output. |
| **Juggernaut** | Survivability | Provides 10-12% damage increase and essential damage reduction. |
| **Rally** | Sustain | Essential for healing since we lack the Bloodthirsty talent. |
| **Fear Monger** | Extra Scaling | Uses leftover points to push your damage ceiling even higher. |

> **Warning:** Do not skip the Rally talent. Without it, your high-speed attacks won't provide the sustain needed to survive boss AOE attacks or missed parries.

## Obtaining Build Components

Acquiring these items requires a journey across the various biomes of Pilgrammed. Some items are guaranteed drops or crafts, while others require a bit of luck with NPC spawns.

### 1. The Sand Pod Helmet
Travel to the **Deep Desert** and hunt Sand Pods. They are relatively common enemies, but you may need to farm them for a short duration to secure the helmet. This piece is vital for the movement speed it provides, allowing you to stay glued to bosses.

### 2. The Diamond Ring
This is one of the more elusive items. You must locate the **Little Green Man**, an NPC with a random chance to spawn in the Forest, Volcano, Desert, Prairie, or Cloud City. 
*   **Cost:** 3 Diamonds and a significant amount of Gold.
*   **Pro Tip:** The best place to grind for Diamonds in 2026 is **Egg Roost Island**.

### 3. The Combo Glove
Head to the **Savannah** and farm Copper Ants. While these are considered "trash mobs" by high-level players, they are the only source for this build-defining glove.

### 4. The Knight's Talisman
This item must be crafted at the NPC Arbord. You will need to gather several high-tier materials:
*   **600 Gold**
*   **6 Iron**
*   **6 Mithril:** Found in the Tundra and Mountain Post areas.
*   **Dead Metal:** Found exclusively in the Volcano region.

### 5. The Rubber Band
Return to the Observatory and cross over to **Egg Roost Island**. You will need to defeat "Eggies." While these enemies are notoriously annoying due to their movement patterns, the 10% damage boost from the Rubber Band is worth the frustration.

## Gameplay Strategy and Combat Tips

When using a **pilgrammed lance**, your goal is "uptime." The more time you spend actively hitting the boss, the higher your damage becomes due to the stacking nature of your accessories.

1.  **Ignore Knockback:** The Rubber Band removes your ability to deal knockback. In most games, this would be a disadvantage, but in [Pilgrammed](https://www.roblox.com/games/11431871221/Pilgrammed-BETA), bosses are often immune to knockback anyway. Removing it allows you to stay in melee range without pushing the enemy away.
2.  **Mana Management:** Since the Knight's Talisman consumes all Mana for a damage buff, do not bother with spells or Mana-heavy abilities. Your lance is your only tool.
3.  **Crit Fishing:** With the Sand Pod Helmet and Mysterious Stone, your crit rate should be high. This triggers the healing from your Patched Armor, allowing you to "tank" minor hits while continuing your combo.

> **Tip:** If you find yourself dying too quickly, swap one accessory for a defensive charm, but be aware that this will significantly lower your "melt" speed against bosses.

## FAQ

**Q: Why use Patched Armor instead of Liar Armor for a pilgrammed lance build?**

A: While Liar Armor claims to offer 15% faster swing speed, many players find that swing speed hits a cap. In practical testing, the healing-on-crit provided by Patched Armor offers much better sustainability during high-level boss fights without a noticeable loss in speed.

**Q: Where is the best place to farm Mithril for the Knight's Talisman?**

A: Mithril is most abundant in the Tundra and around the Mountain Post. Look for blue-tinted ore veins. Having a high-tier pickaxe will speed up this process significantly.

**Q: Does the Combo Glove bonus reset if I take damage?**

A: No, the Combo Glove bonus is based on completing your melee combo hits. As long as you continue attacking and finishing your chains, the 50% damage bonus will remain active.

**Q: Is the Prism Lance the only viable pilgrammed lance?**

A: While there are other lances, the Prism Lance has the best synergy with the current 2026 crit and speed meta. Other lances may offer more utility or defensive stats but won't reach the "insane DPS" levels required for 30-second boss clears.

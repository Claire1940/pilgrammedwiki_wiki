## Scrap Lance Stats and Moveset Analysis

The **scrap lance pilgrammed** is categorized as a spear-type weapon, meaning it excels in linear, piercing attacks. Unlike heavy broadswords or fast-paced daggers, the lance allows you to strike from a safer distance, often hitting enemies before they can enter their own attack range.

The weapon features a reliable M1 (light attack) combo that deals consistent piercing damage with a chance to inflict the Bleed status effect. Bleed is particularly effective against high-health targets, as it deals percentage-based damage over time, allowing you to focus on dodging while the enemy's health slowly depletes.

### The Heavy Attack: Tactical Retreat
The heavy attack (M2) of the Scrap Lance is its most distinctive feature. Instead of a standard lunge, the player performs a powerful kick that launches them backward. This move serves a dual purpose: it deals significant knockback to the enemy and provides the player with an immediate escape route. This is invaluable during boss fights where an enemy might be preparing a devastating close-range area-of-effect (AoE) attack.

| Attack Type | Effect | Utility |
|---|---|---|
| **Light (M1)** | Piercing + Bleed | Primary DPS and status application |
| **Heavy (M2)** | Kick + Self-Launch | Disengagement and spacing tool |
| **Passive** | Venom Synergy | Bonus damage with Serpent Style |

## Serpent Style Synergy: Maximizing Damage

What truly elevates the **scrap lance pilgrammed** from a decent weapon to a top-tier choice is its hidden passive ability. When paired with the "Serpent Style" technique, the lance gains a massive damage boost specifically related to Venom. 

Serpent Style is a combat technique that can be discovered in the Desert region. Once equipped, it modifies how your character interacts with poison and venom-based status effects. The Scrap Lance specifically checks if the player has this style active; if they do, the weapon's damage scaling increases significantly whenever the target is poisoned.

### Where to Find Serpent Style
To unlock this synergy, travel to the Desert biome and look for the hidden master near the ancient ruins. You will likely need to complete a short trial or provide specific items to learn the technique. Once mastered, your Scrap Lance builds should focus on maximizing "Venom" application through accessories or gems to ensure the passive damage bonus is always active.

## Recommended Builds for Scrap Lance Users

To get the most out of your **scrap lance pilgrammed**, your gear should complement its status-heavy nature. Since the weapon relies on Bleed and Venom (via Serpent Style), look for armor sets that increase status duration or damage.

1. **The Venomous Duelist**: Focus on the Serpent Style technique. Use accessories like the "Serpent's Fang" to apply venom on every hit. This ensures the Scrap Lance's passive is always triggered.
2. **The Bleed Tank**: Pair the lance with heavy armor that provides high poise. This allows you to trade hits while applying Bleed stacks, eventually winning the war of attrition.
3. **The Mobility Scout**: Utilize the M2 kick in combination with mobility-focused gems. This build is excellent for PvP, as it makes you incredibly difficult to pin down.

> **Warning:** The M2 kick consumes a fair amount of stamina. If you spam it too often to escape, you may find yourself unable to sprint or dodge when you truly need to.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the Scrap Lance better than the Mythril Spear?**

A: While the Mythril Spear has higher base raw damage, the **scrap lance pilgrammed** is superior for technical builds. The combination of Bleed and the Serpent Style passive allows it to out-damage the Mythril Spear in long-duration fights.

**Q: Can I get Lance Fragments from any enemy in the Swamp?**

A: No, Lance Fragments are specifically dropped by Viper Heads. While other enemies in the Swamp might drop useful crafting materials, only the Viper Head carries the fragments needed for Abelly's shop.

**Q: Does the M2 kick deal damage?**

A: Yes, the kick deals a moderate amount of physical damage and high knockback. However, its primary purpose is repositioning rather than being a main source of DPS.

**Q: Where exactly is Abelly located?**

A: Abelly can be found in a small shack in the mid-section of the Swamp. Look for a structure near the edge of the water, usually guarded by a few smaller swamp mobs.

## The New Dagger Meta: Pitch Dagger and Blacksteel

The 2026 updates have introduced a variety of weapons that have shifted the power balance toward high-dexterity builds. For players looking to maximize their efficiency, the Pitch Dagger is a mandatory acquisition. Found in the Swamp region, this weapon introduces a unique "dash" mechanic that rewards aggressive aerial play. 

When you land an aerial attack with the Pitch Dagger, your next light attack (M1) triggers a forward dash. This mobility is essential for dodging area-of-effect attacks while maintaining constant pressure on the enemy. However, the true power of this weapon class is unlocked when you upgrade it to the Blacksteel.

### How to Obtain and Upgrade

To get started with your dagger journey, you must locate the shopkeeper named Fang in the Swamp. Fang sells the base components for several high-tier builds.

| Weapon | Location | Cost/Requirement | Special Ability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Elf Blade** | Swamp (Fang) | Gold | Triple heavy attack combo capability. |

| **Blacksteel** | Volcano (Chasm) | Pitch Dagger + Blackbird Trade | Direct upgrade; higher base damage and scaling. |

> **💡 Tip:** To reach the Blackbird for the Blacksteel upgrade, head to the Volcano's chasm and use Noxious Water or precise platforming along the side cliffs to reach the trading NPC.

## Advanced Dagger Techniques and Combos

Using a **pilgrammed dagger sheath** build effectively requires more than just clicking rapidly. You must master the "Aerial Dash" loop. By jumping and hitting an enemy, you prime your dash. Use this dash to reposition behind the enemy, then immediately follow up with a heavy attack.

1.  **Initiate:** Jump and land a light attack (M1).
2.  **Dash:** Immediately press M1 again to dash through or toward the target.
3.  **Combo:** If using the Elf Blade, follow up with the three-part heavy attack combo.
4.  **Disengage:** Use the Scrap Lance’s heavy kick if you need to create distance.

The Scrap Lance is a notable mention for dagger users because it scales with similar stats. It deals bleed damage and has a passive that increases venom damage if you have the Serpent Style technique equipped. This makes it an excellent secondary weapon for a **pilgrammed dagger sheath** user who needs a bit more reach than a standard dagger provides.

## Summary of the Dagger Playstyle

The dagger playstyle in 2026 is defined by mobility. Unlike the heavy-hitting but slow Magma Shackles or the cumbersome Rusty Bell, daggers allow for a "hit and run" strategy. By utilizing the **pilgrammed dagger sheath** approach—layering your accessories to focus on speed—you can tackle endgame content like the Catacombs with ease.

The Catacombs introduce a weapon called the "Very Funny Joke," which is a complex mage/melee hybrid. While interesting, it lacks the consistent DPS and reliability of a well-built Blacksteel dagger set. Stick to the blades if you prefer a high-skill ceiling and the ability to dodge almost any incoming attack through superior movement.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the Dagger Sheath accessory in Pilgrammed?**

A: While "Dagger Sheath" is often used as a term for the build style, the most similar actual items are the **Warrior's Sheath** (found early game) and the **Thief's Emblem**. These items provide the necessary buffs to make your **pilgrammed dagger sheath** build viable by increasing speed and critical damage.

**Q: Is the Blacksteel better than the Bone Claws?**

A: It depends on your playstyle. The Blacksteel offers better mobility through its dash mechanic, whereas the Bone Claws offer higher raw DPS when you are in the "Bloodthirst" state. Most players prefer Blacksteel for bosses with high movement and Bone Claws for stationary targets.

**Q: How do I unlock the dash for the Pitch Dagger?**

A: You must land an aerial attack first. Once you hit an enemy while in the air, your very next ground-based M1 attack will turn into a dash. This is the core mechanic of the **pilgrammed dagger sheath** movement meta.

**Q: Can I use the Vine Wand with a dagger build?**

A: Yes! The Vine Wand is an excellent support tool. Its heavy attack summons vines that hold enemies in place, making it significantly easier to land your full dagger combos without the enemy moving out of range.

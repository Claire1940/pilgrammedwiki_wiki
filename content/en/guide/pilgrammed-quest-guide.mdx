## Getting Started: The First Steps and the Thief King

When you first spawn, your inventory contains an Old Sword and an Old Pickaxe. Your immediate priority is gathering resources. Look for small rock outcroppings containing Copper and Tin. Mining these allows you to visit the Blacksmith to craft Bronze, which is the foundation for your first real equipment upgrades.

Your primary quest begins with an NPC named Nebraska. He will task you with eliminating local Thieves, eventually leading to an investigation of the Thief King. Before facing this boss, ensure you have crafted a full set of Bronze armor and a Bronze Sword.

### Boss Strategy: The Thief King

The Thief King is the first major skill check in your journey. Combat in this game revolves around the Parry system. Most of the King's swings can be parried to negate damage and open a window for counter-attacks. However, he possesses a jump attack that must be dodged with a roll, as it cannot be parried.

| Drop Item | Type | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Vuvuzela** | Tool | Summons a Thief minion to fight for you. |
| **Thief Pendant** | Accessory | Chance to spawn a Thief minion when you are hit. |
| **Thief Cloak** | Armor | Grants a Dash ability for better mobility. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** In Pilgrammed, dying results in the loss of 50% of your current gold. Always visit a Banker in major villages to deposit your earnings before attempting a boss fight.

## The Forest and the Hunt for Blunt Damage

After defeating the Thief King, Nebraska will direct you toward the Desert. However, a detour to the Forest is highly recommended. The Forest is home to Ogres, which are relatively easy to kill if you utilize "Sneak Attacks"—approaching enemies from behind to deal massive initial damage.

The goal here is to obtain the **Crude Club**. This weapon deals blunt damage, which is vital for later encounters. Many mid-game bosses, such as Granny, possess high resistance to slashing damage. Using a sword against them will feel like hitting a brick wall, whereas the Crude Club will bypass their defenses.

## Navigating the Desert and "The Door"

The Desert is a significant difficulty spike. Stick to the main paths and avoid the Cactus enemies early on, as they deal extreme damage. Your main objective is to locate "The Door," a sentient quest-giver that provides a series of tasks involving the Raider Captain and local Bandits.

While in the Desert Village, make sure to unlock the Portal. Portals serve as the fast-travel system in Pilgrammed. Once you interact with one, you can return to it at any time. Late-game players eventually acquire the "Poka-Ball" item, allowing for even more efficient travel between these nodes.

### Essential Desert Equipment

The Desert Village smithy offers Iron weapons. Iron is a direct upgrade from Bronze and will be your primary material for a significant portion of the mid-game.

| Item | Material Required | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Iron Pickaxe** | Iron Ore | Faster mining speed; required for Volcano ores. |
| **Fire Knight Armor** | Iron + Fire Essence | High defense and fire resistance. |
| **Night Talisman** | Gold/Quest | Consumes Mana to boost damage by 15%. |

## Advanced Mechanics: Stats and Skill Trees

As you level up, you gain Stat Points and Skill Points. Every level provides one Skill Point, meaning at the level 30 cap, you have 29 points to distribute. 

### Understanding Stats
*   **Strength:** Increases raw physical damage.
*   **Dexterity:** Increases your weapon swing speed.
*   **Defense:** Reduces the amount of damage taken per hit.
*   **Intelligence:** Increases your maximum Mana pool for Mage builds.
*   **Agility:** Increases movement speed and roll distance.

### Recommended Skill Tree Paths

For a beginner following this **pilgrammed quest guide**, the choice between the **Tank** and **Rally** skills is crucial.

1.  **Tank:** Provides a flat HP boost. This is best if you find yourself struggling with parry timings and taking frequent hits.
2.  **Rally:** Restores 5% of your maximum HP every time you successfully parry. This is the superior choice for players who have mastered the combat rhythm, as it provides infinite sustain during long boss fights.

> **💡 Tip:** You can "Ace" a skill by clicking it a second time in the skill tree. This consumes more points but significantly enhances the skill's effect (e.g., Aced Tank provides even more HP).

## Elemental Progression: Volcano and Tundra

Pilgrammed features an elemental system similar to classic RPGs where Fire beats Frost, and Frost beats Fire. To progress efficiently, you should visit the Volcano area after completing the Desert questline.

### The Volcano Village
In the Volcano Village, seek out the merchant who sells the **Metal Sword**. While its base damage might seem comparable to Iron, it applies a burning effect to enemies. This fire damage is incredibly effective against enemies in the Tundra (Mountain) region. Additionally, look for Garen to purchase the **Sunbreaker** armor, which grants massive buffs when you take damage.

### The Mountain (Tundra)
Once you have your fire-based gear, head to the Mountain. You will need **Climbing Gear** to navigate the vertical terrain. This can be purchased from a traveling merchant who spawns either in the Forest or on the path to the Mountain. In the Tundra, you can acquire the **Mithril Sword**, which deals Frost damage—perfect for returning to the Volcano to farm high-level materials.

## Utility and Gold Farming: Fishing

If you find yourself short on gold for reforging or new armor, fishing is the most reliable non-combat income source. To start, head to **Big Bay** near the spawn island. You will need to gather 10 Red Berries and 10 Blue Berries from the Forest to trade for a Fishing Rod.

Fishing involves a mini-game where you must click rapidly when bubbles appear. While common fish can be sold for minor profit, the real goal is catching **Chests**. These chests often contain over 200 gold and have a high chance of dropping rare Gems used for enchanting your gear at the Observatory.

## Reforging and Gems

Late-game progression relies heavily on the Reforge mechanic. Any blacksmith can reforge your weapons or armor for a gold fee, applying a random modifier.

*   **Legendary:** The gold standard for weapons, providing +10% Damage and +10% Swing Speed.
*   **Wonderful:** The best all-around modifier for armor, providing a +2 boost to all primary stats.
*   **Relentless:** Reduces heavy attack cooldowns and end-lag.

Gems, found from "Enhanced" (colored) enemies or fishing chests, can be slotted into your gear at the Observatory. These add elemental effects or utility buffs, such as the Life Gem for lifesteal or the Fire Gem for additional burn damage.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get more Health in Pilgrammed?**

A: You gain a small amount of HP automatically every time you level up. To significantly increase your health, you must invest Skill Points into the "Tank" skill tree. Some armor sets also provide minor health bonuses.

**Q: Where is the best place to farm gold early?**

A: Aside from fishing at Big Bay, farming the Thief King is a great way to earn gold and items to sell. Ensure you always deposit your gold at the Banker in the Desert or Starting Village so you don't lose it upon death.

**Q: What is the best weapon in this pilgrammed quest guide for beginners?**

A: The Crude Club is arguably the most important early weapon because it provides blunt damage, which is necessary to defeat high-armor bosses like Granny. For general mob clearing, the Bronze or Iron Sword is preferred for their speed.

**Q: How do I unlock the Vampire race?**

A: You can find the Vampire NPC in the Forest. Becoming a Vampire allows you to heal HP whenever you kill an enemy, though you take 25% less damage during the day, making it a high-risk, high-reward playstyle for farming.

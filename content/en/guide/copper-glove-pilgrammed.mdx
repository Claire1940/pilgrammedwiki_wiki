## How to Obtain the Copper Glove Pilgrammed

To get your hands on the **copper glove pilgrammed**, you generally have two paths: crafting or purchasing, depending on the current game version and your progression. Most players will opt to craft it to save Gold for other essential upgrades like the Cloud Boots.

### Crafting Requirements
To craft the glove, you will need to visit a Blacksmith NPC. The recipe typically involves basic ores found in the starting plains and the outskirts of the forest.

1.  **Copper Ore**: Gathered from orange-tinted mineral nodes.
2.  **Leather or Cloth**: Dropped by basic animal mobs or purchased from town vendors.
3.  **Basic Tools**: Ensure you have a pickaxe equipped to mine the necessary copper.

> **💡 Tip:** Don't sell your early-game copper! You will need a significant amount of it not just for the glove, but for various armor reinforcements later in the game.

## Upgrading Mobility: From Gloves to Skills

While the **copper glove pilgrammed** is an excellent starting point, the ultimate goal for any serious player is to transition from item-based movement to permanent skills. In the latest 2026 updates, the Duke of Rocks plays a pivotal role in this progression.

According to veteran players, talking to the Duke of Rocks initiates a questline that eventually grants a permanent climbing ability. This skill allows you to climb walls up to three times consecutively after touching the ground, functioning similarly to the climbing claws but without occupying a valuable accessory slot.

### The Duke of Rocks Questline
To unlock the superior version of the glove's ability, follow these steps:
*   Locate the Duke of Rocks near the base of the Mountain biome.
*   Accept his "Mountain Quest," which requires you to eliminate a specific number of mountain-dwelling enemies.
*   Reach the summit of the mountain to prove your worth.
*   Return to the Duke to receive the permanent climbing technique.

## Advanced Strategies with the Copper Glove

Experienced players use the **copper glove pilgrammed** for more than just simple climbing. By combining it with the "Dash Technique" found at the Elite Barracks, you can perform advanced maneuvers.

### The Wall-Kick Reset
By dashing toward a wall while wearing the copper glove, you can often "reset" your momentum. This is particularly useful when trying to scale the Imperial Knight's island or escaping the tangerines that spawn in the higher altitudes. 

1.  Jump toward the surface.
2.  Latch on using the glove.
3.  Dash away from the wall and immediately turn back to latch on higher up.

> **Warning:** Be careful of your stamina bar. Climbing and dashing both consume stamina, and falling from the top of the Mountain biome often results in an immediate trip back to the nearest campfire.

## Synergizing with Other Skills

The 2026 meta for Pilgrammed revolves around stacking mobility. Once you have mastered the **copper glove pilgrammed**, you should look toward the "Double Jump" skill. This requires Cloud Boots, 20 Royal Rubber, and a Great Redbird Feather. 

The Great Redbird Feather is one of the more difficult items to get. You must use a glider and climbing techniques to reach a far-off island, navigate past aggressive tangerines, and steal the feather from a sleeping Redbird. Having the glove or the Duke's climbing skill is mandatory for this sequence, as you will need to latch onto white orbs and specific cliff faces to maintain your height.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use the Copper Glove Pilgrammed and the Duke of Rocks skill at the same time?**

A: While you can have both, they do not "stack" their effects in a way that gives you infinite climbing. The permanent skill effectively replaces the need for the glove, allowing you to use that accessory slot for something that boosts your damage or defense instead.

**Q: Where is the best place to farm Copper Ore for the glove?**

A: The best place to find Copper Ore early on is in the caves located just south of the main town. Look for orange-veined rocks. You will need a basic pickaxe to harvest them.

**Q: Is the Copper Glove required to finish the game?**

A: Technically, no, but it is practically essential. Without some form of climbing—either through the **copper glove pilgrammed** or the mountain skill—you will find it nearly impossible to reach the late-game areas like the Sky Islands or the depths of the Volcano.

**Q: Does the Copper Glove break or lose durability?**

A: In the current 2026 version of Pilgrammed, accessories like the Copper Glove do not have a durability stat. Once you craft or buy it, you can use it indefinitely until you decide to swap it for a different item.

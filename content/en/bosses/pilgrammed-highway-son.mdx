Before you step onto the highway to challenge the boss, your loadout needs to be optimized for mobility and defensive timing. The **pilgrammed highway son** is not a fight where you can simply tank damage; his "one-hit" potential is high if you fail to respect his air-to-ground transitions.

## Defeating the Pilgrammed Highway Son: Phase-by-Phase Breakdown

The fight is divided into two distinct phases. The transition occurs when the boss's health bar reaches approximately 50%, marked by a dialogue shout: "I'm Highway Son!"

### Phase 1: The Golden Rain

In the initial stage of the **pilgrammed highway son** fight, the boss relies heavily on verticality and area-of-effect attacks. His primary move involves jumping high into the air and raining down golden bullets.

1.  **Bullet Rain:** When the boss shoots upward, move away from his current position. The bullets fall in a spread pattern that is easy to avoid if you keep moving.
2.  **Golden Projectiles:** He will fire linear golden bolts. These can be parried or simply sidestepped.
3.  **Vulnerability Window:** After the boss performs his air attack, he will land and remain stationary for a brief moment. This is your primary window to unleash your highest damage combos.

### Phase 2: Increased Aggression

Once the second phase begins, the **pilgrammed highway son** becomes much more dangerous. He retains his Phase 1 moves but enhances them with higher frequency and a new projectile.

*   **Triple Air Shot:** Unlike Phase 1, where he shoots once from the air, he now shoots three times. You must parry or dodge three consecutive times before he becomes vulnerable on the ground.
*   **The Javelin:** A fast-moving linear projectile that does not auto-track but travels quickly. Using your dash or Storm Boots is the most reliable way to avoid this.

| Attack Name | Phase | How to Counter |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bullet Rain** | 1 & 2 | Move laterally away from the boss's shadow. |
| **Single Air Shot** | 1 | Parry once when he reaches the apex of his jump. |
| **Triple Air Shot** | 2 | **Parry three times** in rapid succession. |
| **Javelin Toss** | 2 | Dash sideways; do not jump over it. |

## Expert Strategies and "Cheese" Methods

If you are struggling with the standard mechanics of the **pilgrammed highway son**, there are several ways to simplify the encounter. The most effective "cheese" involves exploiting the landing lag after his air attacks.

### The Parry-Punish Cycle
By focusing entirely on defensive play, you can bait the boss into his air attacks. In Phase 2, if you successfully parry all three air shots, the boss enters a prolonged "stunned" state upon landing. Using a weapon with high burst damage during this window can end the fight significantly faster than trying to chip away at his health during the neutral game.

### Mobility Stacking
Using the Kai Fish in conjunction with the Storm Boots allows for a "kiting" strategy. By maintaining maximum distance, you force the boss to use his Javelin and Air Shot moves, which are more predictable than his close-range bullet spreads. This strategy is particularly effective for ranged builds.

> **💡 Tip:** If you find yourself getting hit by the bullet rain, try to time your dash *just* as the projectiles begin their descent. This uses your invincibility frames (i-frames) to negate the damage entirely.

## Advanced Combat Tips for 2026

As the meta for the game evolves, players have discovered more efficient ways to handle the **pilgrammed highway son**. 

1.  **Audio Cues:** Listen for the distinct "clink" sound before the Javelin is thrown. This is your signal to dash immediately.
2.  **Parry Windows:** The parry window in Pilgrammed is generous, but the Highway Son's projectiles move at varying speeds. Practice the timing in Phase 1 to prepare for the rapid-fire requirements of Phase 2.
3.  **Environmental Awareness:** The "highway" arena is long but narrow. Avoid getting backed into the invisible walls or edges of the combat zone, as this limits your ability to dodge the bullet rain.

By following these steps and maintaining a calm approach to the parry sequences, the **pilgrammed highway son** will go from a frustrating roadblock to a predictable farm for high-tier loot.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the Pilgrammed Highway Son?**

A: The Highway Son is typically located in the desert/highway region of the map. He is often found patrolling the open road area, making him easy to spot from a distance.

**Q: What is the best weapon to use against the Highway Son?**

A: While many weapons work, those with fast attack speeds or high burst damage are preferred. Weapons that allow you to stay mobile while attacking help you avoid his constant projectile pressure.

**: Do I need the Storm Boots to win the fight?**

A: No, the Storm Boots are not strictly required, but they make dodging the **pilgrammed highway son** much easier. If you are confident in your parry timing, you can complete the fight with standard gear.

**Q: How do I craft the Glider after getting the drops?**

A: Once you have the components from the Highway Son, you need to visit a crafting NPC (usually found in the main town or specialized workshops) to assemble the Glider using the boss drops and additional common materials.

## Capturing High-Resolution In-Game Assets

If you cannot find the specific image you want online, creating your own **pilgrammed wallpaper** is the best alternative. To get professional results, you should follow a specific set of steps to ensure the image is clean and high-resolution.

### Optimizing Your Settings
Before taking a screenshot, you must maximize your graphical fidelity. Even if your computer struggles to run the game at 60 FPS on max settings, you only need it to be stable for the second it takes to capture the image.

1.  **Max Graphics:** Set Roblox graphics to 10.
2.  **UI Toggle:** Use the in-game settings to hide the GUI (Heads-Up Display).
3.  **Field of View (FOV):** Lower your FOV for portrait shots to create a "portrait mode" effect with less distortion.
4.  **Shaders:** If your hardware allows, using external shaders (like RoShade) can add depth, shadows, and reflections that aren't present in the base game.

| Setting | Recommended Value | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Roblox Quality** | 10 | Maximum render distance and textures |
| **In-game FOV** | 40-60 | Better focus on subjects/bosses |
| **Brightness** | 0.8 | Prevents "blown out" highlights in bright areas |
| **Camera Mode** | Classic | Allows for precise positioning |

## Technical Tips for the Best Quality

To ensure your **pilgrammed wallpaper** looks crisp, avoid saving images as JPEGs if possible. PNG formats preserve the sharp edges of the game's pixel-style textures much better. If you are downloading from a social media site, be aware that many platforms compress images, which can lead to "artifacting" (blurry blocks in the image). Always look for a "Download Original" or "HD" link.

If you find an older image that you love but it is too small for your 4K monitor, consider using an AI Upscaler. These tools can increase the resolution of a **pilgrammed wallpaper** while maintaining the sharp lines of the game's art style, making old screenshots look brand new.

## The Rusty Pig: Boss Mechanics and Rewards

The centerpiece of the Junkpits’ mechanical menace is the Rusty Pig, a massive robotic beast found within a chrono-mechanical arena. This boss serves as a skill check for players entering the mid-to-late game, requiring precise parry timing and spatial awareness.

The Rusty Pig utilizes a variety of heavy-hitting physical attacks and high-velocity projectiles. Its movement pattern is erratic, often mimicking the leaping strikes of the Patrius but with added mechanical weight. Understanding its telegraphs is the key to surviving the encounter and claiming its rare drops.

### Rusty Pig Attack Patterns

| Attack Name | Description | Counter Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Double Leap** | A two-stage jump similar to the Patrius leap. | Dash during the first landing; parry the second. |
| **Mechanical Dash** | A high-speed forward charge. | This attack is **parriable**; timing is generous. |
| **Alternating Stomps** | Rapid stomps switching between left and right legs. | Maintain distance or use a shield with high stability. |
| **Barrel Roll Stomp** | A rolling move that transitions into a heavy ground slam. | Roll toward the boss to get behind it before the slam. |
| **Minigun Spray** | The pig fires a barrage of bullets from its integrated gun. | Seek cover or use a high-block-rate weapon. |

Defeating the Rusty Pig allows players to craft or loot the **Rusty Pig Helmet** and the **Minigun**. The Minigun, in particular, has become a staple for heavy weapon builds in 2026, offering sustained DPS at the cost of mobility.

> **💡 Tip:** The Minigun has a wind-up time. Ensure you have a defensive accessory equipped to mitigate damage while preparing to fire.

## Lichenshshire: The Evolving Town

One of the most innovative features introduced in the Junkpits is Lichenshshire. Unlike traditional hubs, Lichenshshire is a dynamic "empty" town that grows in size and utility based on the quests you complete and the guilds you join throughout your journey.

The town serves as a gathering point for various NPCs you rescue or assist. For example, completing the Sweepberry Catacombs questline brings Limo to town, while helping the Elite Knight Guild secures Betsy’s presence. This system rewards completionists with a centralized hub filled with specialized vendors and blacksmiths.

## New Regions: Meer of Iron and The Landfill

The Junkpits is divided into several distinct sub-biomes, each with its own environmental hazards and mutated enemy types.

### Meer of Iron
The Meer of Iron is a shallow marshland bordering the poisonous Mercury Sea. This area is characterized by its "alien" climate and mutated versions of familiar enemies. Here, you will encounter **Magnet Moors**, creatures that resemble the Chalkmen but utilize magnetic forces to manipulate projectiles and slowly regenerate their health during combat.

Another notable enemy is the **Metacarb**. While it shares a silhouette with the Sand Pod, the Metacarb is far more dangerous, utilizing energy beams and spike-throwing attacks to zone out players.

### The Landfill
The Landfill is a vast expanse of scrap and garbage heaps, home to scavengers and massive predators. This area introduces the **Giant Worm**, a boss-tier enemy that uses spinning attacks and black energy beams. You will also need to watch out for the **Motocross Car**, a fast-moving vehicle enemy that fires high-caliber cannons.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Mercury Sea is highly toxic. Do not attempt to explore the shoreline without proper corrosion resistance or specialized gear found in the Meer of Iron.

## Lore: The Overseer and the Heart of the Factory

The narrative of the Junkpits revolves around the mysterious figure known as **Krono** and his creation, **Overseer Duffy**. According to dialogue found within the update, Krono has "absconded his flesh" to become the metaphorical heart of the factory. This suggests that the entire Junkpits infrastructure may actually be a living extension of Krono himself.

Players also find clues regarding the "Electric Clown," a creation inspired by a childhood love of the circus. This lore ties back to the Cathedral and the origins of the Chalkmen and Clerics. In the Junkpits, players may find a massive Cathedral-like structure featuring "Bishop" chess piece designs, suggesting a direct link between the mechanical Junkpits and the religious iconography seen elsewhere in Pilgrammed.

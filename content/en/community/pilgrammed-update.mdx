## New Elemental Wands and Magic Weapons

The 2026 magic meta has been completely transformed by the introduction of several specialized wands. These weapons move away from generic projectiles, offering unique synergies with existing armor sets like the Sunbreaker.

### The Demo Wand and Mithril Wand
The Demo Wand is a standout for players who enjoy high-risk, high-reward gameplay. Found in the Volcano region, it features a rapid-fire primary attack with minimal mana consumption. Its heavy attack, however, causes an explosion that damages both the enemy and the user. While this sounds detrimental, it is designed to proc buffs from the **Sunbreaker armor**, which grants massive stat boosts upon taking damage.

Conversely, the Mithril Wand, located in the Mountain region, offers a more tactical approach. It fires homing icicles that lock onto targets before transitioning into a continuous laser beam. This makes it an excellent choice for mobile bosses where manual aiming is difficult.

| Weapon | Location | Requirements | Special Property |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Demo Wand** | Volcano NPC | 200 Gold, 8 Demo, 1 Fire Stone | Self-damage explosion procs armor buffs |
| **Mithril Wand** | Mountain NPC | Mithril, Gold | Homing icicles with laser follow-up |
| **Vine Wand** | Swamp (Friendly Witch) | Crafting Materials | Spawns tracking vines; applies Venom |

### The Magma Shotgun
For those who prefer a more "explosive" ranged approach, the Magma Shotgun is a top-tier addition. Obtained by trading a Blunderbuss and specific materials to the Volcano vendor, this weapon deals heavy fire damage and has a high chance to ignite enemies.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Magma Shotgun requires 10 Magma Scales, which are dropped by the new Magma Turtle mini-boss. Ensure you are geared for fire resistance before attempting this farm.

## The Infinite Damage Vine Wand Build

One of the most talked-about discoveries in the latest **pilgrammed update** is the "Infinite Damage" potential of the Vine Wand. By exploiting the interaction between Pink Slime and Venom stacks, players can reach damage numbers exceeding 50,000 poison damage per second.

### How the Build Works
The core of this strategy involves the Vine Wand's ability to stack Venom exponentially. When an enemy is coated in **Pink Slime**, the poison ticks are significantly amplified. 

1. **Apply Pink Slime:** Use a secondary item or have a teammate apply Pink Slime to the boss.
2. **Stack Vines:** Use the Vine Wand's primary attack to place vines. The more vines that hit, the faster the poison ramps up.
3. **Mana Harmonizer:** Equip the Mana Harmonizer to maintain the mana required for constant vine spawning.
4. **Timing:** For solo players, timing the Pink Slime application just as the vine stacks reach their peak is crucial for the "infinite" ramp effect.

> **💡 Tip:** This build is particularly effective against stationary bosses like Mando, but may struggle against highly mobile targets or in specific "buggy" zones where vine tracking fails.

## Daggers and Secret Upgrades

The **pilgrammed update** introduces a new dagger progression path starting in the Swamp. By visiting the new shopkeeper, Fang, players can access the Elf Blade and the Pitch Dagger.

### From Pitch Dagger to Blacksteel
The Pitch Dagger is a solid mid-game choice that rewards aerial combat. Landing an aerial attack allows your next M1 (basic attack) to perform a long-distance dash. However, the real prize is the **Blacksteel**. 

To obtain Blacksteel, you must take your Pitch Dagger to the Volcano's Chasm and locate the Blackbird NPC. Trading the Pitch Dagger here provides the Blacksteel upgrade—a direct stat improvement that retains the powerful dash mechanic while significantly increasing base damage.

| Dagger | Acquisition | Special Mechanic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Elf Blade** | Purchased from Fang | 3-hit heavy attack combo |
| **Pitch Dagger** | Purchased from Fang | Aerial attack leads into a dash M1 |
| **Blacksteel** | Trade Pitch Dagger at Blackbird | Enhanced stats; retains dash mechanic |

## The Catacombs: Searching for "The Joke"

One of the more complex weapons added in 2026 is found deep within the Catacombs. Titled "The Joke" by the community, this weapon is obtained by searching tombs in the Jailer area. It is a multi-mode spellbook that cycles through three distinct effects with its heavy attack.

1. **Morph Spell:** Applies a "Light" status effect, changing the enemy's elemental affinity.
2. **Self-Burn:** Applies Curse Fire to the player (useful for specific "on-fire" build synergies).
3. **Shadow Summons:** Spawns shadow-clones of Catacomb enemies. This is the weapon's most powerful mode, as there is no limit to the number of summons other than your total mana pool.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the new shopkeeper Fang in the pilgrammed update?**

A: Fang is located in the Swamp region. From the Friendly Witch’s hut, head further into the marsh to find their shop. They sell the Elf Blade, Pitch Dagger, and several utility items.

**Q: Is the Vine Wand build still viable for solo players?**

A: Yes, though it is more difficult. Solo players must manage their mana carefully and use the Mana Harmonizer to ensure they can stack enough vines to trigger the exponential poison ramp.

**Q: How do I get to the Blackbird NPC for the Blacksteel upgrade?**

A: Go to the Volcano and enter the Chasm. You will need to navigate the high ledges. Using Noxious Water or mobility skills can help you reach the Blackbird’s platform more easily.

**Q: What is the best weapon for the new Magma Turtle boss?**

A: The Mithril Wand is highly recommended due to its homing properties, allowing you to focus on dodging the turtle’s heavy fire attacks while maintaining consistent damage.

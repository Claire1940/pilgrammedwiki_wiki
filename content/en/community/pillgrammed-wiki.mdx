## The Evolution of Lichenshshire: A Dynamic Hub

One of the most innovative features arriving in the Junkpits update is Lichenshshire. Unlike previous static towns, Lichenshshire is an empty settlement that grows in size and population based on the quests and guilds you have completed throughout your journey. This mechanic encourages players to revisit older content to ensure their hub is fully optimized with the best vendors and NPCs.

The town serves as a central point for the "Fruit Folk" who have moved underground. Lore enthusiasts on the **pillgrammed wiki** suggest this migration was caused by rising tensions on the surface, though the presence of some Fruit Folk above ground implies a deeper schism within their society.

## Boss Spotlight: The Rusty Pig

The first major gatekeeper players will encounter in the Junkpits is the Rusty Pig. This massive, chrono-mechanical beast resides in a specialized arena and utilizes a mix of high-mobility leaps and heavy ballistic fire. Defeating this boss is essential for players looking to craft the latest high-tier mechanical gear.

### Rusty Pig Attack Patterns

The Rusty Pig’s AI is designed to punish players who stay at a distance for too long. Understanding its telegraphs is the key to victory:

1.  **The Double Leap:** Similar to the Patrius boss, the pig will jump twice in rapid succession. You must time your rolls to avoid the shockwave upon impact.
2.  **Parryable Dash:** The pig charges forward with a metallic screech. This attack can be parried, providing a massive window for counter-attacks.
3.  **Alternating Stomps:** The boss will shift its weight between its left and right legs, creating localized tremors.
4.  **Barrel Roll into Minigun:** This is the boss's signature move. It will roll toward the player and immediately transition into a sustained minigun volley.

## New Biomes: The Meer of Iron and The Landfill

The Junkpits is not just a single cave; it is divided into distinct ecological zones, each with its own hazards. 

### The Meer of Iron
This area is described as a peaceful yet alien countryside. It consists of shallow marshlands sitting adjacent to the **Mercury Sea**. The water here is highly poisonous, and players should avoid prolonged contact. The enemies here, such as the **Magnet Moors**, are mutated versions of familiar surface creatures. Magnet Moors are particularly dangerous because they can rub their hands together to generate magnetic projectiles and possess a slow passive health regeneration.

### The Landfill
A vast expanse of garbage heaps and scavengers. This is where you will find the **Metacarb**, a creature that shares many animations with the Sand Pod but features a metallic shell and the ability to fire beams and spikes from its antennae. The Landfill is also home to the "Motocross Car," a sentient vehicle that utilizes a mounted cannon to zone out intruders.

## Arsenal Expansion: New Weapons and Mechanics

The Junkpits update introduces a new elemental status effect: **Corrosion**. Many of the new weapons found in the **pillgrammed wiki** database feature this effect, which deals damage over time and potentially weakens enemy defenses.

### Top New Weapons to Watch For

*   **Axe Whip:** A mid-range hybrid weapon that applies the corrosion effect. It is ideal for players who prefer a "hit-and-run" playstyle.
*   **Sword-Gun Hybrid:** Similar to the Jade Twin, this weapon allows players to switch modes between melee strikes and ranged shots, offering incredible versatility in boss fights.
*   **Dual Tires:** A unique pair of weapons that can be combined during a heavy attack combo to deal massive blunt damage.
*   **Laser Burst Rifle:** A high-precision ranged weapon found in the deeper factory sectors of the Junkpits.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Mercury Sea surrounding the Meer of Iron will apply a lethal poison stack. Do not attempt to swim in these waters without specialized protective gear or high health regeneration builds.

## Deep Lore: Krono and the Overseer

The narrative of the Junkpits centers around a mysterious figure named **Krono**. According to the NPC Overseer Duffy, Krono was a pioneer of automation who eventually "absconded his flesh" to become the metaphorical heart of the factory. While players may not fight Krono directly in the initial update, his influence is seen in every robotic enemy.

A secret letter found in the game files mentions an "Electric Clown," an automation based on a childhood love of the circus. This suggests that the Junkpits may contain a hidden "Circus" sub-zone or a secret boss encounter related to this design. Late-game leaks suggest that the Junkpits are connected to a massive, submerged Cathedral. This area features enemies designed after chess pieces, such as the **Bishop**, which is a more advanced version of the Clerics found in earlier regions. The presence of magnets and atmospheric changes within these deep caves suggests that the Junkpits might be more than just a landfill—it could be a failed attempt at creating a new world underground.

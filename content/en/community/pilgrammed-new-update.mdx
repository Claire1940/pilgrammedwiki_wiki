## Elemental Mastery: New Wands and Ranged Options

The latest expansion brings a significant buff to magic and hybrid users. Two primary wands have been added to the elemental pool, alongside a powerful new firearm that utilizes magma-based materials. These weapons are scattered across the more extreme biomes, specifically the Volcano and the Snowy Mountains.

### The Demo Wand and Mythro Wand
The **Demo Wand** is a standout for players who enjoy high-risk, high-reward gameplay. It features a rapid-fire rate with minimal mana consumption, making it an excellent primary for sustained DPS. However, its heavy attack triggers a localized explosion that damages the user. While this sounds like a drawback, it synergizes perfectly with the **Sunbreaker Armor**, which grants buffs upon taking damage.

On the other hand, the **Mythro Wand** offers a more tactical approach. Found in the mountain regions, it fires icicles that lock onto targets and transition into laser beams. It provides a reliable source of frost damage for those who prefer kiting enemies from a distance.

| Weapon | Location | Requirements | Special Property |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Demo Wand** | Volcano NPC | 200 Gold, 8 Demo, Fire Stone | Heavy attack causes self-damaging explosion |
| **Mythro Wand** | Mountain Region | Mythro Gold / Materials | Lock-on icicles that fire lasers |
| **Magma Shotgun** | Volcano NPC | 500 Gold, Blunderbuss, Ruby, 10 Magma Scales | High fire damage; lights enemies ablaze |

> **💡 Tip:** Use the Demo Wand's heavy attack intentionally to proc "on-hit" buffs from your armor sets. This is currently one of the most effective ways to maintain 100% uptime on damage steroids.

## Boss Drops: Treasures of the Sinister Sea

The Sinister Sea has become significantly more dangerous with the addition of two new boss-tier weapons. These items are tied to the **Big Fish** and **Patra** encounters, offering unique mechanics for both Mage and Melee builds.

### Big Fish Gauntlet
Contrary to its appearance, the **Big Fish Gauntlet** is a dedicated Mage weapon. Its primary fire launches electric orbs that bounce between enemies, making it a premier choice for crowd control in dense mob areas. The heavy attack is a charged punch that releases several bouncing projectiles, covering a wide area of effect.

### Bone Claws
The **Bone Claws** are a high-tier fist weapon dropped by Patra. These claws feature a 100% drop rate (alternating with the Kudger) and are designed for aggressive, close-quarters combat. Their defining feature is the **Bloodthirst** passive: when active, the weapon gains massive boosts to damage and reach, allowing you to shred through boss health bars with ease.

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Big Fish Gauntlet** | Big Fish | Mage | Bouncing electric projectiles |
| **Bone Claws** | Patra | Fist/Melee | Bloodthirst passive for range/damage |

## Swamp Secrets and Dagger Evolutions

The Swamp biome has received a vendor update through the NPC **Fang**, who sells two of the most interesting daggers in the **pilgrammed new update**. Additionally, the Friendly Witch now offers a new nature-based staff.

### The Blacksteel Upgrade Path
The **Pitch Dagger** is the baseline for a new mobility-focused build. It allows players to perform a dash-follow-up after an aerial attack. However, the real prize is the **Blacksteel**. By taking your Pitch Dagger to the **Blackbird** NPC located in the Chasm of the Volcano, you can trade it for this direct upgrade. The Blacksteel retains the dash mechanics but features significantly higher base stats and a sleek dark aesthetic.

### Vine Wand and Elf Blade
The **Vine Wand** introduces a "Grass" element mechanic. It cannot be fired into thin air; it requires a target. Once an enemy is in range, it summons tracking vines from the ground that inflict Venom. Its heavy attack acts as a crowd-control tool, rooting enemies in place. The **Elf Blade**, meanwhile, is a solid early-to-mid-game choice that allows for a unique three-hit heavy combo, providing more versatility than standard daggers.

| Weapon | Acquisition | Style | Unique Feature |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pitch Dagger** | Fang (Swamp) | Mobility | Aerial attack enables dash M1 |
| **Blacksteel** | Blackbird Trade | Elite | Upgraded Pitch Dagger stats |
| **Vine Wand** | Friendly Witch | Support/Mage | Roots enemies and applies Venom |
| **Elf Blade** | Fang (Swamp) | Combo | 3-stage heavy attack combo |

## The Catacombs: Spell-Cycling and Summoning

Deep within the Jailer area of the Catacombs lies one of the most complex weapons added in 2026. Often referred to by the community as the "Joke" weapon (due to its flavor text), this item is found by searching tombs in the side rooms of the jailer's domain.

This weapon does not have a single fixed attack. Instead, its heavy attack cycles through three distinct spells:
1.  **Light Status:** Changes the enemy's elemental affinity to Light, making them vulnerable to specific counter-elements.
2.  **Curse Fire:** Sets the user on fire. While seemingly negative, this can be used to trigger specific "on-burn" trinkets or armor buffs.
3.  **Shadow Summoning:** The most powerful mode. It allows the player to summon shadow versions of Catacomb enemies. There is no hard limit on the number of summons other than your total Mana pool.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Curse Fire mode can quickly drain your health if you aren't wearing fire-resistant gear or using a build that benefits from status ailments.

## Specialized Melee: Spears and Shackles

Not every weapon in the update is a flashy magical staff. Several new melee options provide niche utility for specific builds.

### Scrap Lance
The **Scrap Lance** is obtained from the Scrap Lands using Lance Fragments (dropped by Viper Heads). It features a 50% drop rate for materials, though player testing suggests it may be rarer. This weapon shines when paired with the **Serpent Style** technique found in the Desert, as it gains a significant damage multiplier when applying Venom. Its heavy attack is a defensive kick that launches the player backward, creating space.

### Magma Shackles
Obtained as a challenge medal reward from the **Magma Turtle**, the Magma Shackles are a heavy, slow-swinging weapon. While they lack the DPS of the Bone Claws, they serve as a powerful "heavy hitter" for early-game players who manage to clear the challenge. They function similarly to the Rusty Bell but with improved scaling for the mid-game.

## FAQ

**Q: Where is the best place to farm for the new weapons in the Pilgrammed new update?**

A: Most of the high-tier weapons are found in the Volcano (Demo Wand, Magma Shotgun) and the Sinister Sea (Bone Claws, Big Fish Gauntlet). If you are looking for the spell-cycling weapon, head to the Catacombs and search the tombs near the Jailer.

**Q: How do I upgrade the Pitch Dagger to Blacksteel?**

A: You must first purchase the Pitch Dagger from Fang in the Swamp. Once you have it, travel to the Volcano and descend into the Chasm. Locate the Blackbird NPC and trade the Pitch Dagger to receive the Blacksteel.

**Q: Is the Vine Wand good for solo bossing?**

A: The Vine Wand is excellent for crowd control and applying damage-over-time (Venom), but its requirement for a target to even fire makes it tricky for highly mobile bosses. It is best used as a secondary weapon to root a boss before switching to a high-DPS option like the Bone Claws.

**Q: Are the new Mythro bows worth crafting?**

A: According to the latest community findings, the Mythro bows added in this update are largely reskins of the standard bows. They do not currently possess unique properties or special heavy attacks, so your resources are better spent on the Mythro Wand or other elemental gear.

Navigating the treacherous world of Pilgrammed requires more than just a sharp blade and high stats; it requires unparalleled mobility. With the release of the Prejunks update in 2026, players have been scrambling to find the **pilgrammed great redfeather**, a rare material essential for unlocking the highly coveted Air Jump skill. This item isn't just a simple drop from a common mob; it represents a significant milestone in a player's progression, demanding a mix of specific gear, precise movement, and environmental awareness.

Securing the **pilgrammed great redfeather** involves traveling to the furthest reaches of the map, navigating aerial platforming challenges, and outmaneuvering aggressive enemies known as Tangerines. Whether you are a veteran looking to optimize your movement tech or a newcomer trying to keep up with the latest meta, understanding how to obtain and use this feather is paramount. In this guide, we will break down the entire process, from purchasing your first pair of Cloud Boots to stealing the feather from a sleeping giant bird.

## The Importance of the Great Redfeather in 2026

The **pilgrammed great redfeather** is the primary reagent for the Air Jump technique. In the current 2026 meta, mobility is king. Being able to reposition mid-air allows players to dodge boss mechanics that were previously unavoidable and reach hidden areas tucked away in the high cliffs of the mountain biomes.

Before you set out on your journey, you must ensure you have the necessary materials and prerequisites. The NPC who grants the skill—the Slingshot Vendor—won't even consider your request unless you bring a specific list of items.

### Required Materials for the Air Jump Skill

| Item | Quantity | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cloud Boots** | 1 | Purchased from the Shop Vendor |
| **Royal Rubber** | 20 | Dropped by Royal/Imperial enemies |
| **Great Redfeather** | 1 | Found on the far Sky Island |
| **Gold** | Variable | Required for initial gear purchases |

> **💡 Tip:** Do not attempt to reach the Sky Island without Cloud Boots. The jump height and gliding duration provided by this gear are mandatory for the white orb sequence.

## How to Find the Pilgrammed Great Redfeather

Finding the **pilgrammed great redfeather** requires a journey to a remote island that isn't easily accessible via standard walking paths. Most players utilize a combination of teleporters and specialized movement items like the Noxious Mortar to bridge the gap.

### Step 1: Reaching the Imperial Island
Your first goal is to reach the island inhabited by Imperial Knights. This area serves as the gateway to the smaller, more vertical islands where the Redbird resides. If you have the Noxious Mortar, you can launch yourself across the gaps. If not, follow the path past the Imperial Knight encampment toward the lower cliffs.

### Step 2: The Hidden Teleporter
Look for a teleporter hidden beneath the main ridge of the Imperial Island. Taking this teleporter will transport you to a small, isolated landmass. This is the starting point for the "White Orb" challenge.

### Step 3: Navigating the White Orbs
Once on the small island, you will see glowing white orbs floating in the air. These are temporary flight boosters. To use them effectively:
1. Equip your **Glider**.
2. Jump into a white orb to gain an immediate upward and forward boost.
3. Use your **Climbing Claws** (unlocked from the Duke of Rocks) to reset your position on vertical walls between orb jumps.
4. Chain the orbs together quickly, as the boost duration is limited.

## The Sky Island Challenge

As you reach the final set of white orbs, the difficulty spikes. This is where the Tangerines begin to spawn. These orange, bird-like enemies will attempt to knock you out of the sky.

### Dealing with Tangerines
While gliding, your defensive options are limited. You cannot parry while in the glider animation, meaning you must rely entirely on manual dodging. If a Tangerine hits you, the hit-stun will likely cause you to fall into the void, forcing a restart from the last teleporter.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If you get "jumped" by multiple Tangerines, prioritize reaching the next white orb immediately. The speed boost from the orb is often enough to outpace their initial dive-bomb attacks.

### Stealing the Feather
At the very top of the island chain, you will find a massive, sleeping Redbird. You do not need to kill this creature. Approach it quietly and interact with it to "steal" the **pilgrammed great redfeather**. Once the item is in your inventory, you can safely teleport back to the main hub or use a waypoint to return to the Slingshot NPC.

## Unlocking the Air Jump Skill

With the **pilgrammed great redfeather**, 20 Royal Rubber, and your Cloud Boots in hand, return to the NPC with the slingshot. Upon turning in these items, you will be granted the permanent Air Jump skill.

### Skill Mechanics and Benefits
The Air Jump is often compared to the Scurry Boots buff but functions as a permanent character upgrade that doesn't require specific footwear once learned.

| Feature | Effect |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Double Jump** | Allows for a second jump while mid-air to gain height. |
| **Extended Dive** | Pressing the jump key during a dive extends the horizontal distance. |
| **Animation Cancel** | Can be used to cancel certain recovery frames after a heavy attack. |
| **Stacking** | Works in conjunction with Climbing Claws for massive verticality. |

By mastering the use of the **pilgrammed great redfeather** skill, you can effectively triple-jump if you time your wall-kicks and air-jumps correctly. This is essential for 2026 end-game raids where platforming is integrated into boss encounters.

## Other Essential Skills in the Prejunks Update

While the **pilgrammed great redfeather** is the star of the show, the Prejunks update introduced several other techniques that compliment your new mobility. To be a truly elite Pilgrim, you should aim to collect all three major skills.

### 1. The Wall Climb (Duke of Rocks)
Talk to the Duke of Rocks at the mountain base. He will task you with killing mountain-dwelling enemies and reaching the peak. Completing this grants you the ability to climb walls three times consecutively before needing to touch the ground. This skill makes the journey to the Great Redfeather significantly easier.

### 2. The Dash Technique (Gareth)
Located at the Elite Barracks, Gareth (or Garth) provides the Dash Technique. While the exact requirements are debated—likely tied to Elite Knight Guild reputation or defeating the Skeleton boss—this skill replaces your standard roll with an empowered dash. This dash covers more ground and offers more I-frames, making it a staple for PvP and high-level PvE.

## Final Strategy for Success

To successfully claim your **pilgrammed great redfeather**, follow this optimized route:
1. **Farm Gold**: Ensure you have enough for Cloud Boots and a decent Glider.
2. **Collect Rubber**: Farm the Imperial Knights on the way to the teleporter to get your 20 Royal Rubber.
3. **Equip Climbing Claws**: Get the Duke of Rocks skill first; it is almost impossible to navigate the Sky Island walls without it.
4. **The Run**: Use the teleporter, ignore the Tangerines as much as possible, and focus entirely on the white orb chain.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I get the pilgrammed great redfeather without Cloud Boots?**

A: It is theoretically possible with high-level movement items like the Noxious Mortar, but it is not recommended. The Cloud Boots provide the necessary hang-time to reach the white orbs consistently.

**Q: Does the Great Redbird wake up when I take the feather?**

A: No, the bird remains sleeping during the interaction. However, the Tangerines surrounding the nest will remain aggressive, so you should leave the area quickly after obtaining the **pilgrammed great redfeather**.

**Q: Can I lose the Air Jump skill if I reset my character?**

A: No, once you turn in the **pilgrammed great redfeather** and the other materials to the NPC, the skill is permanently bound to your character's progression.

**Q: Where is the best place to farm the 20 Royal Rubber?**

A: The Imperial Knights near the teleporter entrance are the most efficient source. They have a high drop rate for Royal Rubber and are conveniently located on the path to the Sky Island.

## How to Obtain Pilgrammed Lance Fragments

The primary source of the **pilgrammed lance fragment** is the Viper Head boss, a formidable opponent located within the Desert region. Unlike some world drops that can be found in chests or through generic mob farming, these fragments are tied specifically to this boss encounter, making it a focal point for players reaching the mid-game transition.

### The Viper Head Boss Fight
Viper Head is known for its aggressive lunges and venomous attacks. In the 2026 update cycle, the drop rate for the lance fragment is officially cited at 50%. However, many players in the community report that RNG can be fickle, sometimes requiring multiple consecutive kills to secure the necessary amount for crafting.

| Drop Item | Drop Chance | Utility |
|---|---|---|
| **Lance Fragment** | 50% | Used to purchase the Scrap Lance |
| **Viper Scale** | 30% | Crafting material for Desert armor |
| **Gold** | 100% | General currency for upgrades |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not underestimate Viper Head's reach. Its lunge attack can pierce through standard block frames if your timing is off. It is recommended to use high-mobility boots or a dash-heavy technique to stay out of its immediate strike zone.

## Where to Spend Your Fragments: Finding Belly

Once you have gathered enough **pilgrammed lance fragment** pieces, you must locate the NPC named Belly. Belly is situated in the "Scrap Lands," a sub-area that requires some platforming to reach. Many players overlook this merchant because he is tucked away in a lower section of the desert canyon, often referred to as the belly of the dunes.

Belly trades specifically in scavenged goods. To obtain the Scrap Lance, you will need a combination of fragments and standard gold. This weapon is considered a "bridge" weapon—perfect for players who have outgrown the Iron Spear but aren't yet ready for the endgame Mythril variants.

## Scrap Lance Stats and Abilities

The Scrap Lance is not just a simple stat-stick; it features a unique moveset designed for tactical retreats and status infliction. It excels in "poke and run" strategies, making it a favorite for solo players tackling high-health enemies.

### Moveset Breakdown
*   **M1 (Light Attack):** A standard piercing thrust with decent range. It has a high chance to inflict a **Bleed** status effect, which drains enemy health over time.
*   **Heavy Attack:** Instead of a traditional charged thrust, the Scrap Lance heavy attack is a defensive kick. This move launches the player backward, creating immediate distance between you and the target. This is invaluable for avoiding "get-off-me" attacks from bosses.
*   **Passive Ability:** The weapon features a unique synergy with the **Serpent Style** technique. When Serpent Style is active, the lance does significantly more damage to enemies currently afflicted by **Venom**.

| Stat | Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Damage Type** | Piercing | Strong against unarmored foes |
| **Status Effect** | Bleed | 15% proc chance on M1 |
| **Range** | High | Outranges most daggers and swords |
| **Scaling** | Dexterity | Primary stat for damage increase |

## Optimizing the Scrap Lance Build

To get the most out of your **pilgrammed lance fragment** investment, you should pair the resulting weapon with gear that complements its piercing and venom capabilities.

1.  **Technique - Serpent Style:** Found in the Desert, this is mandatory for maximizing the Scrap Lance's damage. It turns your standard pokes into devastating strikes against poisoned enemies.
2.  **Armor - Thief or Desert Set:** Look for armor that increases your Dexterity or provides a "Venom" proc on hit. This ensures that the lance's passive is always active.
3.  **Accessories:** Items that increase "Status Potency" will make the Bleed effect from the lance much more threatening in long-duration fights.

> **💡 Tip:** Use a "Noxious Vial" or similar consumable to apply Venom to a boss immediately. Once the boss is poisoned, your Scrap Lance will benefit from the Serpent Style damage boost from the very first hit.

## Farming Strategy for Lance Fragments

If you find yourself struggling to get the **pilgrammed lance fragment** to drop, consider optimizing your farming route. Viper Head respawns relatively quickly, but you can speed up the process by using "Fast Travel" totems if they are placed near the Desert entrance.

*   **Server Hopping:** If you are in a crowded server where the boss is constantly being killed, switching to a lower-population server can give you uninterrupted access to the spawn point.
*   **Luck Buffs:** While the base drop rate is 50%, using any luck-enhancing items or gems can help mitigate bad streaks of RNG.
*   **Group Play:** Fighting Viper Head with a friend doesn't reduce the individual drop chance, making it safer and faster to clear the encounter repeatedly.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I trade Pilgrammed lance fragments with other players?**

A: Currently, in the 2026 version of the game, most boss-specific crafting materials like the lance fragment are soulbound or non-tradable to prevent skipping progression. You must defeat Viper Head yourself to earn them.

**Q: Is the Scrap Lance better than the Prism Bow?**

A: These weapons serve different roles. The Scrap Lance is a melee-focused piercing weapon with high defensive utility (the kick-back heavy), while the Prism Bow is a high-DPS ranged option. For players who prefer staying in the thick of the fight, the lance is the superior choice.

**Q: Where exactly is the Serpent Style technique?**

A: Serpent Style can be found in a hidden alcove within the Desert, not far from where you fight the Viper Head. Look for a series of small platforms leading into a cave decorated with snake motifs.

**Q: Does the Scrap Lance work well for late-game bosses?**

A: While it is an excellent mid-game weapon, you may find its damage falling off against late-game bosses with high piercing resistance. It is best used as a tool to farm the materials needed for the next tier of weaponry.

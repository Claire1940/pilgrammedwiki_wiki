Among the most critical components of any endgame build are **pilgrammed gems**, powerful modifiers that can be slotted into weapons to grant elemental damage, stat boosts, or unique utility effects. Whether you are looking to increase your swing speed with a Light Gem or sustain your health through a Blood Gem, understanding how to acquire and optimize these items is essential for progression.

## Best Ways to Grind Pilgrammed Gems

Acquiring gems is not always a straightforward process, as many are locked behind rare world events or difficult combat encounters. To maximize your efficiency, you should vary your activities based on your current power level and available equipment.

### 1. Meteor Strikes and NPCs
One of the most exciting ways to find gems is by keeping an eye on the sky. When a meteor strikes, the entire game world will shake, making it nearly impossible to miss. These meteors spawn unique NPCs that have a high probability of dropping valuable loot.

| NPC Type | Guaranteed Drop | Notable Loot |
|---|---|---|
| **Meteor Bandit** | Yes | Random Gem |
| **Meteor Raider** | Yes | Random Gem |
| **Meteor Pebble** | Yes | Mondo Ring |

> **💡 Tip:** Always carry a Mondo Ring if you find one. This item allows you to fight "Mondo" versions of bosses, which significantly increases their gem drop rates.

### 2. Boss Farming (Mondo Bosses)
While almost every boss in the game has a slim chance to drop a gem, fighting the "Mondo" variants is the most reliable combat-based method. Bosses like Mr. Normal are known to have exceptionally high drop rates for gems when compared to other early-to-mid-game encounters. If you are powerful enough to take on the Mondo Old Fart or Mondo Viper Head, you can expect a much higher yield of high-tier enhancements.

### 3. The Big Iron Trial
For players who prefer a guaranteed path over RNG, the Big Iron Trial is a fantastic alternative. By completing this trial multiple times, you earn Iron Coins. These coins can be taken to the Iron Guy NPC, who sells specific gems directly. This is often the best route for players looking for a specific build-enabling gem rather than gambling on random drops.

### 4. Sky Grounds Battle Arena
If you have a high-DPS build and good survivability, the Sky Grounds Battle Arena offers a consistent challenge. Reaching high waves (typically 100+ points or Wave 47 and beyond) has been reported to guarantee at least one Magic Gem or better.

### 5. Macro Fishing
For those who prefer a more passive approach, fishing remains a viable, albeit slower, method. Many players utilize macros to fish overnight, accumulating thousands of Gold and a decent collection of gems in the process. While not the most engaging method, it is undeniably effective for building up resources while away from the keyboard.

## How to Use and Equip Gems

Finding a gem is only half the battle; you must know where to go to actually apply it to your weaponry. The central hub for all gem-related activities is the **Observatory**.

### Finding the Observatory
The Observatory is located across the ocean, requiring a bit of a trek. Once you arrive at the base of the cliffs, you will need to climb to the summit. Look for a "dead dude" NPC (the Gem Smith) located around the corner at the top.

### Gem Smith Services
The Gem Smith offers three primary services that are vital for managing your **pilgrammed gems**:

1.  **Forge Gem:** This allows you to slot a gem into a compatible weapon.
2.  **Exchange Gems:** You can trade three gems you don't want for one random gem. This is a great way to recycle "trash" gems like Magic Gems into potentially S-tier options.
3.  **Remove Gem:** If you want to move a gem to a new weapon, the Smith can remove it for a fee, usually around 1,000 Gold.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Always interact with the Mirror at the Observatory to save your location. The trek across the ocean is long, and you don't want to have to make the trip twice if you accidentally fall or die.

### S-Tier Analysis
*   **Light Gem:** Speed is king in Pilgrammed. By reducing startup frames and increasing swing speed, the Light Gem makes heavy weapons more viable and fast weapons absolutely oppressive. 
*   **Blood Gem:** Survivability is the biggest bottleneck in endgame content. The Blood Gem provides essential life steal, allowing you to sustain through boss mechanics without relying solely on potions or specialized healing gear.

### A-Tier Analysis
*   **Haste Gem:** This gem is transformative for weapons with long recovery times. By removing end-lag on the final hit of a combo, it allows for much safer playstyles against aggressive bosses.
*   **Fire Gem:** While niche, the synergies available for fire damage in 2026 make this a powerhouse for specific elemental builds.

## Advanced Gem Strategies

To truly optimize your character, you should consider how your gem interacts with your weapon's innate properties. 

### Multi-Hit Synergy
If you are using a weapon like the Anchor or any multi-hit dagger, the **Explosion Gem** becomes significantly more valuable. Since each hit has a chance to proc the explosion, you can trigger multiple blasts in a single attack string, dealing massive area-of-effect damage that scales better than flat damage gems in mob-heavy scenarios.

### The Ice-Fire Tech
While the **Frost Gem** is generally ranked lower, it has a specific interaction with the Ice Crown. When paired correctly, you can proc "Ice Fire" instead of a standard chill effect. This is an advanced tactic used by veteran players to bypass certain elemental resistances, though it requires a very specific equipment loadout to be effective.

### Farming Efficiency
If you are strictly farming for gold and gems simultaneously, the best method remains the **Mondo Mr. Normal** loop. He is relatively easy to kite and has a drop table that is very generous with gems. ## FAQ

**Q: Can I equip more than one gem on a single weapon?**

A: No, standard weapons in Pilgrammed only have one gem slot. You must choose the gem that best complements your specific build. If you want to change it, you must pay the Gem Smith at the Observatory to remove the old one first.

**Q: Are pilgrammed gems lost if I die?**

A: No, gems are permanent items in your inventory or attached to your weapons. You do not lose them upon death, though you may lose other resources like gold depending on your current world settings.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get a Blood Gem?**

A: The fastest way is to farm Mondo bosses using a Mondo Ring or to use the Exchange service at the Gem Smith. Trading in three unwanted gems (like Magic or Frost) gives you a chance to roll a Blood Gem without having to rely on specific boss drop tables.

**Q: Does the Light Gem affect magic casting speed?**

A: The Light Gem primarily affects weapon swing speed and startup. For magic-focused cooldowns, you are better off looking for gems or accessories specifically labeled for "Magic Cooldown" or "Mana Reduction," though the Haste gem can help with certain animation end-lags.

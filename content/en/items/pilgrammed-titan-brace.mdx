## Understanding Piercing Mechanics

To appreciate why the **pilgrammed titan brace** is so effective, you must first understand how the game calculates damage. Most players assume piercing simply ignores a portion of the enemy's defense stat. While this is partially true, the reality is far more complex. In the world of Pilgrammed, enemies possess two distinct types of damage mitigation: **Defense** and **Damage Taken**.

### Defense vs. Damage Taken

1.  **Defense:** This is a flat value subtracted from your total damage. If you deal 100 damage and the enemy has 10 defense, you deal 90 damage.
2.  **Damage Taken:** This is a percentage multiplier. If an enemy has a "50% Damage Taken" stat, they only take half of the damage they would normally receive after defense is calculated.

The game applies defense first, then applies the damage taken multiplier. The formula looks like this:
`Final Damage = (Base Damage - Adjusted Defense) * Adjusted Damage Taken`

### The Role of the Titan Brace

The **pilgrammed titan brace** is unique because its 15% piercing effect is applied to the "Damage Taken" stat specifically. This is applied *after* your standard piercing has already modified the enemy's stats. This "multi-layered" piercing approach ensures that even the tankiest enemies in the game remain vulnerable to your attacks.

## The Mathematical Formula for Piercing

For the players who want to calculate their exact damage output, the 2026 formulas have been decoded. Standard piercing (P) affects both Defense (D) and Damage Taken (T). 

| Mechanic | Formula |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Adjusted Defense** | $D \times (1 - P)$ |
| **Adjusted Damage Taken** | $T + (1 - T) \times 0.375 \times P$ |
| **Titan Brace Bonus** | Applied to the final "Damage Taken" result |

When you equip the **pilgrammed titan brace**, the game takes the "Adjusted Damage Taken" value and increases it by a factor of 15% of the remaining reduction. This ensures that the accessory remains useful even if you already have high innate piercing from your weapon or other gear like the Shark Tooth Necklace.

Not every weapon benefits equally from the Titan Brace. Weapons that already possess innate piercing or high base damage tend to see the most significant scaling. In the 2026 landscape, certain combinations have emerged as the "gold standard" for physical DPS builds.

### 1. The Grave Tree
The Grave Tree is a powerhouse weapon that scales exceptionally well with Strength. Because the Titan Brace provides both Strength and damage reduction piercing, it covers both of the Grave Tree's needs. In testing against high-defense targets like the Observatory metal dummies, the Titan Brace can increase the Grave Tree's effective DPS by nearly 12-15% depending on the enemy's base stats.

### 2. The Rot
The Rot features a 25% innate piercing stat. When combined with the Titan Brace, the math becomes very favorable. The innate piercing reduces the enemy's flat defense and boosts the damage taken percentage, and then the Titan Brace's passive kicks in to push that damage taken percentage even higher.

### 3. Shark Tooth Necklace Interaction
The Shark Tooth Necklace is a common pairing with the Titan Brace. It provides a flat +4 defense reduction and modifies piercing during critical hits. 

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when stacking too much piercing. If an enemy's defense is reduced into negative values (common when using the Shark Tooth Necklace on low-armor targets), adding more piercing can actually *decrease* your damage. This is because piercing always moves the defense value closer to zero. If you are at -5 defense, piercing will move it toward 0, effectively lowering your damage bonus.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the pilgrammed titan brace work with magic damage?**

A: The Titan Brace is primarily designed for physical builds. While the damage reduction piercing may apply to some hybrid attacks, the +6 Strength bonus is wasted on pure magic builds. Mages are better off using accessories that provide Intelligence or Mana Regeneration.

**Q: Is the Titan Brace better than the Shark Tooth Necklace?**

A: It depends on the enemy. Against enemies with very high flat defense, the Shark Tooth Necklace's -4 defense reduction is superior. However, against "tanky" enemies with high damage reduction percentages (like bosses), the **pilgrammed titan brace** provides a more significant DPS increase. Many players choose to wear both for a balanced build.

**Q: Can I stack multiple Titan Braces?**

A: No, you cannot equip two of the same accessory in Pilgrammed. You must pair the Titan Brace with other unique items to maximize your stats.

**Q: Why is my damage lower when I equip more piercing items?**

A: This usually happens when the enemy has negative defense. In Pilgrammed, negative defense acts as a damage bonus. Since piercing always forces defense toward zero, it will actually "weaken" a negative defense value, resulting in lower total damage dealt to the target.

## Understanding the Pilgrammed Explosive Gem Mechanics

The explosive gem operates on a proc-on-hit system. Unlike the Light Gem, which provides a flat stat increase to swing speed, or the Blood Gem, which focuses on sustainability, the explosive gem introduces a secondary damage instance. Every time you strike an enemy, there is a calculated chance to trigger an explosion at the point of impact.

This mechanic makes the gem exponentially more valuable on weapons that hit multiple times in a single animation or have very high attack speeds. While a slow, single-hit heavy sword might not see frequent procs, a weapon like the Anchor or rapid-fire ranged tools can trigger a cascade of explosions, effectively doubling or tripling your DPS during high-intensity encounters.

## Best Weapons for Explosive Gem Synergy

To get the most out of your **pilgrammed explosive gem**, you need to pair it with hardware that maximizes its proc rate. In the current 2026 landscape, certain weapons stand out as the premier choices for this specific upgrade.

### 1. The Anchor
The Anchor is widely considered one of the best candidates for an explosive build. Its heavy impact and multi-hit hitbox allow for consistent explosion triggers. When combined with the gem, the Anchor's already impressive AoE capabilities are bolstered, making it a "room clearer" in dense mob areas.

### 2. Multi-Hit Rapid Weapons
Any weapon that features a "flurry" attack or multiple hits per swing will benefit significantly. Because each individual hit check has a chance to trigger the gem, you are essentially rolling the dice more often per second than with a standard blade.

### 3. Ranged Projectile Weapons
While often used with the Blood Gem for safely sniping health, the explosive gem can turn ranged weapons into mortar-like tools. This is particularly effective against large-bodied bosses where the explosion radius is guaranteed to hit multiple hitboxes of the same enemy.

## How to Farm Gems in Pilgrammed

Acquiring a **pilgrammed explosive gem** requires patience and a solid understanding of the game's drop tables. Gems are rare drops, but there are several reliable methods to "grind" them out effectively.

### Meteor Events
One of the most visually spectacular ways to get gems is by hunting meteors. When a meteor strikes, the entire game world will shake—a signal that is nearly impossible to miss. These meteors spawn specific NPCs, such as:
*   Meteor Bandit
*   Meteor Raider
*   Meteor Pebble

These NPCs have a guaranteed gem drop rate, making them the most sought-after targets for players looking to upgrade their gear quickly.

### Boss Farming (Mondo Bosses)
Every boss in Pilgrammed has a latent chance to drop a gem upon defeat. However, this chance is significantly amplified when fighting "Mondo" versions of these bosses. To fight a Mondo boss, you typically need a Mondo Ring, which can be obtained from meteor NPCs.
*   **Mr. Normal:** This boss is noted for having a very high gem drop frequency. Farming Mr. Normal is a staple strategy for players who want to stock up on various gem types.
*   **Mondo Viper:** While more difficult, the Mondo variant of the Viper has been known to drop gems at a higher rate than its standard counterpart.

### The Big Iron Trial
For those who prefer a more structured challenge, the Big Iron Trial is a consistent source of gems. If you can complete the trial twice in a row, you will earn roughly 30 Iron Coins. These coins can be taken to the Iron Guy NPC, who sells gems directly to players. This removes the RNG element associated with boss drops, providing a guaranteed path to the **pilgrammed explosive gem**.

## Advanced Gem Grinding Strategies

If you find boss hunting too tedious, there are alternative methods to fill your inventory with gems in 2026.

### Sky Grounds Battle Arena
The Sky Grounds offer a survival-based challenge that rewards high-level play. Reaching a score of 100+ points (usually around wave 40 to 50) is widely believed to guarantee a gem drop. While the difficulty spikes significantly in the later waves, it remains one of the most engaging ways to farm high-tier loot.

### Fishing: The Passive Alternative
For players who prefer a more relaxed pace—or those who utilize macros—fishing is a viable, albeit slower, method for obtaining gems. By fishing in high-level zones, you can occasionally pull up gems along with gold and other rare treasures. It is not the fastest method, but it is the only one that doesn't involve life-threatening combat.

> **💡 Tip:** If you are struggling with the Big Iron Trial, ensure you have a build with high mobility to dodge the pressure plate traps and NPC ambushes.

## Combat Tactics: Making Every Proc Count

When using a weapon slotted with a **pilgrammed explosive gem**, your positioning becomes more important than ever. Because the explosions deal AoE damage, you want to "clump" enemies together. 

1.  **Kiting:** Run in circles to draw multiple mobs into a tight group.
2.  **The Corner Trap:** Back yourself into a corner (if your health allows) to force enemies into a single point of impact.
3.  **Aerial Attacks:** Some weapons have different hitboxes in the air. Experiment to see which animation triggers the most gem procs.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the Pilgrammed explosive gem better than the Fire Gem?**

A: In most scenarios, yes. While the Fire Gem provides consistent damage over time, the explosive gem offers higher burst potential and better AoE coverage, especially on weapons that hit multiple times quickly.

**Q: Can I remove a gem once it's been slotted into a weapon?**

A: Gem slotting is generally permanent in Pilgrammed. You should think carefully before applying your **pilgrammed explosive gem** to a weapon, as you cannot retrieve it without destroying the weapon or using specific rare items (depending on the current patch).

**Q: Does the explosive gem damage the player?**

A: No, the explosions triggered by the gem are purely offensive and do not cause self-damage, making them safe to use even in close-quarters combat.

**Q: Where is the Iron Guy NPC located for the Big Iron Trial?**

A: The Iron Guy is typically found near the end of the Big Iron Trial area. You must have enough Iron Coins from successful runs to interact with his shop and purchase specific gems.

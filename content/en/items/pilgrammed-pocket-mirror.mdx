## Prerequisites: Preparing for the Climb

Before you can even think about purchasing a pocket mirror, you must satisfy several gameplay requirements. The game progression in Pilgrammed is strictly gated by equipment, meaning you cannot reach the necessary NPCs without specific movement tools.

### The Slingshot Requirement
The Slingshot is your primary tool for vertical mobility early in the game. To unlock it, you must prove your mettle in the Sky Battlegrounds. You can find this area on a peripheral island away from the main hub.

| Requirement | Method | Estimated Time |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sky Battlegrounds** | Reach Wave 30 | 20–25 Minutes |
| **NPC Quest** | Speak to the Sky Guard | 5 Minutes |
| **Gold Cost** | Varies by vendor | N/A |

> **💡 Tip:** If you find Wave 30 difficult, focus on area-of-effect (AoE) weapons to clear the smaller mobs quickly, saving your stamina for the tougher elite spawns.

### The Glider Requirement
You cannot reach the NPC Mirror without a Glider. The Glider is found in a hidden chest within the Cloud City outskirts, but accessing it requires navigating a teleporter guarded by a mini-boss.

## Step-by-Step Guide to the Glider

To get the Glider, start at the Middle Island. Look for a landmark consisting of a very large, tall pole. This pole is your anchor point for the Slingshot.

1.  **Navigate to the Outstretch:** Head to the edge of the island near the pole.
2.  **Use the Slingshot:** Hold the "G" key (or your designated tool key) to launch yourself across the gap toward the floating platforms.
3.  **Defeat the Highway Son:** This boss guards the teleporter. If you attempt to use the teleporter while he is alive, it will reject you.
4.  **Enter the Teleporter:** Once the area is clear, step through the portal to enter a sub-area filled with Eyeballs.
5.  **Loot the Chest:** Avoid or parry the Eyeball enemies and locate the wooden chest at the end of the path. This contains your Glider.

## Locating the NPC Mirror

Once you have the Glider and at least **500 Gold**, you are ready to find the merchant who sells the **pilgrammed pocket mirror**. The NPC, aptly named Mirror, is located in a secluded section of the Cloud Wilds that is only accessible by gliding.

### The Path to Mirror
Reset your character or travel back to Cloud City. From the main platform, you will head back toward the Cloud Wilds using the same Slingshot method you used for the Glider. However, instead of entering the teleporter where you fought the Highway Son, you must jump off the cliffside and activate your Glider.

Aim your flight toward the isolated floating island positioned slightly below the main teleporter level. On this small patch of land, you will find Mirror standing near the edge.

### Mirror's Shop Inventory 2026
Mirror doesn't just sell the pocket mirror; she carries several high-utility items that are worth the investment if you have extra gold.

| Item | Price | Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pocket Mirror** | **500 Gold** | Essential for teleportation and navigation. |
| **Sound Staff** | 400 Gold | Magic-based weapon with unique sound-wave attacks. |
| **Nimbus Pendant** | 600 Gold | Increases mobility and jump height. |
| **Green Balloon** | 300 Gold | Slows fall speed, useful for precision landing. |

## Combat Strategy: Dealing with Guardians

The journey to the pocket mirror is fraught with enemies that can easily end your run if you aren't prepared. The two main threats are the Highway Son and the Eyeballs.

### The Highway Son
This boss is often considered a "parry check" for mid-game players. He utilizes fast melee strikes and occasional ranged lunges. 
*   **Strategy:** Do not spam your attacks. Wait for the visual flash of his weapon and time your parry. Successfully parrying him opens a window for a full combo.
*   **Warning:** If you try to ignore him and run for the teleporter, his tethered aggro will cause the teleporter to malfunction.

### Eyeballs
These enemies are found in the room containing the Glider chest. They are aggressive and will swarm you if you stay still for too long.
*   **Strategy:** They have low health but high burst damage. Use a fast-swinging weapon to knock them back. Like the Highway Son, their attacks can be parried to negate all incoming damage.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Falling off the islands in Cloud Wilds will result in an immediate death and loss of some progress. Always ensure your Slingshot is fully aimed before releasing.

## Why the Pocket Mirror is Essential

You might wonder why players go through the trouble of navigating boss fights and high-altitude platforming just for a mirror. The **pilgrammed pocket mirror** is one of the most significant quality-of-life upgrades in the game.

1.  **Fast Travel:** It allows you to set return points, significantly cutting down on the time spent backtracking through lower-level zones.
2.  **Safety Net:** If you find yourself trapped in a high-level area with low health, the pocket mirror can be your "get out of jail free" card.
3.  **Quest Efficiency:** Many late-game quests require you to bounce between Cloud City and the mainland. The mirror makes this transition seamless.

## Advanced Movement Tips
Once you have both the Glider and the pocket mirror, the way you interact with the world of Pilgrammed changes. You can use the momentum from a Slingshot launch to transition directly into a Glide, allowing you to cross massive gaps that were previously impossible to traverse. 

*   **Slingshot-Glide Combo:** Launch yourself with the Slingshot, and at the peak of your arc, deploy the Glider. This preserves your forward velocity.
*   **Stamina Management:** Keep an eye on your stamina bar while gliding. If it runs out, you will fall. Using the Green Balloon (also sold by Mirror) can help you descend safely if your stamina fails.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I get the pilgrammed pocket mirror without fighting the Highway Son?**

A: No. The teleporter required to reach the Glider—which is a prerequisite for reaching the NPC Mirror—will reject any player who is currently in combat or has not defeated the guardian of that platform.

**Q: What happens if I die after buying the pocket mirror?**

A: The pocket mirror is a permanent tool. Once purchased for 500 Gold, it stays in your inventory even if you die or reset. You do not need to buy it again.

**Q: Is the Nimbus Pendant better than the pocket mirror?**

A: They serve different purposes. The Nimbus Pendant is an accessory that improves your active movement (jumping/speed), while the pocket mirror is a utility item for teleportation. Most players recommend buying the pocket mirror first due to its immense utility.

**Q: Does the Slingshot require ammo?**

A: No, the Slingshot in Pilgrammed functions as a mobility tool rather than a traditional ranged weapon, so you do not need to worry about carrying projectiles.

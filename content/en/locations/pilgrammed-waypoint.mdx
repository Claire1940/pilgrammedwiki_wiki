## Understanding the Mirror System

In Pilgrammed, "Waypoints" are officially referred to as Mirrors. These are large, interactable objects scattered throughout the major biomes. Unlike some games where fast travel is unlocked automatically upon entering a zone, you must manually find and "attune" to each Mirror to add it to your travel network.

Once a Mirror is unlocked, you can access the travel menu from any other Mirror you have previously discovered. This creates a web of interconnected nodes that make high-level farming and questing significantly more manageable.

| Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| **Activation Method** | Walk up to the Mirror and click/interact with it. |
| **Travel Cost** | Generally free, though some specific world events may restrict usage. |
| **Visual Indicator** | Large stone frames with a shimmering, reflective surface. |
| **Death Mechanics** | Most players respawn at the last Mirror or "Home" point they interacted with. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always prioritize finding the Mirror in a new zone before you start farming mobs. If you die without activating the local pilgrammed waypoint, you may have to run through several dangerous biomes to return.

## Early Game Waypoint Locations

For new players, the first few hours are spent in the Prairie and the surrounding outskirts. However, the difficulty spikes quickly once you move toward the Desert or the Volcano. Unlocking these waypoints early allows you to access better shops and reforging NPCs.

### The Prairie (Starting Area)
The first Mirror is located near the central hub of the Prairie. This is your primary anchor point. From here, most players head toward the Thief King’s forest or the nearby mines to gather initial resources like Bronze.

### The Desert Waypoint
Reaching the Desert is a major milestone. To find this **pilgrammed waypoint**, you must head North-East from the Prairie. The path is guarded by tougher mobs, so it is recommended to have at least a Bronze Greatsword before making the trek. Once you enter the sandy dunes, look for a rocky outcropping near the entrance; the Mirror is situated on a slight elevation, providing a safe spot away from the sand-burrowing enemies.

### The Volcano Waypoint
The Volcano is one of the most hazardous zones in the early-to-mid game. The environment itself deals damage if you aren't careful, and the mobs are significantly more aggressive. The Mirror here is tucked away near the base of the mountain, often used by players who are farming the local boss for high-tier weapon drops.

| Region | Landmark | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| **Prairie** | Central Village | Very Low |
| **Desert** | Entrance Outcropping | Medium |
| **Volcano** | Mountain Base | High |

## Combat Preparation for Waypoint Hunting

You cannot simply run to every **pilgrammed waypoint** without a solid combat foundation. Many Mirrors are guarded by "gatekeeper" mobs or are located deep within hostile territory. Before you set out to unlock the Volcano or Desert mirrors, follow these gear recommendations.

### 1. Crafting a Reliable Weapon
Starting with a basic sword is fine for the first ten minutes, but you should quickly transition to a **Bronze Greatsword**. 
*   **Gather Materials:** Mine rocks in the local caves to obtain Bronze Ore.
*   **Reforge:** Visit the blacksmith to reforge your weapon. In 2026, the "Sharp" or "Legendary" prefixes are highly sought after for their massive damage boosts.

### 2. Defeating the Thief King
The Thief King is the first "real" boss most players encounter. Defeating him is almost mandatory for waypoint hunters because he drops the **Thief King Cloak**. This accessory provides essential stat boosts that help you survive the journey to more distant waypoints.

### 3. Armor and Agility
While defense is important, agility allows you to dodge attacks while running toward a new Mirror. The **Wonder Armor** is a fantastic early-game choice because it balances physical protection with movement speed, making it easier to outrun mobs that are too high-level for you to fight.

| Item | Primary Benefit | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Bronze Greatsword** | High Base Damage | Crafting (Bronze Ore) |
| **Thief King Cloak** | Strength & Utility | Thief King Boss Drop |
| **Wonder Armor** | Defense & Agility | Armor Shop |
| **Gold Ring** | Misc. Stat Boosts | Thief King / Chests |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Dying in Pilgrammed results in losing half of your current gold. Before heading out to find a new pilgrammed waypoint, always visit the Banker to deposit your coins.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Reaching the Volcano Mirror

The trek to the Volcano is the most common point where players get stuck. Follow these steps to ensure you reach the **pilgrammed waypoint** safely:

1.  **Set Spawn at the Desert:** Before attempting the Volcano, ensure you have already unlocked and interacted with the Desert Mirror. This cuts your travel time in half if you fail.
2.  **Equip Movement Skills:** Use skills like "Roll" (default F or Shift depending on layout) to avoid projectiles from the lava-themed mobs.
3.  **Ignore Small Mobs:** Do not stop to fight every enemy. Your goal is the Mirror. Keep moving in a straight line toward the mountain base.
4.  **Locate the Mirror:** The Mirror is located near the entrance path of the Volcano. It is a large, glowing frame. Once you click it, you are safe to explore or engage the local boss.

## Advanced Navigation and Reforging

As you progress into 2026, you will find that simply reaching a waypoint isn't enough; you need to be able to survive the area the waypoint leads to. Reforging your accessories is just as important as reforging your weapons.

### Accessory Reforges
When you obtain items like the Thief King Cloak or various rings, take them to a reforge NPC.
*   **Jagged:** Best for pure damage builds.
*   **Tough:** Recommended for players who find themselves dying frequently while traveling between waypoints.
*   **Wonderful:** A balanced reforge that provides a mix of useful utility stats.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I use a pilgrammed waypoint to teleport?**

A: You must first walk up to a Mirror and interact with it to "unlock" it. Once unlocked, interacting with any other Mirror will open a map menu, allowing you to select any previously discovered location for instant travel.

**Q: Can I set a custom home point at a waypoint?**

A: Yes, interacting with a Mirror usually sets it as your current spawn point. However, some specific items or NPCs in the main hub can allow you to set a permanent "Home" regardless of the last Mirror you touched.

**Q: Are there any waypoints in the underground areas?**

A: Yes, several cave systems and sub-zones have their own Mirrors. These are often hidden behind breakable walls or guarded by mini-bosses, so keep a close eye on your surroundings while exploring dark areas.

**Q: Why can't I see the Desert waypoint on my map?**

A: If a **pilgrammed waypoint** isn't appearing, it means you haven't physically clicked on the Mirror in that region yet. Simply entering the zone is not enough; you must trigger the interaction to add it to your fast-travel network.

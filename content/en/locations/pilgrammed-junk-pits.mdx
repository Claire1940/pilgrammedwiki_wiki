## Elemental Mastery: New Wands and Ranged Options

The 2026 update introduces two primary elemental wands that cater to different mana-management styles. The **Demo Wand** and the **Mithril Wand** represent the pinnacle of mid-game magical utility.

### The Demo Wand
Located in the Volcano region, the Demo Wand is a must-have for players who enjoy high-intensity, "glass cannon" builds. By trading 200 Gold, 8 Demo materials, and a Fire Stone, you gain access to a weapon with an incredibly high fire rate. Its heavy attack causes a self-damaging explosion, which may seem detrimental at first. However, when paired with the **Sunbreaker Armor**, this self-damage procs significant stat buffs, turning a disadvantage into a powerful combat loop.

### The Mithril Wand
Found in the snowy peaks of the Mountain, the Mithril Wand offers a more tactical approach. It fires icicles that lock onto targets and emit lasers upon impact. While its standard damage is moderate, its tracking capabilities make it excellent for mobile enemies that are difficult to hit with standard projectiles.

### Magma Shotgun and Shackles
The Magma Shotgun is a powerhouse of the **pilgrammed junk pits** arsenal. Crafted using a Blunderbuss, 500 Gold, a Ruby, and 10 Magma Scales (dropped by the Magma Turtle), it provides immense burst damage and a reliable burn effect. Conversely, the **Magma Shackles**, obtained from the Magma Turtle challenge medal, serve as a heavy, slow-hitting alternative for players who prefer the "Rusty Bell" playstyle of the early game.

## Sinister Sea Boss Drops

The Sinister Sea remains one of the most dangerous zones, but the rewards for defeating its guardians have been significantly buffed in 2026.

### Big Fish Gauntlet
Despite its appearance, the Big Fish Gauntlet is a **Mage weapon**. It fires electric orbs that bounce between enemies, making it the premier choice for crowd control. Its heavy attack is a charged punch that releases additional bouncing projectiles, effectively clearing rooms of smaller mobs in seconds.

### Bone Claws
Dropped by Pathus, the Bone Claws are a high-tier fist weapon. Their unique passive, **Bloodthirst**, grants increased damage and range as the fight progresses. For players who enjoy aggressive, close-quarters combat, the Bone Claws offer some of the highest DPS potential in the current version of the game.

| **Bone Claws** | Fist | Strength/Dex | Bloodthirst Stat Scaling |
| **Scrap Lance** | Spear | Dexterity | Venom Synergy (Serpent Style) |

## Swamp Secrets and Dagger Evolutions

The Swamp has received a significant update with the introduction of Fang’s shop and new crafting recipes at the Friendly Witch. The **Vine Wand** is particularly noteworthy; it is a "Grass" element weapon that summons tracking vines from the ground. Unlike other staves, it requires an enemy to be nearby to activate, but its ability to inflict Venom and root enemies in place makes it a top-tier support tool.

### The Dagger Progression
Fang offers two new daggers: the **Elf Blade** and the **Pitch Dagger**.
*   **Elf Blade:** An early-to-mid game weapon that allows for a three-part heavy attack combo, providing more fluidity than standard daggers.
*   **Pitch Dagger:** A highly mobile weapon. Landing an aerial attack allows your next light attack (M1) to become a long-range dash.

### Upgrading to Black Steel
To reach the true potential of the Pitch Dagger, players must travel to the Volcano Chasm. By locating the **Blackbird NPC** hidden along the cliffs, you can trade your Pitch Dagger for the **Black Steel**. The Black Steel retains the dash mechanics but features significantly higher base stats and a sleek, darkened aesthetic.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Reaching the Blackbird requires precise platforming. Using **Noxious Water** or mobility-focused armor is highly recommended to avoid falling into the chasm.

## The Catacombs: Secret Summoning

Deep within the Jailer area of the Catacombs lies a unique weapon often referred to by the community as "The Joke" or the **Catacombs Grimoire**. To obtain it, players must search the various tombs located to the right of the main entrance. While the drop is RNG-based, it is not excessively rare.

This weapon cycles through three distinct spells:
1.  **Morph Spell:** Applies a "Light" status effect to enemies, changing their elemental affinity.
2.  **Cursed Fire:** Ignites the user in exchange for a temporary power boost.
3.  **Shadow Summon:** Spawns shadow entities based on the Catacombs' enemies. This skill has no limit other than your current mana pool, allowing for a "Summoner" build that can overwhelm bosses through sheer numbers.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the new shopkeeper Fang?**

A: Fang is located in the Swamp region. From the Friendly Witch's hut, head deeper into the swamp toward the clearing with the large stone ruins. He sells the Elf Blade and the Pitch Dagger.

**Q: How do I get Lance Fragments for the Scrap Lance?**

A: Lance Fragments are a 50% drop from the **Viper Head** boss found in The Belly. While the drop rate is officially 50%, community testing in the **pilgrammed junk pits** update suggests it may take several kills to acquire the full set needed for the lance.

**Q: Is the Black Steel better than the Bone Claws?**

A: It depends on your build. The Black Steel offers superior mobility and dash-ins, making it great for PvP and fast-moving bosses. The Bone Claws provide higher sustained DPS in long fights due to the Bloodthirst passive.

**Q: Can the Shadow Summons from the Catacombs weapon be healed?**

A: No, the shadow entities cannot be healed by standard player spells, but since there is no limit to how many you can summon (other than mana), it is usually more efficient to simply summon new ones as they fall in battle.

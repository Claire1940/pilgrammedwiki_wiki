## Locating and Entering the Strange Chasm

Reaching the **pilgrammed strange chasm** is a journey that begins in the Volcano region. Players must navigate to the higher elevations of the volcanic peaks before scouting for a specific drop-off point. It is highly recommended to bring a glider or items that mitigate fall damage, as the descent into the chasm is steep and can easily deplete your health before the fight even begins.

| Step | Action | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Travel to the **Volcano** | High heat resistance or speed |
| **2** | Ascend the peaks | Climbing gear or **Glider** |
| **3** | Locate the drop point | Visual confirmation of the pit |
| **4** | Descend safely | Use a **Glider** to reduce damage |

> **💡 Tip:** If you are playing in a group, ensure everyone drops at the same time. The boss can target individuals during the fall, leading to a chaotic start to the encounter.

## The Stone Boss: Mechanics and Phases

Once inside the **pilgrammed strange chasm**, you will face the primary antagonist of the area, often referred to by the community as the "Stone Guy" or "The Boulder." This boss is a high-health tank that punishes players who stay too close for too long without a parry strategy.

### Boss Attack Patterns
The fight is divided into two distinct phases. In the first phase, the boss focuses on heavy slams and slow-moving projectiles. Once its health drops below a certain threshold, it enters a second phase where it introduces a fast-moving kick and a ground-lift attack that can catch unobservant players off guard.

| Attack Name | Phase | Description | Counter Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Stomp** | 1 & 2 | A heavy foot slam dealing AoE damage. | Jump or dash away from the impact. |
| **Punch** | 1 & 2 | A direct melee strike with high knockback. | **Parry** this for a massive opening. |
| **Ground Lift** | 2 | Lifts the earth to create a barrier/strike. | Move to the side immediately. |
| **The Roll** | 2 | A high-speed rolling attack (Insta-kill). | Move perpendicular to the roll path. |
| **Kick** | 2 | A fast follow-up after a punch. | Requires a double-parry timing. |

## Recommended Gear and Builds

Surviving the **pilgrammed strange chasm** is significantly easier with a build focused on sustain and heavy impact. Since the boss is made of stone, certain "organic" or light-slashing weapons may feel less effective compared to blunt force or explosive damage.

### Top Tier Weapon Choices
*   **Megaton Maul:** While this is a drop from the boss itself, once obtained, it is the premier choice for farming the area due to its high poise damage.
*   **Haunted Scythe:** Excellent for players who need life-steal to stay in the fight without relying heavily on potions.
*   **Accurate Longbow:** If you prefer a ranged approach, the Accurate Longbow (40 damage) outperforms the Champions Bow (36 damage) for consistent DPS from the edges of the arena.

### Essential Accessories
Using the right accessories can negate the boss's high damage output. The **Megaton Pendant** is a standout choice, as it grants super armor while swinging heavy weapons, preventing the boss's smaller hits from cancelling your animations. Additionally, the **Blood Chalice** is a vital consumable for maintaining health during the lengthy Phase 2 transition.

> **Warning:** Avoid using the Mondo Ring during this encounter unless you are looking for an extreme challenge. It significantly increases boss difficulty and can make the roll attack nearly impossible to dodge.

## Strategy for Solo vs. Group Play

When entering the **pilgrammed strange chasm** solo, your focus must be entirely on parrying. The boss's health pool is manageable for a single player, but a missed parry on a punch or kick can lead to a combo that ends the run.

In group play, the boss's health scales upward. It is effective to designate one player as the "Support" or "Cook," utilizing items that buff the party's defense, while the others focus on "Suppressing Fire" with bows or heavy melee strikes. Be careful with targeting; the boss will frequently switch aggro, and if the player currently being targeted isn't prepared to dodge the roll, the entire group could suffer.

## Exiting the Chasm

A common mistake for new players is getting stuck in the pit after the boss is defeated. To exit the **pilgrammed strange chasm**, look for a large **orange button** located near the edge of the arena walls. Pressing this will teleport you back to a safe zone, typically near Cloud City or the Prairie, depending on your recent mirror interactions.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I access the pilgrammed strange chasm if I keep dying to fall damage?**

A: The most effective way is to use a Glider. You can also use the "Sandy Flute" or specific mounts to help navigate the terrain, but a Glider provides the most control during the steep drop from the Volcano peaks.

**Q: Is the Megaton Maul a guaranteed drop?**

A: No, the Megaton Maul is a legendary drop and has a lower percentage chance to appear. Most players report needing 5–10 successful kills to see a drop, though your luck may vary.

**Q: Can I use Magic builds in the Strange Chasm?**

A: Yes, but be aware that the boss has high resistance to certain elemental types. Using a Magic Gem to convert your damage to Explosive or Fire is generally more effective than pure Frost or Organic magic.

**Q: What is the best way to dodge the Boulder's roll attack?**

A: The roll moves in a straight line but can track slightly. The best method is to wait until the boss begins the roll animation, then dash or run horizontally (left or right) relative to the boss's path. Do not try to outrun it in a straight line.

## Preparing for the King's Den

Before stepping foot into the **pilgrammed kings den**, you must evaluate your current level and equipment. While high-level players may find the encounter trivial, those between levels 1 and 10 must be meticulous in their approach. The environment inside the Den is often crowded with additional mobs that can complicate the boss fight, making crowd control and spatial awareness your best allies.

The most critical factor in your preparation is weapon choice. While the standard sword is functional, many players find it lacks the necessary utility to keep the Thief King at bay.

### Recommended Early Game Weapons

For players tackling this challenge at a low level, the **Bronze Greatsword** is the gold standard. Its superior range and knockback capabilities allow you to strike the boss while remaining outside his immediate threat zone.

| Weapon | Range | Knockback | Best Use Case |

> **💡 Tip:** If you are level 10 or higher, your weapon choice becomes more flexible. However, for anyone under level 5, the Bronze Greatsword is almost mandatory for a safe solo run.

## Navigating the Pilgrammed Kings Den

The Den itself is more than just a boss arena; it is a gauntlet. As you approach the Thief King, you will likely draw the attention of various minions. In a solo run, these "adds" can quickly overwhelm you if you allow them to surround you. 

When you enter the **pilgrammed kings den**, try to clear as many smaller enemies as possible before engaging the King. If you find yourself being chased by a large group, do not panic. Use the Greatsword’s wide swing arc to keep them at a distance while focusing your main damage on the boss.

### The Thief King's Moveset

The Thief King relies on speed and aggressive lunges. Understanding his animation cues is the difference between a successful dodge and a quick trip back to the spawn point.

1.  **Standard Slash:** A basic horizontal strike. Easily avoided by kiting.
2.  **Lunge Attack:** A forward-moving thrust with decent range.
3.  **Shoulder Dash (Phase 2):** Once the King drops below **50% health**, he gains a triple shoulder dash. He will zip across the arena three times in rapid succession.

## Combat Strategy: The Kiting Method

The most effective way to clear the **pilgrammed kings den** solo is through a technique known as "kiting." This involves staying in constant motion, moving in a circular pattern around the boss (strafing) rather than standing still or rushing him head-on.

### Step-by-Step Solo Tactics

*   **Maintain Distance:** Use the reach of the Bronze Greatsword to land heavy attacks from a safe distance.
*   **Strafing:** Always move sideways relative to the boss. This causes many of his forward-lunging attacks to miss entirely.
*   **Utilize iFrames:** Your roll is your most powerful defensive tool. The "invincibility frames" (iFrames) during the roll animation allow you to pass through damage unharmed. Time your rolls to coincide with the King’s swing.
*   **Heavy Attack Spam:** Land a heavy hit, then immediately roll away. Do not get greedy; one or two hits are all you should attempt before repositioning.

| Phase | Health Threshold | Primary Danger | Strategy Shift |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Phase 1** | 100% - 51% | Basic Slashes | Focus on kiting and clearing adds |
| **Phase 2** | 50% - 0% | **Triple Shoulder Dash** | Save stamina for consecutive rolls |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Rushing the Thief King and attempting to "spam" attacks will result in an instant death. He has higher poise than you and will win any trade of blows.

## Advanced Tips for Mastery

If you find yourself still struggling with the **pilgrammed kings den**, consider these advanced tips used by the community:

1.  **Audio Cues:** The Thief King often makes a specific sound before his shoulder dash. Playing with game audio on can give you a split-second advantage.
2.  **Stamina Management:** Never deplete your stamina bar entirely. Always leave enough for at least one emergency roll.
3.  **Environmental Obstacles:** Use the pillars and terrain within the Den to snag the King’s pathfinding, giving you a moment to heal or reset your position.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I solo the Pilgrammed Kings Den at Level 1?**

A: Yes, it is possible but very difficult. You must use a weapon with high knockback, like the Bronze Greatsword, and master the kiting technique. It will likely take several attempts to learn the Thief King's timing.

**Q: What should I do if there are too many mobs in the Den?**

A: Try to lure the smaller enemies away from the boss before the fight starts. Use wide-swinging weapons to clear them out. If they spawn during the fight, prioritize kiting them in a circle so they clump together, making them easier to manage.

**Q: Why does the Thief King keep killing me instantly when he reaches half health?**

A: At 50% health, the King enters his second phase and begins using a triple shoulder dash. You must be prepared to roll three times in a row or stay far enough away that the dashes don't reach you.

**Q: Is the Bronze Greatsword better than the Iron Sword for this fight?**

A: For beginners and solo players, yes. The extra range allows you to hit the boss without getting into his "kill zone," providing a much larger margin for error.

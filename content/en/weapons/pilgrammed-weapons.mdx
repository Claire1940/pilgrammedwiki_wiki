The massive Prejunks update has completely shifted the combat meta, introducing over a dozen new **pilgrammed weapons** that cater to every imaginable playstyle. From the depths of the Sinister Sea to the scorching peaks of the Volcano, players now have access to a versatile arsenal ranging from rapid-fire wands to experimental summoning tools. ## Elemental Wands and Magic Staves

The magical landscape of the game has been significantly expanded with the introduction of elemental wands. These weapons often serve as the backbone for Mage builds, offering consistent DPS with low mana costs.

### The Demetal and Mithril Wands
Found in the Volcano and Mountain regions respectively, these wands represent two sides of the elemental spectrum. The **Demetal Wand** is a high-speed projectile weapon that excels in rapid-fire scenarios. Its heavy attack causes an explosion that deals self-damage, which might seem like a drawback until paired with the **Sunbreaker Armor**. This set provides massive buffs when the user takes damage, allowing you to manually trigger your own power spikes.

In contrast, the **Mithril Wand** is often considered an early-game option. While its base stats are lower, it shoots icicles that lock onto targets and fire secondary lasers. It serves as a reliable introductory tool for players transitioning into specialized magic builds.

### The Vine Wand
The **Vine Wand** is a unique "Grass" element weapon available from the Friendly Witch in the Swamp. Unlike traditional staves, it requires an enemy to be nearby to function. It summons tracking vines from the earth that apply Venom.

| Weapon | Location | Cost/Requirements | Primary Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Demetal Wand** | Volcano (Alglorn) | 200 Gold, 8 Demetal, 1 Fire Stone | Rapid fire, Self-explosive heavy |
| **Mithril Wand** | Mountain | Mithril, Gold | Homing icicles with lasers |
| **Vine Wand** | Swamp (Witch) | 200 Gold, Magic Branch, 2 Arch Energy | Tracking vines, Venom status |

> **💡 Tip:** Use the Vine Wand's heavy attack to root enemies in place. This is exceptionally effective in PvP for setup combos or in boss fights to prevent high-mobility targets from escaping your high-damage spells.

## Close-Quarters Combat: Gauntlets and Fists

For players who prefer to get up close and personal, the Prejunks update has delivered some of the most mechanically interesting fist weapons to date.

### Bone Claws
Dropped by the boss **Patrice** in the Sinister Sea, the **Bone Claws** are a top-tier choice for Hemorrhage builds. They possess a passive ability that triggers during "Bloodlust," granting the user increased range and significantly higher damage output. Because they proc bleed effects rapidly, they synergize perfectly with accessories that boost damage against debuffed enemies.

### Big Fish Gauntlet
Despite its name, the **Big Fish Gauntlet** is actually a Mage weapon. It fires electric balls that bounce between enemies, making it a premier choice for clearing groups of mobs. Its heavy attack is a lunging punch that releases even more bouncing projectiles.

### Magma Shackles
If you are looking for "Super Armor" and raw power, the **Magma Shackles** are obtained by defeating the Magma Turtle while wearing a **Challenge Medal**. This heavy weapon is slow but grants the user super armor during its animations. Its heavy attack slams the ground, erupting in flames that scale directly with your Strength stat.

## Specialized Daggers and Lances

Agility-based players have not been forgotten. The new daggers and lances emphasize mobility and combo potential, rewarding players who can time their strikes and aerial maneuvers.

### The Black Stiletto Upgrade
The journey for the ultimate dagger begins with the **Pitch Dagger** (sold by Fang in the Swamp). This weapon enhances your next M1 attack into a dash if you land an aerial hit first. However, the true power lies in the **Black Stiletto**. By taking the Pitch Dagger to the **Strange Chasm** in the Volcano and offering it to the **Blackbird**, you receive a direct upgrade that maintains the dash mechanics with significantly higher base stats.

### Scrap Lance
The **Scrap Lance** is a technical weapon sold by Billy in the Observatory. While its base performance is modest, it becomes a top-tier threat when paired with the **Serpent Style** technique found in the Desert. This combination increases the damage of the lance's Venom procs, making it a deadly tool for damage-over-time (DoT) builds.

| Weapon | Type | Key Feature | Synergy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Elf Blade** | Dagger | 3-hit Heavy Combo | High-stamina builds |
| **Black Stiletto** | Dagger | Aerial Lunge/Dash | Mobility-focused sets |
| **Scrap Lance** | Spear | Venom/Hemorrhage | Serpent Style technique |

## Ranged Artillery and Rift Weapons

For those who prefer to keep their distance, the update introduces heavy-hitting ranged options that can be found through exploration and Rift farming.

1.  **Magma Shotgun:** Purchased from Alglorn in the Volcano. It requires a Blunderbuss and Magma Scales. It fires 8-9 pellets that ignite targets, providing massive burst damage at close-to-medium range.
2.  **Tektite Ballista:** A rare drop from Rift enemies like the Dune Gunner. It functions like a traditional ballista with a high-arching shot that deals massive single-target damage.
3.  **Tektite Crusher:** A Rift-exclusive heavy weapon that allows players to charge up bursts of energy, similar to the Tektite Arc. It is highly effective for breaking through enemy defenses.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Magma Shotgun has significant recoil and a wide spread. To maximize damage, you must be positioned close enough for all pellets to hit, but far enough to avoid getting caught in enemy melee counters.

## The Catatomb: A Masterclass in Versatility

The most complex of the new **pilgrammed weapons** is undoubtedly the **Catatomb**. Found by searching the tombs within the **Sweet Berry Catacombs**, this weapon features four distinct modes that you cycle through using the heavy attack button.

*   **Mode 1 (Stun):** Smashes enemies down, dealing decent damage and applying a brief stun.
*   **Mode 2 (Cursed Fire):** Ignites the user with Cursed Fire. While dangerous, this can be used to trigger specific "on-fire" buffs from late-game accessories.
*   **Mode 3 (Summoning):** Perhaps the most "broken" feature, this mode allows you to summon shadow versions of Catacomb mobs (Luto, Twig Face, etc.). There is no hard limit on the number of summons other than your available mana.
*   **Mode 4 (Light Status):** Applies a "Light" status effect to enemies, effectively changing their elemental affinity and making them vulnerable to specific counter-elements.

## FAQ

**Q: Where is the best place to farm for the Catatomb?**

A: You must enter the Sweet Berry Catacombs and head to the Jailer area. Take a right into the tomb room and interact with the various graves. It is a RNG-based drop, so it may take anywhere from 5 to 30 minutes of searching to find.

**Q: How do I get the Lance Fragments for the Scrap Lance?**

A: Lance Fragments are a 50% drop from the **Viper Head** boss. You will need at least two fragments, along with Scrap Metal and 900 Gold, to purchase the lance from Billy at the Observatory.

**Q: Are the new pilgrammed weapons better than the old meta?**

A: Many of the new weapons, like the Black Stiletto and Bone Claws, are side-grades or direct upgrades to older gear. However, they often require more specific builds (like Serpent Style or Sunbreaker Armor) to reach their full potential.

**Q: Can the Big Fish Gauntlet be used in a Melee build?**

A: While it is a fist weapon, it scales primarily with Mage stats. Using it in a pure Strength build will result in significantly lower damage compared to the Magma Shackles or other physical gauntlets.

## Locating Kai: The Path to the Master

Before you can begin your training, you must find Kai. He is tucked away in a remote area that requires some platforming and navigation. Most players find it easiest to start their journey from the Mountain, a central landmark that serves as a gateway to many of the game's higher-level challenges.

To reach the master of the **pilgrammed kai fist**, follow these navigational steps:

| Step | Location/Action | Key Landmarks |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Start at the Mountain | Look for the main climbing path. |
| **2** | Head toward the outskirts | Follow the trail leading away from the central hub. |
| **3** | Platforming Section | Navigate across the rocky outcroppings. |

Once you arrive at Kai’s location, you must interact with him to initiate the trial. He won't give up his secrets easily; you must accept his request to fight. Upon acceptance, you will be teleported to a dedicated arena where the real challenge begins.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Kai is a "gimmick" boss. Attempting to hit him with standard M1 or M2 attacks while he is standing will result in zero damage. You must play by his rules to win.

## Understanding the Kai Boss Fight Mechanics

The encounter to unlock the **pilgrammed kai fist** is unique because Kai is functionally invulnerable to standard attacks. You cannot simply out-DPS him with high-level gear. Instead, the fight is a test of your parrying capabilities.

### The Down State Mechanic
To damage Kai, you must successfully parry his incoming attacks. Every successful parry reduces his "posture" (an invisible meter). Once you have parried enough attacks, Kai will enter a "Down State." During this brief window, he will fall to one knee and become vulnerable to all forms of damage.

| Phase | Requirement | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Neutral** | 0% Vulnerability | Focus entirely on defense and parrying. |
| **Down State** | Successful Parries | Unleash your highest DPS combos immediately. |
| **Recovery** | Kai stands back up | Back away to avoid any wake-up attacks. |

Generally, you will need to force Kai into this Down State approximately four times to fully deplete his health bar. Note that at 50% health, Kai will enter a "Rage Mode," increasing the speed and frequency of his attacks, making the timing for your parries much tighter.

## Kai's Moveset: How to Counter Every Attack

To master the **pilgrammed kai fist** trial, you must memorize Kai's four distinct moves. He does not follow a set rotation, meaning he can use any move at any time, requiring quick reflexes.

### 1. The Triple Punch Combo
This is Kai's most common attack and your primary opportunity to down him. He will throw a series of three rapid punches. 
*   **How to handle:** Each of these three punches can be parried. If you successfully parry all three, you will significantly accelerate his progress toward the Down State.
*   **Note:** Kai can occasionally "faint" this attack, pulling back at the last second to mess up your timing. Stay focused on his arm movements rather than his sound cues.

### 2. Blue Fireball (Projectile)
Kai will charge back his fists and launch a blue projectile at you from a distance.
*   **How to handle:** This move is easily parried. If you time it correctly, you take no damage and build posture damage against him.
*   **Alternative:** If you are low on health and don't want to risk a mistimed parry, you can dash to the side to dodge it entirely.

### 3. The Lethal Uppercut
This is the most dangerous move in Kai's arsenal. He makes a distinct sound and prepares his fist for a massive upward strike.
*   **How to handle:** **Do NOT attempt to parry this.** This attack is unparryable. If it connects, the knockback and subsequent fall damage will likely kill you instantly, even if you have the Tenacity stat leveled up.
*   **Strategy:** When you see the wind-up, dash backward immediately to stay out of range.

### 4. Meteor Stomp
Kai stomps the ground, summoning meteors that rain down across the entire arena.
*   **How to handle:** The stomp itself does no damage, but the meteors are lethal. They deal high damage and can stun you, leaving you open to Kai's other attacks.
*   **Strategy:** Unlock your camera and look upward. Run in wide circles and use your dash to avoid the shadows on the ground where the meteors are about to land. Do not try to attack Kai during this phase.

## Advanced Strategies for Success

If you are struggling to secure the **pilgrammed kai fist**, consider adjusting your character build specifically for this encounter. Since the fight relies on parrying, gear that increases your "Parry Window" can be incredibly helpful.

1.  **Focus on Tenacity:** While the uppercut is often a one-shot, having high Tenacity can help you survive accidental meteor hits or mistimed fireball parries.
2.  **Patience is Key:** Many players lose the fight because they get greedy during the Down State. Ensure you have enough stamina to dash away once Kai begins to stand back up.
3.  **Audio Cues:** Kai has specific grunts and sound effects for his uppercut and fireball. Playing with sound on is almost mandatory for reacting to his unparryable moves.

By following these steps and practicing your parry timings, the **pilgrammed kai fist** will be yours. It remains one of the most satisfying weapons to use in the game, rewarding players who have mastered the fundamental mechanics of Pilgrammed's combat system.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I get the Pilgrammed Kai Fist without fighting Kai?**

A: No. The only way to obtain the Kai Fists is to defeat Kai in his arena and then speak to him afterward to receive the style. It cannot be traded or purchased from vendors.

**Q: What happens if I die during the Kai boss fight?**

A: If you die, you will respawn at your last set spawn point. You will need to travel back to Kai's location and talk to him again to restart the challenge. There is no penalty other than the time spent traveling back.

**Q: Is the Pilgrammed Kai Fist good for PvP?**

A: Yes, it is quite viable in PvP due to its fast attack speed and the pressure applied by the burn damage. However, it requires the player to be very close to their opponent, which can be risky against long-range magic or heavy weapon users.

**Q: Do I need a specific level to fight Kai?**

A: While there is no hard level requirement, it is recommended to have a decent amount of health and stamina. Most players find the fight manageable around the mid-game (Level 25-40), provided they are comfortable with the parry mechanic.

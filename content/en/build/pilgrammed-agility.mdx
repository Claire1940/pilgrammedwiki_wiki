## The Core Skill Tree for Agility

To build a character capable of extreme speeds, you must distribute your skill points effectively. Unlike glass cannon builds, a high-mobility build needs a balance of speed and defensive utility to ensure that when you do get hit, it isn't an immediate trip back to the spawn point.

The foundation of this build is the **Athlete** skill. However, there is a specific strategy regarding how many points to invest here. While it might be tempting to max out every speed-related node, efficiency is key to maintaining a viable health pool.

| Skill Name | Version | Effect in Build |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Athlete** | Base | Reach max running speed 50% faster. Essential for quick repositioning. |
| **Rally** | Aced | Heals a portion of health on every successful Parry. |
| **Resilient** | Base | Provides a flat reduction to incoming damage. |
| **Tank** | Aced | Significantly increases your total HP pool. |
| **Juggernaut** | Aced | Reduces damage taken and provides a scaling damage boost. |

> **💡 Pro Tip:** We avoid the Aced version of Athlete in this specific build because the base version provides enough acceleration. The saved points are better spent in the Defense tree (Tank and Juggernaut) to compensate for the lower defense stats found on agility-based accessories.

## Essential Gear: Armor and Weapons

While accessories do the heavy lifting for your movement tech, your armor and weapon choices provide the stat baseline necessary to make **pilgrammed agility** effective in combat.

### The Kaigi Armor
The **Kaigi** armor is a cornerstone of the Speed Demon build. Even if you aren't using a fist-based combat style, the raw stats provided by this set are too good to pass up. It is one of the few armor sets in the game that grants an additional jump, which is vital for maintaining airtime and dodging ground-based AOE attacks. It also provides a healthy mix of Agility, Dexterity, and Strength.

### Recommended Weapon: Prism Launch
While this build is flexible enough to work with various weapons, the **Prism Launch** is highly recommended for 2026. Because you will be moving at high speeds, you need a weapon that allows for consistent chip damage without requiring you to stand still for long animations. 

When refining your weapon, look for the following traits and gems:
*   **Traits:** Light (for faster swings) or Relentless.
*   **Gems:** The **Insignia Gem** is the preferred choice to maximize your efficiency while on the move.

3.  **Highway Sun Boss:** This is one of the few bosses that can match your speed. Use your dashes to move diagonally rather than straight back to avoid his lunges.

## Advanced Movement Tech: The Speed Combo

Simply equipping these items isn't enough; you must master the "Speed Combo" to maximize your **pilgrammed agility**. To cross the map efficiently and avoid fall damage, practice the following sequence:

1.  **Jump once** to gain initial height.
2.  **Double jump** immediately to activate the Celestial Ring's boost.
3.  **Press Q** to initiate the Scurry Dash (hovering forward).
4.  **Jump again** (using your Nimbus Pendant charges) to maintain momentum.
5.  **Activate the Ivory Anklet** for a final forward spin.
6.  **Press Q** right before hitting the ground to land safely and negate fall damage.

This rotation allows you to maintain a high velocity that most enemies cannot track. In PVE combat, this makes you nearly untouchable, as you can dash behind a boss, land a few hits, and be across the arena before they can turn around.

## Combat Strategy: Speed vs. DPS

The main trade-off when focusing on **pilgrammed agility** is that your individual hits may do less damage than a dedicated "Glass Cannon" build. However, your total damage over the course of a fight is often higher because you spend less time dead or retreating to heal.

*   **The Hit-and-Run:** Use your Ivory Anklet to spin through enemies, dealing damage as you pass.
*   **The Nightcap Reset:** When your health drops below 30%, use your multiple jumps and Scurry Dash to fly to the furthest corner of the arena. Immediately use the Nightcap. By the time the boss traverses the arena to reach you, your health will be restored, and your dash cooldowns will have reset.
*   **Parry Synergy:** Since you are using the Aced Rally skill, look for easy-to-read telegraphs. A single parry can keep your momentum going without needing to stop for a full heal.

## FAQ

**Q: Is pilgrammed agility better than a full Strength build?**

A: It depends on your playstyle. A Strength build will kill bosses faster, but an agility build is much safer for solo players and those who struggle with dodging high-speed attacks. In 2026, many players prefer the agility build for its versatility in both exploration and combat.

**Q: What if I can't find 5 Diamonds for the Nimbus Pendant?**

A: You can use the **Cloud Pendant** as a temporary substitute. It provides fewer extra jumps but still benefits from the same mobility scaling. Diamonds can be found in various chests throughout the world or as rare drops from high-level ores.

**Q: Does the Nightcap freeze you forever?**

A: No, the freeze effect lasts only a few seconds while your health regenerates. The key is to ensure you have enough distance from your enemies before using it, which is why high agility is a prerequisite for using the Nightcap effectively.

**Q: Can I use this build for PvP?**

A: Yes! This build is incredibly frustrating for opponents in PvP because it is very difficult to hit. Your ability to hover and dash makes you a hard target for projectile weapons and slow melee swings.
